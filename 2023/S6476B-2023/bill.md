# Directs state agencies to submit reports detailing their telework policies

**Bill ID:** S6476BB
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Kristen Gonzalez
**Status:** In Assembly Committee

## Summary

Directs state agencies to submit reports detailing their telework policies; permits input from employees anonymously; directs the department of civil service to compile reports and submit a single report to the legislature.

  Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may  be  cited  as

  ยง  2.  Legislative intent. The legislature takes notice of the growing
 practice of the use of telework as a future form of work away  from  the

 benefiting the state and its employees.

  (b) "Telework" means to perform normal and regular work functions on a
 workday  that  ordinarily would be performed at the state agency's offi-
 cial principal location or locations, at a different location.
  ยง 4. State agency telework report.  (a) Each state agency shall submit
 a  report to the department of civil service by July 1, 2025 which shall
 contain the following:
  (i) Number of employees that teleworked in 2024;
  (ii) Job titles that teleworked in 2024;
  (iii) Number of days teleworked by anonymized employee in 2024;
  (iv) Number of days teleworked by job title in 2024;
  (v) Any benefits of telework remarked by the agency;
  (vi) Any disadvantages of telework remarked by the agency; and
  (vii) A copy of the agency's official telework policy, if any, includ-

  In addition, each state agency shall allow  employees  to  anonymously

 service pursuant to subdivision (b) of this section.
  (b)  On  or  before  September 1, 2025 the department of civil service
