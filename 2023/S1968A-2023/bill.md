# Authorizes emergency medical care personnel to provide basic first aid to dogs and cats

**Bill ID:** S1968AA
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Luis R. Sepúlveda
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S1968A PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2023/S1968A)

## Summary

Authorizes emergency medical care personnel to provide basic first aid to dogs and cats.

  THE  PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND ASSEM-

  Section 1. Section 3013 of the public health law is amended by  adding

 ADVANCED LIFE SUPPORT FIRST RESPONSE SERVICE OF WHICH SUCH PERSON  IS  A
 MEMBER  OR  EMPLOYEE,  WHO, IN THE COURSE OF RESPONDING TO AN EMERGENCY,

 DER,  EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN OR ADVANCED EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNI-
 CIAN PROVIDING BASIC FIRST AID TO A DOG OR CAT SHALL NOT BE  LIABLE  FOR
 DAMAGES  FOR INJURIES OR DEATH ALLEGED TO HAVE BEEN SUSTAINED BY ANY DOG

 OR CAT AS A RESULT OF SUCH INSTRUCTION  WHERE  SUCH  VETERINARIAN  ACTED

  9.  THE  COMMISSIONER  OF  EDUCATION  SHALL PROMULGATE RULES AND REGU-

  §  2. The public health law is amended by adding a new section 3019 to
 read as follows:
  § 3019. BASIC FIRST AID TO DOGS AND CATS. 1. IN THE COURSE OF RESPOND-
 ING TO ANY EMERGENCY, A CERTIFIED  FIRST  RESPONDER,  EMERGENCY  MEDICAL
 TECHNICIAN  OR  ADVANCED EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN MAY, IN ACCORDANCE
 WITH THE RULES AND REGULATIONS OF THE COMMISSIONER OF EDUCATION, PROVIDE
 BASIC FIRST AID TO A DOG OR CAT PRIOR TO THE TRANSFER OF SUCH ANIMAL  TO
 THE CARE OF A LICENSED VETERINARIAN, PROVIDED HOWEVER, THAT THERE ARE NO
 PERSONS  REQUIRING MEDICAL ATTENTION OR TRANSPORTATION AT SUCH TIME. THE
 BASIC FIRST AID PROVIDED TO CATS AND DOGS PURSUANT TO THIS SECTION SHALL
 BE LIMITED TO THE FOLLOWING, IF THE CERTIFIED FIRST RESPONDER, EMERGENCY
 MEDICAL TECHNICIAN OR ADVANCED EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN IS ABLE  AND
 AUTHORIZED TO PROVIDE SUCH CARE TO A HUMAN:
  (A) OPENING AND MANUALLY MAINTAINING AN AIRWAY;
  (B) PROVIDING MOUTH TO MOUTH OR MOUTH TO BARRIER VENTILATION;
  (C) ADMINISTERING OXYGEN;
  (D) MANAGING VENTILATION BY MASK;
  (E) CONTROLLING HEMORRHAGE WITH DIRECT PRESSURE;
  (F) IMMOBILIZING FRACTURES;
  (G) BANDAGING; AND
  (H)  ADMINISTERING  NALOXONE HYDROCHLORIDE TO THE EXTENT AUTHORIZED BY
 LAW, RULE OR REGULATION AND AS DIRECTED BY A LICENSED VETERINARIAN.
  2. TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, EACH CERTIFIED FIRST RESPONDER, EMERGEN-
 CY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN OR ADVANCED EMERGENCY MEDICAL  TECHNICIAN,  DURING
 THE COURSE OF PROVIDING BASIC FIRST AID TO A DOG OR CAT PURSUANT TO THIS
 SECTION,  SHALL COMMUNICATE WITH A LICENSED VETERINARIAN FOR THE PURPOSE
 OF CONSULTING UPON THE PROVISION OF SUCH CARE.
  3. THE COMMISSIONER OF EDUCATION SHALL PROMULGATE SUCH RULES AND REGU-
 LATIONS AS SHALL BE  NECESSARY  TO  IMPLEMENT  THE  PROVISIONS  OF  THIS
 SECTION.
  §  3.  Section  6702  of  the education law is amended by adding a new
 subdivision 3 to read as follows:
  3. LICENSED VETERINARIANS MAY PROVIDE  INSTRUCTION  TO  ANY  CERTIFIED
 FIRST  RESPONDER,  EMERGENCY  MEDICAL  TECHNICIAN  OR ADVANCED EMERGENCY
 MEDICAL TECHNICIAN PROVIDING BASIC FIRST AID TO A DOG OR CAT PURSUANT TO
 SECTION THREE THOUSAND NINETEEN OF THE PUBLIC HEALTH LAW.
  § 4. The opening paragraph of section 6703 of  the  education  law  is
 designated  subdivision  1  and  a new subdivision 2 is added to read as
 follows:
  2. THE COMMISSIONER SHALL PROMULGATE RULES  AND  REGULATIONS  FOR  THE
 PROVISION  OF BASIC FIRST AID TO DOGS AND CATS BY EMERGENCY MEDICAL CARE
 PERSONNEL PURSUANT TO SECTION THREE  THOUSAND  NINETEEN  OF  THE  PUBLIC
 HEALTH LAW.

  16. A CERTIFIED  FIRST  RESPONDER,  EMERGENCY  MEDICAL  TECHNICIAN  OR
 ADVANCED  EMERGENCY  MEDICAL TECHNICIAN PROVIDING BASIC FIRST AID TO THE
 EXTENT AUTHORIZED BY SECTION  THREE  THOUSAND  NINETEEN  OF  THE  PUBLIC
 HEALTH LAW.
