# Honoring The Desert Of New York for our outstanding achievements in community service under the leadership of HPIP Damien E. Hall and PC Glenda A. Simmons

**Bill ID:** J820
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Kevin S. Parker
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 820
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  HONORING  The  Desert  Of  New  York  for  our
  outstanding achievements in community service  under
  the  leadership of HPIP Damien E. Hall and PC Glenda
  A. Simmons
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize  that
the  quality  and  character  of life in the communities across New York
State are reflective of the concerned and  dedicated  efforts  of  those
organizations and individuals who would devote themselves to the welfare
of the community and its citizenry; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
The Desert Of New York for our  outstanding  achievements  in  community
service  under  the  leadership  of HPIP Damien E. Hall and PC Glenda A.
Simmons, to be celebrated at The Ancient Egyptian  Arabic  Order  Nobles
Mystic  Shrine  (A.E.A.O.N.M.S.)  Desert  Conference, which will be held
Friday, April 28, to Sunday April 30, 2023, in Latham, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, The weekend conference consists  of  a  general  membership
meeting  where HPIP Damien E. Hall (251), Imperial Deputy Of The Desert,
and PC Glenda A. Simmons (171), Imperial Deputy  For  The  Desert,  will
address the membership; and
 
  WHEREAS, Guests will include members of the Imperial Council and the
national governing body, who will address the members in attendance; and
 
  WHEREAS,  During  this  eventful weekend, those in the leadership of
their local Temples and Courts will attend workshops for the  Presidents
(Illustrious Potentates and Commandresses), Secretaries, Treasurers, and
other local Temple and Court officers; and
 
  WHEREAS,  There  will additionally be workshops on mental health and
the health concerns specific to members of the Desert Temple  community;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  There  is  a  tradition by which the host Temple and Court
arrange for the members at the conference to visit a  local  charity  to
make  a  financial  contribution to that local organization, truly going
out of their way to support the valued members of local communities; and
 
  WHEREAS, Over the years,  this  organization  has  visited  schools,
homeless  shelters, hospitals, and nursing homes and financially donated
to their social programs that serve the community; charity is the  basis
of  the  organization,  and they are committed to making a difference in
the communities where there are local Temples and Courts; and
 
  WHEREAS, The names of these  illustrious  Temples  are  as  follows:
Medina  Temple No. 19, New York, New York, Hadji Temple No. 61, Buffalo,
New York, Aleppo Temple No. 88, Syracuse, New York, Abu Bekr Temple  No.
91,  Brooklyn,  New  York,  Al  Tabari Temple No. 121, Albany, New York,
Elejmal Temple No.  185,  Ossining,  New  York,  Nyla  Temple  No.  238,
 
Watertown, New York, and Al Maseer Temple No. 251, Amityville, New York;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  names  of  the esteemed Courts are as follows: Medina
Court No. 11, New York, New York, Al Tabari Court No.  28,  Albany,  New
York,  Hadji  Court  No.  62, Buffalo, New York, Abu Bekr Court No. 140,
Brooklyn, New York, Elejmal Court No. 171, Ossining, New York, Al Maseer
Court No.  226, Amityville, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is  the  custom  of  this  Legislative  Body  that  when
individuals  and institutions of such noble aims and accomplishments are
brought to our attention, they should be celebrated  and  recognized  by
all the citizens of this great Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor The Desert  Of  New  York  for  our  outstanding  achievements  in
community  service  under  the  leadership of HPIP Damien E. Hall and PC
Glenda A.  Simmons; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That copies of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to HPIP Damien E. Hall and PC Glenda A. Simmons.
