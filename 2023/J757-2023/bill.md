# Honoring Sister Patricia Vetrano upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Bishop Joseph M. Sullivan Founders Award on May 9, 2023

**Bill ID:** J757
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Kevin S. Parker
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 757
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  HONORING Sister Patricia Vetrano upon the occasion
  of her designation as recipient of the Bishop Joseph
  M. Sullivan Founders Award on May 9, 2023
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the sense of this Legislative Body to take note of
and  publicly  acknowledge  individuals  of  remarkable  integrity  and
character whose endeavors have enhanced the quality of education in this
great State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Sister  Patricia  Vetrano  upon  the  occasion  of  her  designation  as
recipient  of  the  Bishop  Joseph  M.  Sullivan  Founders  Award, to be
celebrated at Cristo Rey Brooklyn High School's Annual Gala on  Tuesday,
May 9, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Raised  in  Tarrytown,  New  York,  Patricia  Vetrano  was
educated by the Sisters of Mercy; she subsequently earned her bachelor's
degree  from  Mercy  College  and  master's  degree  in  education  and
counseling from Manhattan; and
 
  WHEREAS,  After 12 years of teaching in elementary schools, ministry
in parishes, and serving as Director of Formation for  her  NY  Regional
Community,  Sister  Patricia Vetrano became the principal of Our Lady of
Victory Academy in Dobbs Ferry in 1990; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2000, Sister Patricia Vetrano was  elected  to  the  New
York  Sisters of Mercy Community Leadership Team; from 2000 to 2021, the
Sister served  on  Community  Leadership,  including  15  years  on  the
Mid-Atlantic  Community  Leadership Team, eventually becoming President;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Sister Patricia Vetrano, in her various ministries,  served
on  many  boards,  such  as educational, health care systems, and social
services; and
 
  WHEREAS, In her role as President of the Sisters  of  Mercy,  Sister
Patricia  Vetrano facilitated the relocation of Cristo Rey Brooklyn High
School to its new location, providing better resources for education for
young people in the community; and
 
  WHEREAS, For  the  last  decade,  under  Sister  Patricia  Vetrano's
leadership  and  advocacy,  the  Sisters  of  Mercy  have  served as the
Spiritual Endorser of Cristo Rey Brooklyn High School; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Within  every  community,  there  are  inevitably  some
individuals  who,  by  virtue  of  their energy, vision, and dedication,
command the greatest respect among their  friends  and  colleagues,  and
within  the  community  at  large;  Sister  Patricia Vetrano is one such
individual; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Brooklyn community is indeed fortunate and  blessed  to
have  such  a dedicated and respected educational leader; her wisdom and
 
contributions reach into every classroom, truly making a  difference  in
the lives of those lucky enough to work with her; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  significant contributor to education in New York State,
Sister  Patricia  Vetrano  continually  demonstrates  an  impressive
dedication,  an  unparalleled devotion and an unremitting enthusiasm for
the quality of the educational programs  and  opportunities  offered  at
Cristo  Rey  Brooklyn  High  School  for  the benefit of its faculty and
students, as well as for the entire community; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  Sister  Patricia  Vetrano upon the occasion of her designation as
recipient of the Bishop Joseph M. Sullivan  Founders  Award  on  May  9,
2023; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Sister Patricia Vetrano.
