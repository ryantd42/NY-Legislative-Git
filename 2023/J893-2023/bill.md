# Commending Senior Chief Michael J. Grogan, upon the occasion of his designation for special recognition by the Lake Delta Kiwanis on May 17, 2023

**Bill ID:** J893
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Joseph A. Griffo
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 893
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  COMMENDING  Senior  Chief  Michael J. Grogan, upon
  the  occasion  of  his  designation  for  special
  recognition  by  the  Lake  Delta Kiwanis on May 17,
  2023
 
  WHEREAS, Members of the Armed Services from the State of  New  York,
who  have  served  so  valiantly  and  honorably  in  wars in which this
country's freedom was at stake, as well as in the preservation of  peace
in peacetime, deserve a special salute from this Legislative Body; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It is the intent of this Legislative Body to commend those
who have served in times of active conflict, as well as those  who  have
served in times of peace; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  is justly proud to commend Senior
Chief Michael J. Grogan upon the occasion of his designation for special
recognition by the Lake Delta Kiwanis on May 17, 2023, to be  celebrated
at this year's 6th Annual Veteran's Recognition Dinner at the Delta Lake
Inn of Rome, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  dedication  and  sacrifices of our military personnel
ensure our continued role as a  Nation  which  embodies  the  ideals  of
democracy,  and  is  a  defender  of  liberty for peoples throughout the
world; and
 
  WHEREAS, Michael J. Grogan was born in Albany, New York in 1962, and
graduated from Colonie Central High School in 1980; ten days  later,  he
was  attending  Boot Camp in San Antonio, Texas with the U.S. Air Force;
and
 
  WHEREAS, While on active duty, Michael J.  Grogan  spent  ten  years
stationed  at  Griffiss Air Force Base (AFB), Rome, New York; during his
tour at  Griffiss  AFB,  he  was  assigned  to  the  416  Organizational
Maintenance Squadron (OMS) to maintain B-52's; and
 
  WHEREAS,  He was later selected in 1985 to represent Griffiss AFB as
their Crew Chief to compete in the Strategic Air Command  (SAC)  Weapons
Load Competition; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In 1990, after ten years of active-duty service in the Air
Force, Michael J. Grogan instead decided to pursue  a  civilian  career;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  1994,  Michael J. Grogan affiliated with the U.S. Navy
Reserves as a Seabee; he  was  assigned  to  Naval  Mobile  Construction
Battalion  (NMCB)  13; Detachment 13 Frankfort, New York; shortly after,
he was assigned to Construction Battalion Maintenance Unit  (CBMU)  202,
Groton, Connecticut; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2000, while assigned to CBMU 202, he was promoted to the
rank of Chief Petty Officer; after his promotion, he was assigned as the
Officer  in  Charge  (MC)  of  a detachment of Seabees in Newport, Rhode
Island; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  In  2001, he was mobilized with the New York Naval Militia
in support of the World Trade Center Attack; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  2004,  the  Naval  Construction  Force  had  a  force
realignment  which  combined all CBMU 202 personnel to now be reassigned
to Naval Mobile Construction Battalion (NMCB) 27; while assigned to NMCB
27, Michael J. Grogan maintained his position as OIC at  Newport,  Rhode
Island; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  2005,  Michael J. Grogan was mobilized with NMCB 5, an
active-duty battalion in support of Operation Iraqi  Freedom;  while  in
Iraq,  he  earned  his  Seabee  Combat  Warfare Specialist Badge and was
awarded the U.S. Army Commendation Medal for his duties while supporting
the U.S. Special Operations Forces; and
 
  WHEREAS, Upon his return in 2006, Michael J. Grogan was promoted  to
his current rank of Senior Chief Petty Officer and was reassigned to the
7th  Naval  Construction  Regiment, Newport, Rhode Island as the Current
Operations Chief; and
 
  WHEREAS, Senior Chief Michael J. Grogan is a qualified Seabee Combat
Warfare Specialist, Senior Aircraft Maintenance Specialist,  and  Master
Crew  Chief;  he  also  holds  a  Federal  Aviation Administration (FAA)
Airframe and Powerplant License; and
 
  WHEREAS, The recipient of numerous accolades, Senior  Chief  Michael
J.  Grogan has been given the following esteemed awards and medals: Army
Commendation  Medal; Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal; Air Force
Commendation Medal;  Navy  and  Marine  Corps  Achievement  Medal;  USAF
Outstanding  Unit  Award;  USAF  Good  Conduct  Medal;  National Defense
Service Medal;  Meritorious  Service  Medal;  Global  War  on  Terrorism
Service  Medal;  Military  Outstanding  Volunteer  Service  Medal;  Iraq
Campaign  Medal;  Armed  Forces  Reserve  Medal  WI  "M";  Sea  Service
Deployment  Ribbon;  Navy  and Marine Corp Overseas Service Ribbon; USAF
Longevity Service Award; Air Force NCO PME  Graduate  Ribbon;  USAF  BMT
Ribbon; USAF Small Arms Expert Ribbon; Navy Expert Rifleman Marksmanship
Medal;  Navy  Expert  Pistol  Marksmanship  Medal;  New  York Defense of
Liberty Medal with the World Trade Center (WTC) device; and the New York
Conspicuous Service Cross; and
 
  WHEREAS, Always active in his community,  Senior  Chief  Michael  J.
Grogan  has  been  involved  with  the  following veterans and community
organizations: Commander Henry P. Smith Post #24; Chaplin Henry P. Smith
Post  #24;  President  Rome  Veterans  Council;  Chaplin  Rome  Veterans
Council;  President  Rome  Memorial  Day  Association;  and Chaplin Rome
Memorial Day Association; and
 
  WHEREAS, Residents of this great State must never forget the courage
with which these men and women served their country, and must  recognize
that  no  greater  debt  is  owed than that owed to those who gave their
lives for their beloved Nation and to those who continue to  be  missing
in action; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Having exhibited their patriotism both at home and abroad,
these men and women demonstrated their love for their country and  merit
forevermore, the highest respect of their State and Nation; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  Our Nation's veterans deserve to be recognized, commended,
and thanked by the people of the State of New York for their service and
for their dedication  to  their  communities,  their  State,  and  their
Nation; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend Senior  Chief  Michael  J.  Grogan  upon  the  occasion  of  his
designation for special recognition by the Lake Delta Kiwanis on May 17,
2023; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Senior Chief Michael J. Grogan.
