# Authorizes certain facilities to provide treatment for the mental health and health care needs of individuals admitted for a substance use disorder

**Bill ID:** S3525
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Nathalia Fernandez
**Status:** In Senate Committee

## Summary

Authorizes certain facilities to provide treatment for the mental health and health care needs of individuals admitted for a substance use disorder; provides that such services shall be reimbursable by Medicaid and private insurance.

  THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND  ASSEM-

  Section 1. Subdivision (f) of section 19.17 of the mental hygiene law,
 as  amended by section 1 of part K of chapter 58 of the laws of 2009, is
 amended to read as follows:
  (f) There shall be in the office the facilities named  below  for  the
 care,  treatment  and  rehabilitation  of  the mentally disabled and for
 clinical research and teaching in the science and  skills  required  for
 the  care, treatment and rehabilitation of such mentally disabled.  SUCH
 FACILITIES ARE AUTHORIZED TO PROVIDE TREATMENT FOR THE MENTAL HEALTH AND
 HEALTH CARE NEEDS OF INDIVIDUALS ADMITTED FOR A SUBSTANCE USE  DISORDER,
 PROVIDED, FURTHER, THAT THE MENTAL HEALTH AND HEALTH CARE SERVICES SHALL
 BE  REIMBURSABLE  BY  MEDICAID  AND  PRIVATE  INSURANCE.  MENTAL  HEALTH
 SERVICES SHALL BE PROVIDED BY A PROFESSIONAL  WHOSE  SCOPE  OF  PRACTICE
 INCLUDES THE DIAGNOSIS AND TREATMENT OF MENTAL HEALTH DISORDERS.  HEALTH
 CARE  SERVICES  SHALL  BE  PROVIDED  BY A HEALTH CARE PROFESSIONAL WHOSE
 SCOPE OF PRACTICE INCLUDES DIAGNOSING AND TREATING  HUMAN  RESPONSES  TO
 ACTUAL  OR  POTENTIAL HEALTH PROBLEMS. THE OFFICE SHALL CONSULT WITH THE
 OFFICE OF MENTAL HEALTH AND THE DEPARTMENT  OF  HEALTH  TO  ASSURE  THAT
 APPLICABLE  RULES  AND  REGULATIONS  OF SUCH AGENCIES ARE COMPLIED WITH.
 NOTHING IN THIS SECTION SHALL  PRECLUDE  THESE  FACILITIES  FROM  TRANS-
 FERRING  A  PERSON  TO  A  DIFFERENT  FACILITY IN THE EVENT THE PERSON'S
 MENTAL HEALTH OR HEALTH CARE NEEDS ARE DEEMED TO  EXCEED  THE  TREATMENT
 CAPACITY OF SUCH FACILITY.
  R.E. Blaisdell Addiction Treatment Center
