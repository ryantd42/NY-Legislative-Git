# Commending Saint Joseph Lodge No. 117, Most Worshipful Prince Hall Grand Lodge, Free and Accepted Masons of the State of New York upon the occasion of hosting its Annual Breakfast

**Bill ID:** J799
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Kevin S. Parker
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 799
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  COMMENDING  Saint  Joseph  Lodge  No.  117,  Most
  Worshipful  Prince  Hall  Grand  Lodge,  Free  and
  Accepted  Masons  of  the State of New York upon the
  occasion of hosting its Annual Breakfast recognizing
  their widows on March 5, 2023
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to  recognize  and
pay  tribute  to  those  organizations  of  high  purpose  and  worthy
accomplishment whose endeavors have faithfully served the welfare of the
citizens of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
commend Saint Joseph Lodge No. 117, Most Worshipful  Prince  Hall  Grand
Lodge,  Free  and  Accepted  Masons  of  the  State of New York upon the
occasion of hosting its Annual Breakfast to recognize  their  widows  on
Sunday, March 5, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS, This auspicious occasion is a time to give an expression of
love  and  gratitude  to all of the widows of the lodge, and to reflect,
acknowledge, and give thanks for their past; and
 
  WHEREAS, The dedicated Masons of Saint Joseph Lodge No. 117 are  men
with  a  social  conscience  and a charitable perspective, also known as
Freemasons; it is a fraternal  organization  that  shares  common  moral
ideals  and  esoteric  values  and is based on the doctrine of Brotherly
Love, Relief and Truth; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Masons  of  Saint  Joseph  Lodge  No.  117  have  truly
demonstrated  continuous  service to the residents and communities which
it serves; and
 
  WHEREAS, Saint Joseph Lodge No. 17 was founded  in  1953,  in  Mount
Vernon,  New  York,  in the basement of Deacon William Grant of Mt. Zion
Church; the first Worshipful Master of the lodge was Herbert Grant, then
Charles Webb, and finally Willie Gibson; and
 
  WHEREAS, Saint Joseph Lodge No. 17 was a member of the  Alpha  Grand
Lodge  of  New  York  City,  and  used to hold their meetings at various
churches and the Odd Fellow Hall in Mount Vernon; eventually the members
purchased a building at 28 E. 1st Street where  they  continue  to  hold
their meetings; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  1983, under the Leadership of Worshipful Master Willie
Gibson, the Lodge became Under Dispensation (UD), to become a member  of
the  Most  Worshipful  Prince Hall Grand Lodge of the State of New York,
and one year later, it received its Charter; the Lodge was renumbered to
117 and became a member of the Third Masonic District; and
 
  WHEREAS, The first elected Principal officers of Saint Joseph  Lodge
No.  117 were Paul Bates, Worshipful Master; Amos Jones, Senior Warden;
and Earlie Albright, Junior Warden; and
 
  WHEREAS, Staying true to the principles of freemasonry, Saint Joseph
Lodge No. 117 has participated in various community activities  such  as
cooking and serving meals at the local soup kitchen, sponsoring a Little
League  baseball  team,  and supporting its Grand Lodge Scholarship fund
program to assist the local youth with their education needs; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is proudly moved to honor and commend
the members of Saint Joseph Lodge No. 117, Prince Hall Affiliation, Free
and  Accepted  Masons,  past  and  present,  who  have  labored  so
conscientiously to secure and  improve  the  quality  of  life  for  the
community it serves; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend Saint Joseph Lodge No. 117, Most Worshipful  Prince  Hall  Grand
Lodge,  Free  and  Accepted  Masons  of  the  State of New York upon the
occasion of hosting its Annual Breakfast to recognize  their  widows  on
March 5, 2023; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Saint Joseph Lodge No. 117, Most Worshipful  Prince  Hall
Grand Lodge, Free and Accepted Masons of the State of New York.
