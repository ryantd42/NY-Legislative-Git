# Honoring The Reverend Sean P. O'Brien upon the occasion of his 30th Pastoral Anniversary

**Bill ID:** J1295
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Joseph A. Griffo
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1295
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING  The  Reverend  Sean  P. O'Brien upon the
  occasion of his 30th Pastoral Anniversary
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative  Body  to  commend  the
efforts  of  those  individuals within the State of New York who give of
their time and energies in support of spiritual endeavors which  aim  to
benefit the community at large; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
The Reverend Sean P. O'Brien upon the  occasion  of  his  30th  Pastoral
Anniversary on June 5, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS, Sean P. O'Brien was born in Binghamton, New York in 1965 to
John Francis O'Brien and Donna O'Brien, and graduated from Vestal Senior
High School in 1983; and
 
  WHEREAS,  After  studying  to  become  a radio announcer at Herkimer
County Community College, Sean P. O'Brien applied to  seminary  and  was
accepted  by  the Diocese of Syracuse; after three years at Wadhams Hall
in Ogdensburg, New York, he finished Major Seminary  at  St.  Mary's  in
Baltimore, Maryland, and was ordained to the priesthood on June 5, 1993;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Currently,  Father  Sean  P.  O'Brien is the pastor of St.
Peter's Church, the Faith Community of St. Mary's/St. Peter's, in  Rome,
New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  Reverend  Sean  P.  O'Brien's  previous  diocesan
assignments include: Our Lady of Lourdes, Utica, NY from 1993-1998;  St.
Patrick's  Church, Forestport, NY and St. Mary of the Snows, Otter Lake,
NY from 1998-2002; Christ Our  Light  in  Pulaski,  NY  from  2003-2009;
Christ  Our  Hope  Catholic  Community  of  Northern Oneida County, from
2009-2014; St.  Matthew's, East Syracuse/St. Mary's,  Minoa/St.  Francis
of Assisi, Bridgeport, NY from 2015-2019; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  dedication and devotion to making a difference, Sean
P.  O'Brien joined the Navy in May  of  1991;  his  Reserve  assignments
include:  Naval  Hospital  Oakland,  Strike  Fleet, Submarine Squadron
Support Unit in New London, CT; 5th Fleet in the Arabian Gulf; NMCB  27,
25th  Marines,  7th  Naval  Construction  Regiment, 4th Marine Air Wing,
Command Naval Installation Command, Washington DC and  Military  Sealift
Command;  Father  Sean  P.  O'Brien  retired from the Navy in October of
2020, with the rank of Captain; and
 
  WHEREAS, Sean P. O'Brien's mobilizations/deployments include: Ground
Zero (NYC) 2001; Operation Iraqi Freedom 2002-2003;  Operation  Enduring
Freedom (CJTF-82 Afghanistan) 2007; CJTF-HOA (Horn of Africa) 2014-2015;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  recipient  of  numerous awards and accolades, Sean P.
O'Brien has received the Legion of Merit,  Defense  Meritorious  Service
Medal  (2  awards),  Meritorious Service Medal, Joint Commendation Medal
 
and Navy Achievement Medal (3  awards)  throughout  the  course  of  his
service; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  January  of  2022,  The  Reverend  Sean P. O'Brien was
appointed Vicar Forane (V.F.), previously referred as "Regional  Vicar,"
for Region IV of the Syracuse Diocese by Bishop Douglas J. Lucia; Region
IV  covers  Boonville,  Camden, Canastota, Forestport, Hamilton, Holland
Patent, Lee Center, Marcy,  Morrisville,  North  Bay,  Oneida,  Oriskany
Falls, Otter Lake, Rome, Sherrill, Sylvan Beach, Taberg, Vernon, Verona,
and Waterville, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that when a person
of such noble aims and accomplishments is brought to our attention it is
appropriate  to  publicly  and  jubilantly  proclaim  and  commend  that
individual for the edification of others; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  The  Reverend  Sean  P.  O'Brien  upon  the  occasion of his 30th
Pastoral Anniversary on June 5, 2023; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the The Reverend Sean P. O'Brien.
