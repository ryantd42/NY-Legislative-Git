# Mourning the death of Marianne H. Lindquist, devoted mother and grandmother, distinguished citizen and dedicated member of her community

**Bill ID:** J1299
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Pete Harckham
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1299
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
  MOURNING  the  death  of  Marianne  H.  Lindquist,
  devoted  mother  and  grandmother,  distinguished
  citizen and dedicated member of her community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
citizens  of  the  State  of  New York whose lifework and civic endeavor
served to enhance the quality of life  in  their  communities  and  this
great Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianne  H. Lindquist of Carmel, New York, passed away on
Sunday, April 9, 2023, at the age of 85; and
 
  WHEREAS, Marianne H. Lindquist  was  born  to  Eugene  and  Margaret
(Thiele) Schmidt on April 25, 1937; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianne  H.  Lindquist  distinguished  herself  in  her
profession and by her sincere dedication and substantial contribution to
the welfare of her community; and
 
  WHEREAS, For 38 years, Marianne H. Lindquist worked as the executive
secretary for IBM Thomas J. Watson Research Center in Yorktown  Heights,
New  York; in this capacity, she directly managed six other secretaries;
and
 
  WHEREAS, In addition to this role, Marianne H. Lindquist worked  for
the Putnam Board of Elections, and was a devoted mother and grandmother;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Predeceased  by  her  sister  Barbara Schmidt, Marianne H.
Lindquist is survived by her beloved  son,  Eric  (Julie)  and  her  two
treasured  grandchildren, Grayson and Harper; and her longtime companion
of 22 years, Al Witte, all of whom will long cherish her memory; and
 
  WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a  sense  of
compassion, Marianne H. Lindquist leaves behind a legacy which will long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
she served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn  the  death  of  Marianne  H.  Lindquist,  devoted  mother  and
grandmother,  distinguished  citizen  and  dedicated  member  of  her
community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Marianne H. Lindquist.
