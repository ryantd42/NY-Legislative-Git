# Mourning the death of George Graniero, meritorious veteran and firefighter, distinguished citizen, and devoted member of his community

**Bill ID:** J920
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Pete Harckham
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 920
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
  MOURNING the death of George Graniero, meritorious
  veteran  and firefighter, distinguished citizen, and
  devoted member of his community
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is moved to publicly mourn the  death
of those prominent citizens whose lifework and purposeful endeavors have
served  to  enrich  and  enhance  the  quality and character of life for
others in their community and the great State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is with feelings of deepest regret that this Legislative
Body recognizes the passing of George  Graniero,  a  preeminent  citizen
whose distinguished public service and caring commitment will long serve
as a standard to which others might aspire; and
 
  WHEREAS,  George  Graniero  of  Bedford  Hills,  New  York,  died on
February 19, 2023, at the age of 94; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born a first generation  American  on  March  14,  1928  to
Michael and Assunta Graniero, George Graniero was a lifelong resident of
Bedford Hills, New York until moving to Syracuse last year; and
 
  WHEREAS,  After  graduating  from  Bedford Hills High School, George
Graniero served his country in the Army Medical Corps as a medic  during
World War II; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Further serving the community, George Graniero volunteered
with the  Bedford  Hills  Fire  Department  as  a  teenager;  he  was  a
firefighter for a remarkable 76 years; and
 
  WHEREAS, In addition to his career in service, George Graniero spent
many  years  working  as a barber at his shop, in Mount Kisco, New York;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  George  Graniero,  and  his  wife  Della  Crandall,  who
tragically  passed in 2014, led full lives; they often attended concerts
at Caramoor and Tanglewood, planned ski trips in the East and out  West,
hiked  many  of  the  National Parks, travelled to Italy, and spent many
peaceful moments at their vacation home on Cape Cod; and
 
  WHEREAS, George Graniero is survived by  his  three  sons:  Michael,
George Jr., and Douglas; three grandsons: Marc, Jeffrey, and Samuel; two
great-grandchildren: Audrey and Marc Jr.; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Forever  lasting  are the memories of George Graniero, his
family remembers attending many baseball and football games; he  was  an
avid  New  York  Yankees  and Giants fan and passed the love of the team
down through the generations; and
 
  WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a  sense  of
compassion,  George  Graniero  leaves  behind  a  legacy which will long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
mourn the death of George Graniero, meritorious veteran and firefighter,
distinguished  citizen,  and  devoted member of his community; and be it
further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the family of George Graniero.
