# Congratulating Michael M. Grant upon the occasion of his retirement after 37 years of dedicated service to the residents of the County of Rockland, the Town of Haverstraw, and the Village of West Haverstraw

**Bill ID:** K686
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Kenneth Zebrowski
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 686
 
BY: M. of A. Zebrowski
 
  CONGRATULATING  Michael M. Grant upon the occasion
  of  his  retirement  after  37  years  of  dedicated
  service  to the residents of the County of Rockland,
  the Town of Haverstraw,  and  the  Village  of  West
  Haverstraw
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body, in keeping with
its time-honored traditions, to recognize and pay tribute to  those  who
have  served to better the quality of life in our community and have had
a measurable positive impact on the lives of its residents;  Michael  M.
Grant is one such individual; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud to congratulate
Michael M. Grant upon the occasion of his retirement after 37  years  of
dedicated  service  to the residents of the County of Rockland, the Town
of Haverstraw, and the Village of West Haverstraw; and
 
  WHEREAS, A community activist who was born and  raised  in  Rockland
County,  Michael  M.  Grant  is  a  lifelong  resident  of  the  Town of
Haverstraw, and the son of the late Mary  Zippilli  Grant  and  John  T.
Grant, Rockland County's first County Executive; and
 
  WHEREAS, Michael M. Grant has tirelessly worked for the residents of
the  County  of Rockland, the Town of Haverstraw and the Village of West
Haverstraw  for  a  combined  37  years,  beginning  in  1986  as  West
Haverstraw's Village Treasurer; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  2001,  Michael  M.  Grant  took  office  as  a Town of
Haverstraw Councilman and was subsequently elected in November  2001  to
fill  a  two-year  unexpired term and again for a full four-year term in
2003; and
 
  WHEREAS, Legislator Michael  M.  Grant  was  sworn  into  office  on
January  6,  2005,  and has since been re-elected to the Rockland County
Legislature four times by the voters of District 2; and
 
  WHEREAS, This extraordinary man has served as Majority Leader  seven
times;  he  has  also  served  as  Chair  of  the  Government Operations
Committee, Vice Chair of the Budget and Finance Committee, Vice Chair of
the Planning and Public Works  Committee,  Vice  Chair  of  the  Special
Committee  on  Transit,  and  as  a  member  of the Economic Development
Committee, and as Chairman of Electrical Examiners; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2011 and 2022, Legislator Michael  M.  Grant  co-chaired
the Rockland County Legislature's Special Committee on Redistricting; in
2011,  he was the Legislature's Liaison to the County Comprehensive Plan
Task Force; and
 
  WHEREAS, For more than three decades, Michael M. Grant has played an
active role in the community as a  former  Haverstraw  King's  Daughters
Public Library Trustee and Finance Officer; he has also served as a past
board  member  on  several  community-based  organizations,  including
 
Rockland County Girl Scout Council, Arts  Alliance  of  Haverstraw,  and
North Rockland Youth Recreation Program, Inc.; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Michel M. Grant retired from Orange and Rockland Utilities
after 38 years, and in 2013, he  received  the  prestigious  Living  Our
Values Award from Con Edison; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rare  indeed  is  the  occurrence  of such a compassionate
blending of strength, leadership,  intellect,  and  commitment  as  that
demonstrated  by  Michael  M.  Grant  over  a  lifetime of sacrifice and
dedication to others; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
congratulate  Michael M. Grant upon the occasion of his retirement after
37 years of distinguished service to the County of Rockland, the Town of
Haverstraw and The Village of West Haverstraw, and to wish him  well  in
all his future endeavors; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Michael M. Grant.
