# Honoring Jennifer Drofenik upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Thomas Clarke Memorial Award by LeadingAgeNY

**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Fred Thiele
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [K19 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2023/K19)

## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 19
 
BY: M. of A. Thiele
 
  HONORING  Jennifer  Drofenik  upon the occasion of
  her designation as recipient of  the  Thomas  Clarke
  Memorial Award by LeadingAgeNY on December 7, 2022
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize that
the  quality  and  character  of life in the communities across New York
State are reflective of the concerned and  dedicated  efforts  of  those
individuals  who would devote themselves to the welfare of the community
and its citizenry; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Jennifer  Drofenik  upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of
the Thomas Clarke Memorial Award by  LeadingAgeNY,  a  statewide  agency
that represents nursing home and retirement communities; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  auspicious  occasion  was  celebrated at an event on
Wednesday, December 7, 2022, at  the  Peconic  Landing  Care  Retirement
Community in Greenport, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  As  Director  of  Health  Services for the Peconic Landing
Continuing Care Retirement Community,  Jennifer  Drofenik  is  receiving
this  prestigious  award  for her outstanding leadership and superlative
work in long-term care, housing and service administration under the age
of 40; and
 
  WHEREAS, A graduate of Southold High School, Jennifer Drofenik began
her distinguished career with Peconic Landing as a  college  intern;  in
2016, she was named Administrator of Assisted Living; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  this  role,  Jennifer  Drofenik  spearheaded  the
construction and opening of two  new  neighborhoods,  Harbor  South  for
memory support, and The Bluffs for short-term rehabilitation; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Under the luminous leadership of Jennifer Drofenik, Harbor
South became the first memory support neighborhood in  New  York  to  be
recognized  as a Distinguished Provider by Dementia Care Specialists and
the Crisis Prevention Institute; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2022, Jennifer Drofenik was promoted to the position  of
Director  of Health Services; in this capacity, she oversees the skilled
nursing, short-term rehabilitation, assisted living, and memory  support
neighborhoods; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Always  eager  to further her education in order to assist
the next generation of leaders in the industry, Jennifer Drofenik  is  a
graduate  of  the  LeadingAge  IGNITE  Leadership  Academy, and became a
Certified Dementia Practitioner; and
 
  WHEREAS, In addition to her  work  with  Peconic  Landing,  Jennifer
Drofenik  is  actively  involved  with  several  local  elder-care
organizations and is a board member of the  Alzheimer's  Association  of
 
Long Island, participating in their Walk to End Alzheimer's for the past
several years; and
 
  WHEREAS, Jennifer Drofenik also serves on the LeadingAge cabinet for
assisted  living,  advocating  for  changes  to regulations, funding and
policy in order to receive the best quality  of  service  for  those  in
assisted living; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense of this Legislative Body that those who
enhance the quality of life in their community and have shown a long and
sustained commitment to the  maintenance  of  high  standards  in  their
profession,  certainly  have  earned the recognition and applause of all
the citizens of this great Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  Jennifer  Drofenik  upon  the  occasion  of  her  designation  as
recipient of  the  Thomas  Clarke  Memorial  Award  by  LeadingAgeNY  on
December 7, 2022; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Jennifer Drofenik.
