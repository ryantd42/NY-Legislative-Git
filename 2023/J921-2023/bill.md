# Mourning the death of Irwin W. Schriro, distinguished citizen and devoted member of his community

**Bill ID:** J921
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Pete Harckham
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 921
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
  MOURNING  the  death  of  Irwin  W.  Schriro,
  distinguished citizen  and  devoted  member  of  his
  community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
citizens  of  the  State  of New York whose lifework and civic endeavors
served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and the great
State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Irwin W. Schriro of Somers, New York, died on Sunday, March
5, 2023, at the age of 63; and
 
  WHEREAS, Irwin W. Schriro distinguished himself  in  his  profession
and  by  his  sincere  dedication  and  substantial  contribution to the
welfare of his community; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born in Mt. Kisco, New York, on April 27, 1959, the son  of
Lew  and  Rose  Schriro,  Irwin  W. Schriro graduated from Fox Lane High
School and began his career in the alarm and security business;  he  was
the lead inventory and service coordinator at Scarsdale Security Systems
in Scarsdale, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  His  volunteer fire service journey began with the Bedford
Hills Volunteer Fire Department until he and his wife moved  to  Somers;
there  he joined the Somers Volunteer Fire Department where he is a life
member, and once held the esteemed positions of Chief and  Commissioner;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Irwin  W. Schriro was also a member of the Putnam Northern
Westchester Fire Police Association and Red Knights Motorcycle  Club  NY
Chapter VII, with whom he enjoyed many memorial rides and trips to visit
the  retired members residing at the Fireman's Home in Hudson, New York;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his life he enjoyed fishing,  hunting,  golfing,
bowling  in  leagues  with  his wife and friends, attending his wife and
children's many sports events,  and  the  company  of  his  many  animal
companions; and
 
  WHEREAS, Irwin W. Schriro's commitment to excellence, and his spirit
of  humanity,  carried  over  into  all  fields of enterprise, including
charitable and civic endeavors; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Irwin  W.  Schriro  is  survived  by  his  wife,  Suzanne;
daughter,  Stacey;  son,  Craig; sister, Debra, and three grandchildren,
Anabelle, Mason, and Brendon; he is predeceased by his  sister,  Bonnie;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Armed  with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of
compassion, Irwin W. Schriro leaves behind  a  legacy  which  will  long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn the death of Irwin W. Schriro, distinguished citizen  and  devoted
member of his community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the family of Irwin W. Schriro.
