# Establishes the office of the caregiving economy

**Bill ID:** S1062
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Rachel May
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S1062 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2023/S1062)

## Summary

Establishes the "office of the caregiving economy", for the purpose of promoting and supporting the state's caregiving needs by developing the caregiving economy in the state.

  § 147-B. DIRECTOR AND EMPLOYEES OF THE OFFICE. THE HEAD OF THE  OFFICE
 SHALL  BE THE DIRECTOR OF THE CAREGIVING ECONOMY, WHO SHALL BE APPOINTED
 BY THE COMMISSIONER, AND WHO SHALL HAVE EXPERTISE AND EXPERIENCE IN  THE
 CAREGIVING  ECONOMY.  THE  COMMISSIONER  MAY  APPOINT  SUCH OFFICERS AND
 EMPLOYEES  HAVING EXPERIENCE IN THE CAREGIVING INDUSTRY, INCLUDING INDI-
 VIDUALS WITH EXPERIENCE WORKING WITH ORGANIZED LABOR  ORGANIZATIONS  AND
 INFORMAL CAREGIVERS, AS THE COMMISSIONER MAY DEEM NECESSARY TO CARRY OUT
 THE PROVISIONS OF THIS ARTICLE AND PRESCRIBE THEIR DUTIES.
  § 147-C. POWERS AND DUTIES OF THE OFFICE. 1. THE OFFICE SHALL HAVE THE
 POWER TO:
  (A)  ADVISE  THE  COMMISSIONER  ON  INCORPORATING  CAREGIVING INTO THE
 DEPARTMENT'S MISSION, WITH THE GOAL OF MAKING SUPPORT FOR THE CAREGIVING
 ECONOMY A FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLE  OF  THE  STATE'S  ECONOMIC  DEVELOPMENT
 APPROACH,  INCLUDING  BUT NOT LIMITED TO, IMPLEMENTING THE PROVISIONS OF
 SECTION ELEVEN OF THIS CHAPTER,  PROVIDING  SOLUTIONS  TO  ANY  RELEVANT
 FINDINGS OF THE REIMAGINING LONG-TERM CARE TASK FORCE ESTABLISHED PURSU-
 ANT  TO  CHAPTER  SEVEN  HUNDRED FIFTY-EIGHT OF THE LAWS OF TWO THOUSAND
 TWENTY-ONE, AND ENSURING ROBUST  FUNDING  OPPORTUNITIES  FOR  CAREGIVING
 ECONOMY PROJECTS;
  (B) ENSURE THAT CAREGIVING ECONOMY CAREERS ARE INCLUDED AS PART OF ALL
 APPROPRIATE  WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT EFFORTS UNDERTAKEN BY THE DEPARTMENT,
 INCLUDING DEVELOPING MATERIALS TO HELP JOBSEEKERS LEARN MORE ABOUT  SUCH
 CAREERS,  PROGRAMMING  TO  INCLUDE  JOBSEEKERS  IN  ALL  OUTREACH BY THE
 DEPARTMENT, AND TRAINING FOR DEPARTMENT STAFF TO KEEP JOBSEEKERS  UP-TO-
 DATE ON CAREER OPTIONS;
  (C)  WORK  WITH  ORGANIZED LABOR AND OTHER STAKEHOLDERS TO ENSURE THAT
 CAREERS IN THE CAREGIVING ECONOMY ARE SUFFICIENTLY FUNDED AND SUPPORTED;
  (D) ENCOURAGE INNOVATION IN THE CAREGIVING ECONOMY, INCLUDING BUT  NOT
 LIMITED TO, MODELS FOR DELIVERING SERVICES AND SUSTAINABLE APPROACHES TO
 LONG-TERM CARE;
  (E)  CONDUCT RESEARCH ON THE REACH AND VALUE OF THE CAREGIVING ECONOMY
 IN NEW YORK STATE, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO GATHERING  DATA  ON  THE
 INDIVIDUALS  SERVED  BY  EACH  SECTOR OF THE CAREGIVING ECONOMY, AND ANY
 AREAS OF THE STATE HAVING A POPULATION THAT IS UNDERSERVED BY THE EXIST-
 ING CAREGIVING ECONOMY; AND
  (F) COORDINATE THE DEPARTMENT'S  DUTIES  UNDER  SUBDIVISION  FIFTY  OF
 SECTION ONE HUNDRED OF THIS CHAPTER.
  2.  ON  OR  BEFORE JANUARY THIRTY-FIRST OF EACH YEAR, THE OFFICE SHALL
 SUBMIT AN ANNUAL REPORT TO THE TEMPORARY PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE AND THE
 SPEAKER OF THE ASSEMBLY DETAILING THE  ECONOMIC  IMPACT  OF  FORMAL  AND
 INFORMAL  CAREGIVING  ON  THE  STATE, AND ANY SAVINGS ACHIEVED BY PUBLIC
 INVESTMENT IN THE CAREGIVING ECONOMY.
  § 2. Section 100 of the economic development law is amended by  adding

  50.  TO  PROMOTE  AND  SUPPORT  THE  CAREGIVING ECONOMY, AS DEFINED BY
