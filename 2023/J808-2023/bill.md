# Commending Rudy Carmenaty upon the occasion of his retirement after five years of dedicated service to the Columbia Law School Association

**Bill ID:** J808
**Session:** 2023
**Sponsor:** Steve Rhoads
**Status:** Adopted

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 808
 
BY: Senator RHOADS
 
  COMMENDING Rudy Carmenaty upon the occasion of his
  retirement  after five years of dedicated service to
  the Columbia Law School Association
 
  WHEREAS, It is the practice of this Legislative Body to take note of
and  publicly  acknowledge  individuals  of  remarkable  integrity  and
character whose endeavors have enhanced the quality of education in this
great State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud to commend Rudy
Carmenaty upon the occasion  of  his  retirement  after  five  years  of
dedicated service to the Columbia Law School Association; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A highly respected entity in the law field, Rudy Carmenaty
has served as President of the Columbia  Law  School  Association  since
July 1, 2018; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rudy  Carmenaty  has  a  vast  education,  he received his
Bachelor of Arts degree with Honors from Rutgers College in 1987 and his
Juris Doctor degree from Columbia Law School in 1990; and
 
  WHEREAS, Formerly the Director of Legal Services from 2011 to  2022,
presently,  Rudy  Carmenaty serves as Deputy Commissioner for the Nassau
County Department of Social Services; and
 
  WHEREAS, Truly an  individual  dedicated  to  his  profession,  Rudy
Carmenaty has been admitted to a myriad of bar associations; he has been
admitted  to  the Bar of the State of New York, the Southern and Eastern
Districts of New York; the Bar of the United States Supreme  Court;  the
Bar of the United States Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces, Court of
Appeals  for  the  Second  Circuit,  Court  of  Federal Claims, Court of
International Trade, Tax Court, Court of  Appeals  for  Veteran  Claims,
Court  of  Appeals for the Federal Circuit, and Court of Appeals for the
District of Columbia Circuit; and
 
  WHEREAS, A testament to his expertise, Rudy Carmenaty  has  received
citations  from the United States Congress, Nassau County Executive, and
the Towns of Hempstead, New York and Oyster Bay, New York;  he  is  also
the  recipient  of  the  President's  Award  from  the Nassau County Bar
Association and has been recognized by the  Hispanic  Brotherhood  Inc.,
the Long Island Hispanic Bar Association, the Kings County Housing Court
Bar  Association,  the  Columbian Lawyer's Association of Nassau County,
Inc., and the New Jersey Folk Festival; and
 
  WHEREAS, Rudy  Carmenaty  has  been  faithfully  dedicated  to  many
professional affiliations such as the Board of Advisors of the Kernochan
Center  for Law, Media & the Arts, Columbia Law School's Public Interest
and Public Service Council,  the  Board  of  Trustees  of  the  Theodore
Roosevelt  Association,  the  Board  of  Directors  of  the  Hispanic
Brotherhood, Inc., the Board of  Directors  of  the  Nassau  County  Bar
Association,  the  American Bar Association & Fellow of the American Bar
Foundation, the NYC  Bar  Association,  the  Long  Island  Hispanic  Bar
Association,  the  Nassau  County  Police  Reserves,  the  International
 
Churchill Society, the United States Naval Institute, the United  States
Navy League, and Kiwanis International; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Columbia Law School Association is indeed fortunate and
blessed  to  have had such a dedicated and respected educational leader;
his wisdom and contributions to the Columbia Law School have truly  made
a  difference in the lives of those fortunate enough to have worked with
him; and
 
  WHEREAS, A significant contributor to education and law in the State
of New York, Rudy Carmenaty has continually demonstrated  an  impressive
dedication,  an unparalleled devotion, and an unremitting enthusiasm for
the quality of the educational programs and  opportunities  offered  for
the  benefit  of  its  faculty  and  students, as well as for the entire
community; and
 
  WHEREAS, In completing his service as President on  June  30,  2023,
Rudy Carmenaty leaves behind more than mere bricks and mortar; he leaves
behind  a  legacy  to the students of the Columbia Law School and to the
residents of New York City; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
commend  Rudy  Carmenaty  upon the occasion of his retirement after five
years of dedicated service to the Columbia Law School  Association,  and
to  wish  him  continued  success in all his future endeavors; and be it
further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Rudy Carmenaty.
