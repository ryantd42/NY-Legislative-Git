 § 1104. OATH AND BOND
  THE DIRECTOR OF THE DIVISION BEFORE ENTERING UPON THE DUTIES OF HIS OR
 HER OFFICE SHALL TAKE AND FILE THE CONSTITUTIONAL OATH OF OFFICE.
 § 1105. COMMISSIONS
  1. THE DIRECTOR SHALL RETAIN OVER AND  ABOVE  ALL  NECESSARY  EXPENSES
 UPON  ALL  MONEYS WHICH SHALL COME UNDER THE CONTROL OF THE DIVISION THE
 SAME COMMISSIONS AS ARE NOW OR MAY HEREAFTER BE ALLOWED BY LAW TO  FIDU-
 CIARIES UNDER SECTION 2307 OF THIS ACT.
  2. THE VALUE OF ANY REAL OR PERSONAL PROPERTY AND THE INCREMENT THERE-
 OF  RECEIVED,  DISBURSED  OR  DELIVERED  SHALL BE CONSIDERED AS MONEY IN
 COMPUTING COMMISSIONS, EXCEPT WHERE THE REAL OR  PERSONAL  PROPERTY  HAS
 BEEN SPECIFICALLY DEVISED OR BEQUEATHED.
  3.  ON THE SETTLEMENT OF THE ACCOUNT OF THE DIVISION IN EACH INSTANCE,
 NOTWITHSTANDING THE PROVISIONS OF SUBDIVISIONS 1 AND 2 OF THIS  SECTION,
 THE  COURT MAY ALLOW REASONABLE AND NECESSARY EXPENSES AND DISBURSEMENTS
 OF THE DIVISION, AND IN ADDITION, TO BE FIXED BY THE COURT.

  4. THE COMMISSIONS AND ALLOWANCES PROVIDED FOR BY THIS SECTION MAY  BE
 RETAINED  BY  THE  DIRECTOR  IN PREFERENCE TO ANY DEBTS OR CLAIMS EXCEPT
 FUNERAL EXPENSES.
  5.  THE  DIRECTOR  SHALL PAY INTO THE TREASURY OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK
 ALL COMMISSIONS AND ALLOWANCES RECEIVED BY THE DIVISION FROM ANY SOURCE.
 SUCH FUNDS SHALL BE USED BY THE CITY TO OFFSET THE EXPENSES OF THE DIVI-
 SION AND THE COMPENSATION PAID TO THE DIRECTOR, OFFICERS  AND  EMPLOYEES
 OF THE DIVISION.
 § 1106. DEPOSIT OF FUNDS
  1. THE DIRECTOR SHALL DEPOSIT ALL MONEYS COLLECTED AND RECEIVED BY THE
 DIVISION  WITHIN  5  DAYS AFTER RECEIPT IN DEMAND, TIME, THRIFT OR OTHER
 ACCOUNTS IN ONE OR MORE BANKS  OR  TRUST  COMPANIES  DESIGNATED  BY  THE
 MAYOR,  COMPTROLLER AND COMMISSIONER OF FINANCE OF THE DEPOSIT OF MONEYS
 OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK. ALL INTEREST RECEIVED ON SUCH DEPOSITS SHALL BE
 CREDITED TO THE RESPECTIVE ESTATES. IN CASE OF THE INSOLVENCY OR  INVOL-
 UNTARY  LIQUIDATION  OF  THE  DEPOSITORY ALL MONEY SO DEPOSITED SHALL BE
 ENTITLED TO EQUAL PRIORITY OF PAYMENT WITH THAT GIVEN BY LAW TO DEPOSITS
 OF MONEY BY THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT OF BANKS.
  2. THE DIRECTOR SHALL WHENEVER REQUIRED  EXHIBIT  TO  THE  CITY  COMP-
 TROLLER  AND  THE  SURROGATE  OF  THE  COUNTY WITH JURISDICTION OVER THE
 ESTATE THE BANK BOOKS OR STATEMENTS AND ALL OTHER VOUCHERS AND DOCUMENTS
 RELATING TO THE DIVISION. THE COMPTROLLER AND  SURROGATE  SHALL  EXAMINE
 THE  BANK  BOOKS AND STATEMENTS SHOWING THE DEPOSITS AND THE VOUCHERS ON
 WHICH THE CHECK IS REQUIRED TO BE DRAWN AND  SHALL  SATISFY  HIMSELF  OR
 HERSELF  FULLY  AS  TO  THE  CORRECTNESS THEREOF AND IN CASE OF DOUBT OR
 DIFFICULTY HE OR SHE SHALL REPORT THE CASE TO THE MAYOR FOR HIS  OR  HER
 DIRECTION.
 § 1107. RESPONSIBILITY OF THE CITY
  1. THE CITY OF NEW YORK SHALL BE ANSWERABLE FOR THE FAITHFUL EXECUTION
 BY  THE  DIRECTOR OF ALL THE DUTIES OF THE DIVISION AND FOR THE APPLICA-
 TION BY HIM OR HER OF ALL MONEYS AND PROPERTY RECEIVED BY  THE  DIVISION
 AND  FOR  ALL MONEYS AND SECURITIES AND THE INTEREST, EARNINGS AND DIVI-
 DENDS THEREON ACTUALLY RECEIVED BY THE DIVISION OR  WHICH  THE  DIVISION
 SHOULD HAVE COLLECTED OR RECEIVED.
  2. ANY PERSON AGGRIEVED BY ANY ACT OR OMISSION OF THE DIVISION AND ANY
 PERSON  ENTITLED TO RECEIVE ANY MONEY OR PROPERTY FOR WHICH THE DIVISION
 MAY BE HELD TO ACCOUNT, SHALL HAVE EACH AND  EVERY  REMEDY  AGAINST  THE
 CITY  OF NEW YORK AS WOULD BE AVAILABLE AGAINST A FIDUCIARY IN LIKE CASE
 AND MAY INITIATE IN THE SURROGATE'S COURT HAVING JURISDICTION A PROCEED-
 ING FOR THE ENFORCEMENT OF HIS OR HER CLAIM OR  RIGHT  AND  SHALL  SERVE
 PROCESS  THEREON  ON  THE  COMPTROLLER OF THE CITY. THE DIRECTOR THEN IN
 OFFICE SHALL BE A NECESSARY PARTY IN SUCH PROCEEDING.
 § 1108. AUTHORITY TO ACT
  THE DIRECTOR SHALL HAVE AUTHORITY TO TAKE POSSESSION  OF,  MANAGE  AND
 COLLECT  THE  RENTS OF THE REAL PROPERTY AND TAKE CHARGE OF THE PERSONAL
 PROPERTY OF AN INTESTATE:
  1. WHENEVER ANY PERSON NOT KNOWN TO HAVE LEFT HIM OR HER  SURVIVING  A
 PERSON  ELIGIBLE  TO  RECEIVE  LETTERS SHALL DIE INTESTATE EITHER WITHIN
 THIS STATE OR ELSEWHERE LEAVING ANY REAL OR PERSONAL PROPERTY  WITHIN  A
 COUNTY LOCATED WITHIN THE CITY OF NEW YORK.
  2. WHENEVER ANY PERSONAL PROPERTY OF SUCH INTESTATE SHALL ARRIVE WITH-
 IN A COUNTY LOCATED WITHIN THE CITY OF NEW YORK AFTER HIS OR HER DEATH.
  3.  WHENEVER ALL OF THE SURVIVING RELATIVES ARE NOT KNOWN THE DIRECTOR
 SHALL CONDUCT A SEARCH FOR ALL LIVING DISTRIBUTEES OF  THE  DECEDENT  BY
 REFERENCE  TO  BANK, POSTAL, AND TELEPHONE RECORDS AND INQUIRY OF NEIGH-
 A. 570  5

 BORS, AND OTHER REASONABLE STEPS UNDER THE CIRCUMSTANCES.  THE  DIRECTOR
 SHALL MAINTAIN RECORDS OF ALL RESPONSES TO SUCH NOTICES.
 § 1109. REPORTS OF DEATHS AND BURIALS
  1. EVERY PERSON KEEPING A HOTEL, BOARDING OR ROOMING HOUSE IN ANY COUN-
 TY  LOCATED  WITHIN  THE CITY OF NEW YORK SHALL REPORT IN WRITING TO THE
 DIRECTOR INFORMATION CONCERNING EVERY PERSON NOT A MEMBER OF HIS OR  HER
 FAMILY  WHO SHALL DIE IN HIS OR HER HOTEL OR HOUSE WITHIN 12 HOURS AFTER
 SUCH DEATH.
  2. THE CHIEF MEDICAL EXAMINER OR SUCH SIMILAR PUBLIC  OFFICER  OF  ANY
 COUNTY  LOCATED  WITHIN  THE  CITY  OF NEW YORK WITHIN 12 HOURS AFTER AN
 INQUEST SHALL REPORT  IN  WRITING  TO  THE  DIRECTOR,  SUCH  INFORMATION
 REGARDING THE DECEDENT WHICH HE OR SHE MAY HAVE.
  3. EVERY UNDERTAKER DOING BUSINESS IN A COUNTY LOCATED WITHIN THE CITY
 OF  NEW  YORK SHALL FILE A REPORT IN WRITING WITH THE DIRECTOR WITHIN 12
 HOURS AFTER RECEIVING AN ORDER FOR THE BURIAL BY HIM OR HER OF ANY DECE-
 DENT HAVING NO KNOWN DISTRIBUTEES OR HAVING BEEN SURVIVED ONLY BY  COUS-
 INS  OR  RELATIVES  BY MARRIAGE, SUCH INFORMATION REGARDING THE DECEDENT
 WHICH HE OR SHE MAY HAVE.
 § 1110. INQUIRY AND SUBPOENA
  1. THE DIRECTOR MAY INSTITUTE AN INQUIRY IN ANY CASE IN  WHICH  HE  OR
 SHE  IS  AUTHORIZED  TO ACT AS TO ANY MATTER AFFECTING THE ESTATE OF THE
 DECEDENT.
  2. FOR THE PURPOSES SET FORTH IN SUBDIVISION 1 OF  THIS  SECTION,  THE
 DIRECTOR  MAY  ISSUE A SUBPOENA OR SUBPOENA DUCES TECUM EITHER BEFORE OR
 AFTER THE ISSUANCE OF LETTERS IN THE NAME OF THE  COURT  WITH  THE  SAME
 EFFECT AS IF EITHER WERE ISSUED BY THE COURT.
  3.  FAILURE  TO COMPLY WITH THE DIRECTIONS OF THE SUBPOENA OR SUBPOENA
 DUCES TECUM SHALL BE PUNISHABLE AS A CONTEMPT OF COURT.
 § 1111. LETTERS NOT REQUIRED FOR SMALL ESTATES
  1. WITHOUT THE ISSUANCE OF LETTERS THE DIRECTOR BY VIRTUE  OF  HIS  OR
 HER  OFFICE  SHALL  HAVE  ALL  THE POWERS OF A FIDUCIARY OF A DECEDENT'S
 ESTATE WHENEVER THE GROSS ASSETS OF THE ESTATE DO NOT EXCEED  THE  MONE-
 TARY  AMOUNT  DEFINED  AS  A  SMALL  ESTATE PURSUANT TO SUBDIVISION 1 OF
 SECTION 1301 OF THIS ACT.
  2. IN THE EVENT THE AGGREGATE SUM OF THE ASSETS OF AN ESTATE IN  WHICH
 THE  DIRECTOR COMMENCES TO ACT PURSUANT TO SUBDIVISION 1 OF THIS SECTION
 SHALL EXCEED THE MONETARY AMOUNT DEFINED AS A SMALL ESTATE  PURSUANT  TO
 SUBDIVISION  1  OF SECTION 1301 OF THIS ACT THE DIRECTOR SHALL FORTHWITH
 APPLY FOR LETTERS.
  3. THE DELIVERY BY THE DIRECTOR TO A DEBTOR, TRANSFER AGENT OR  PERSON
 HOLDING  PERSONAL  PROPERTY  OF THE DECEDENT OF A CERTIFICATE EVIDENCING
 HIS OR HER AUTHORITY TO ACT UNDER THIS SECTION, HIS OR HER  RECEIPT  AND
 THE  SURRENDER  OF  ANY EVIDENTIARY DOCUMENT SHALL CONSTITUTE A COMPLETE
 RELEASE AND DISCHARGE FOR ANY PAYMENT OF MONEY OR DELIVERY  OF  PROPERTY
 MADE  PURSUANT  TO THE CERTIFICATE WITHOUT SUCH PERSON BEING REQUIRED TO
 SEE TO THE APPLICATION THEREOF AND WITH THE SAME EFFECT AS  IF  MADE  TO
 ANY OTHER FIDUCIARY.
 § 1112. APPLICATION FOR LETTERS; WHEN COURT MAY DIRECT PROBATE
  1.  THE  DIRECTOR  IS  AUTHORIZED  TO APPLY FOR AND RECEIVE LETTERS AS
 DEFINED IN THIS ACT.
  2. THE COURT MAY DIRECT THE DIRECTOR TO PRESENT A PETITION FOR PROBATE
 OF A WILL AT ANY TIME AFTER IT IS FILED.
 § 1113. NO SEPARATE BOND OR OATH
  THE DIRECTOR OR ANY OFFICER OR EMPLOYEE OF THE DIVISION SHALL  NOT  BE
 REQUIRED  TO  FILE  THE  DESIGNATION,  SEPARATE BOND OR OATH REQUIRED BY
 SECTION 708 OF THIS ACT BEFORE THE ISSUANCE OF LETTERS TO THE DIRECTOR.

 § 1114. POWERS BEFORE ISSUANCE OF LETTERS
  BEFORE  LETTERS  ARE ISSUED TO THE DIRECTOR THE DIRECTOR IS AUTHORIZED
 TO:
  1. TAKE POSSESSION OF, COLLECT AND SECURE THE PERSONAL PROPERTY WITHIN
 HIS OR HER COUNTY OF ANY PERSON WHO SHALL DIE INTESTATE OR TESTATE WITH-
 IN THIS STATE OR ELSEWHERE OR WHOSE PROPERTY  SHALL  ARRIVE  WITHIN  THE
 COUNTY  AFTER  HIS  OR HER DEATH WHENEVER THERE IS NO PERSON ELIGIBLE TO
 ACT AS FIDUCIARY OF AN INTESTATE OR OF A TESTATOR, IF THE EXECUTOR NAMED
 IN THE WILL REFUSES OR NEGLECTS TO ACT OR IS DEAD OR HIS OR  HER  WHERE-
 ABOUTS ARE UNKNOWN OR IF HE OR SHE IS NOT ELIGIBLE TO RECEIVE LETTERS.
  2.  TAKE POSSESSION OF, MANAGE AND COLLECT THE RENTS OF THE REAL PROP-
 ERTY OF AN INTESTATE WHOSE DISTRIBUTEES ARE UNKNOWN OR, WHOSE  DISTRIBU-
 TEES  IF KNOWN,  ARE NON-DOMICILIARIES OR OF A TESTATOR IF THE EXECUTOR
 NAMED IN THE WILL REFUSES OR NEGLECTS TO ACT OR IS DEAD OR  HIS  OR  HER
 WHEREABOUTS  ARE  UNKNOWN  OR  IF  HE  OR SHE IS NOT ELIGIBLE TO RECEIVE
 LETTERS.
  3. PAY THE FUNERAL EXPENSES OF THE DECEDENT.
  4. SELL AS AND WHEN AUTHORIZED BY THE  COURT  PERISHABLE  PROPERTY  OR
 SUCH  OTHER  PROPERTY  OF THE DECEDENT AS THE PRESERVATION OF THE ESTATE
 REQUIRES.
  5. SELL AT PUBLIC AUCTION ALL PROPERTY DELIVERED TO HIM OR HER AS  THE
 EFFECTS  OF  PERSONS  WHOSE DEATHS HAVE BEEN REPORTED TO HIM OR HER BY A
 HOSPITAL, THE POLICE DEPARTMENT OR ANY OTHER DEPARTMENT OR PERSON.
  6. DEFRAY THE EXPENSES OF THE FOREGOING ACTIVITIES  AND  THE  EXPENSES
 INCIDENTAL TO HIS OR HER APPLICATION FOR LETTERS.
 § 1115. NOTICE TO CONSULS
  WHEN THE ESTATE IS THAT OF AN ALIEN IT SHALL BE THE DUTY OF THE DIREC-
 TOR WHEN MAKING APPLICATION FOR LETTERS TO MAIL A NOTICE TO THE CONSULAR
 REPRESENTATIVE  OF  THE  NATION  OF  WHICH THE DECEDENT WAS A CITIZEN OR
 SUBJECT IF THERE BE ANY IN THE CITY OF NEW YORK.
 § 1116. APPEARANCE BY CONSULS
  1. IN ANY ACTION OR PROCEEDING BROUGHT BY OR AGAINST THE  DIRECTOR  IN
 WHICH  A NON-DOMICILIARY ALIEN IS A NECESSARY OR PROPER PARTY THE CONSU-
 LAR REPRESENTATIVE OF THE COUNTRY OF WHICH THE ALIEN  IS  A  CITIZEN  OR
 SUBJECT  MAY  APPEAR  IN PERSON OR BY ATTORNEY IN BEHALF OF THE ALIEN IF
 THE LATTER SHALL DEFAULT IN APPEARANCE.
  2. NO POWER OF ATTORNEY OR OTHER SPECIFIC  AUTHORITY  FROM  THE  ALIEN
 SHALL BE REQUIRED AS A CONDITION TO SUCH APPEARANCE.
  3. SERVICE UPON AN ALIEN PURSUANT TO PARAGRAPH (E) OF SUBDIVISION 3 OF
 SECTION  307  OF  THIS  ACT MAY BE MADE WITHOUT PRIOR SERVICE OF PROCESS
 PERSONALLY OR OTHERWISE UPON THE ALIEN.
  4. THE INTERESTS OF ALIEN PERSONS UNDER DISABILITY SHALL, HOWEVER,  BE
 REPRESENTED  IN ALL SUCH PROCEEDINGS BY THEIR GUARDIANS OF THE PROPERTY,
 COMMITTEE OF THE PROPERTY OR CONSERVATORS OF THE PROPERTY, OR BY GUARDI-
 ANS AD LITEM APPOINTED BY THE COURT.
 § 1117. GRANT OF LETTERS TO OTHERS
  IF ANY ELIGIBLE DISTRIBUTEE OF THE DECEDENT SHALL PETITION  TO  SUPER-
 SEDE  THE  DIRECTOR WITHIN 4 MONTHS AFTER THE DIRECTOR HAS BECOME VESTED
 WITH THE POWERS OF A FIDUCIARY ON THE ESTATE, LETTERS SHALL  BE  GRANTED
 TO  HIM  OR HER UPON PROOF THAT THE PETITIONER DID NOT RECEIVE NOTICE OF
 THE APPLICATION BY THE DIRECTOR AND UPON THE FURTHER  SHOWING  THAT  ALL
 PERSONS  PRIOR  OR EQUAL IN RIGHT HAVE BEEN SERVED AND HAVE DEFAULTED OR
 WAIVED; BUT THE COURT MAY REFUSE TO SUPERSEDE THE DIRECTOR IF  6  MONTHS
 HAVE  ELAPSED SINCE HE OR SHE BECAME VESTED WITH THE POWERS OF A FIDUCI-
 ARY.
 A. 570  7
 
 § 1118. DELIVERY OF ESTATE ASSETS  TO  FIDUCIARY;  EXPENSES,  COSTS  AND
  COMMISSIONS
  WHEN  THE  DIRECTOR  HAS  BEEN  SUPERSEDED HE OR SHE SHALL ACCOUNT AND
 DELIVER TO THE FIDUCIARY SUPERSEDING HIM OR HER ALL THE PERSONAL PROPER-
 TY BELONGING TO THE ESTATE WHICH HE OR SHE HAS IN HIS OR HER  POSSESSION
 OR CONTROL AFTER DEDUCTING HIS OR HER EXPENSES, COSTS AND COMMISSIONS AS
 PROVIDED BY LAW.
 § 1119. APPEAL; BOND
  1.  THE  DIRECTOR  IS  AUTHORIZED  TO APPEAL FROM ANY ORDER, DECREE OR
 JUDGMENT AFFECTING AN ESTATE IN THE CHARGE OF THE DIVISION OR  IN  WHICH
 HE OR SHE IS A PARTY.
  2.  ON  SUCH  AN  APPEAL  NO BOND ON THE PART OF THE DIRECTOR SHALL BE
 REQUIRED TO STAY EXECUTION OF THE ORDER,  DECREE  OR  JUDGMENT  APPEALED
 FROM.
 § 1120. PROPERTY WITHOUT VALUE WHERE NO ADMINISTRATION HAD
  WHENEVER  THE  DIRECTOR SHALL RECEIVE PAPERS OR PROPERTY OF A DECEDENT
 UPON WHOSE ESTATE NO LETTERS HAVE BEEN ISSUED OR WHOSE PROPERTY  IS  NOT
 SUFFICIENT  TO  REQUIRE  ADMINISTRATION,  THE  DIRECTOR IS AUTHORIZED TO
 ABANDON OR DESTROY ALL SUCH PAPERS AND PROPERTY IN THE POSSESSION OF THE
 DIVISION AFTER 5 YEARS HAVE ELAPSED FROM THE DEATH OF THE  DECEDENT  AND
 UPON PROOF SATISFACTORY TO THE DIRECTOR THAT THEY ARE WITHOUT VALUE. THE
 DIRECTOR,  THE  DIVISION AND THE CITY OF NEW YORK SHALL NOT BE LIABLE OR
 HELD TO ACCOUNT FOR SUCH PROPERTY.
 § 1121. DISPOSITION OF RECORDS AFTER 10 YEARS
  THE DIRECTOR IS AUTHORIZED TO DESTROY THE FILE AND ALL OF  THE  PAPERS
 RELATING  TO  A DECEDENT AFTER THE LAPSE OF 10 YEARS FROM THE COMPLETION
 OF THE ADMINISTRATION OF HIS OR HER ESTATE. THE DIRECTOR,  THE  DIVISION
 AND THE CITY OF NEW YORK SHALL NOT BE LIABLE OR HELD TO ACCOUNT FOR SUCH
 ACTION.
  §  2.  The  surrogate's court procedure act is amended by adding a new

 § 1220. ADMINISTRATIVE BOARD FOR THE OFFICES OF THE  PUBLIC  ADMINISTRA-
 TORS

 ASSOCIATION  OF WHOM 1 SHALL BE A RESIDENT OF THE CITY OF NEW YORK AND 1
 MEMBER EACH SHALL BE SELECTED BY THE  STATE  COMPTROLLER,  THE  ATTORNEY
