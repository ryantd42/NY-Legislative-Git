## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 2397
 
BY: M. of A. Levenberg
 
  COMMEMORATING  the  100th Anniversary of Peekskill
  City School District on April 12, 2024
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to  commend  those
illustrious organizations of the State of New York who have made stellar
efforts on behalf of young people and their futures; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
commemorate  the 100th Anniversary of Peekskill City School District, to
be celebrated on Friday, April 12, 2024, at their Centennial Gala hosted
by the Peekskill Education Foundation at The Factoria at  Charles  Point
in Peekskill, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Paying  tribute  to  this  year's  honorees,  the Lifetime
Commitment Award was received  Mr.  James  B.  Taylor;  alumni  who  are
receiving  recognition  are: STEAM: Ms. Bria Waterman (Arts), STEAM: Dr.
Sitembile Sales (Science), Business: Mr. Chappy Manzer,  Athletics:  Mr.
Lou  Panzanaro,  Jr.,  Athletics:  Mr.  Elton  Brand, Community Service:
Peekskill  Police  Department,  Community  Service:  Peekskill  Fire
Department,  Community  Service:  Peekskill Youth Bureau, Education: Dr.
Bridget Holloman, Elected Official: Mayor Vivian McKenzie,  and  Elected
Official:  Mr.  George  Pataki;  the Gone, but Not Forgotten Award was
received by Regent Judith Johnson and Mr. Daniel Callahan;  and  Friends
of  PCSD  that  are  being  honored  are  Mr.  Wilfredo  Morel  of
Arts10566/SunRiver Health and Mr.  Louie  Lanza  of  Hudson  Hospitality
Group; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Peekskill City School District's mission is to educate and
empower all students to strive for excellence as life-long learners  who
embrace diversity and are contributing members of a global society; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  Peekskill  Education  Foundation,  or  PEF,  is  a
community-based organization dedicated to enriching  and  expanding  the
educational  experiences  and  cultural  opportunities  for all students
within the Peekskill Cit School District; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 1923,  School  District  No.  7,  or  Drum  Hill  School
District,  and  School  District  No.  8,  Oakside School District, were
consolidated to become the Peekskill Union Free School District; and
 
  WHEREAS, Educating  thousands  of  students  over  its  hundred-year
history,  Peekskill  City  School  District  serves  as a beacon for the
Peekskill community and has shown impressive dedication through a myriad
of sports successes and programs; and
 
  WHEREAS, The success of Peekskill City School District is in  direct
correlation to the efforts of its dedicated staff, whose involvement is,
and  always  has  been,  characterized  by  an impressive commitment, an
unbridled enthusiasm, and an uncompromising standard  of  excellence  in
all endeavors on behalf of the organization and the community it serves;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  custom  of  this  Legislative  Body that when
individuals and organizations of such noble aims and accomplishments are
brought to our attention, they should be celebrated  and  recognized  by
all the citizens of this great Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commemorate the 100th Anniversary of Peekskill City School  District  on
April 12, 2024; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted  to  Peekskill  City  School  District  and  the  Peekskill
Education Foundation.
