# Mourning the untimely death and paying tribute to the life and heroic service of Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello who died in the line of duty on March 10, 2024

**Bill ID:** J1974
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** George Borrello
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J1974 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J1974)

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1974
 
BY: Senator BORRELLO
 
  MOURNING  the untimely death and paying tribute to
  the life and heroic service of  Sergeant  Thomas  A.
  Sanfratello  who  died  in the line of duty on March
  10, 2024
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this  Legislative  Body  to  convey  its
grateful appreciation and heartfelt regret in recognition of the loss of
a  courageous  and  decorated  New  York  State  Sheriff's  Sergeant who
dedicated his purposeful life and career in faithful service to both his
family and the residents of New York State; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is with profound  sadness  that  this  Legislative  Body
records  the tragic and untimely death of Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello
who made the ultimate sacrifice while on special assignment  at  Batavia
Downs on Sunday, March 10, 2024; he was 54; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  32-year-veteran of the Genesee County Sheriff's Office,
Sergeant Thomas  A.  Sanfratello  began  his  illustrious  career  as  a
dispatcher  in  February  of 1992; he rose through the ranks, becoming a
deputy sheriff in 1996, and earning a promotion to sergeant in 2007; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his career, Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello  held
various  positions  and roles, including serving as the president of the
Genesee County Deputy Sheriff's Association; and
 
  WHEREAS, Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello exemplified  what  it  truly
means  to  give  of  himself  in  providing vital police services and in
keeping with the noble mission of the Genesee County  Sheriff's  Office;
he was twice honored as the department's Deputy of the Year; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  a  steadfast  and  unwavering  commitment  to  his
community, Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello was also a certified paramedic
and doubled as an active member of the Alexander  Fire  Department  from
1986 to 2003; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Furthermore, he was a graduate of Alexander Central School
District and was previously a member of the Alexander Town Board; and
 
  WHEREAS, An avid sports fan, Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello  favored
the  New  York  Yankees  and  the Buffalo Sabres; he had an overwhelming
collection of Legos and Hot Wheels, and spent much  time  at  model  car
conventions and Motorama racing events across the United States; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  2015, Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello was inducted into
the Model Car Hall of Fame as the Collector of the Year;  he  was  later
presented with the President's Award by the association in 2021; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  devoted  father,  son,  brother,  and sheriff, Sergeant
Thomas A. Sanfratello  loved  spending  time  with  his  family;  he  is
survived  by  his mother, Dorothy; and his three children, Ian, Kyla and
Alexis; as well as numerous other relatives and friends; and
 
  WHEREAS, For more than three decades, Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello
served the Genesee County Sheriff's Office with dedication, loyalty  and
compassion,  earning  the admiration, affection and esteem of his fellow
officers; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello's infinite selflessness and
benevolence will shine on through his family's vivid and happy memories;
his life of service and strength will forever serve as a beacon of love,
light and hope to the countless lives he touched; and
 
  WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a  sense  of
compassion,  Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello leaves behind a legacy which
will long endure the passage of time and will  remain  as  a  comforting
memory  to  all  who  were  privileged  to  have known and loved such an
amazing man; he will be deeply missed  and  truly  merits  the  grateful
tribute of this Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn the untimely death if  Sergeant  Thomas  A.  Sanfratello,  and  to
express its deepest condolences to his family; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the family of Sergeant Thomas A. Sanfratello.
