## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2417
 
BY: Senator BORRELLO
 
  COMMENDING Gregg "Gio" Giannotti upon the occasion
  of  his  designation as the 2024 Italian American of
  the Year by  the  New  York  Conference  of  Italian
  American State Legislators
 
  WHEREAS,  From  time  to  time  this  Legislative Body takes note of
certain extraordinary individuals  it  wishes  to  recognize  for  their
valued contributions to the success and progress of society and publicly
acknowledge their endeavors which have enhanced the basic humanity among
us all; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
commend  Gregg  "Gio"  Giannotti upon the occasion of his designation as
the 2024 Italian American of the Year by  the  New  York  Conference  of
Italian American State Legislators; and
 
  WHEREAS, Successful in the sports radio business, Gregg Giannotti is
the co-host of the hit morning drive sports radio program, Boomer & Gio,
on  WFAN;  also featuring former NFL MVP Boomer Esiason, the show blends
sports talk with general life topics, dominating the ratings as the  top
overall morning show in the market; and
 
  WHEREAS,  After  graduating from Hofstra University, Gregg Giannotti
began working at WFAN in 2005 as an intern, before  taking  a  full-time
position  in  2007  as  a  board  operator; he quickly took on producing
responsibilities and by 2008, he was hosting his own show on the  iconic
station; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rising  through  the  ranks,  Gregg  Giannotti  gained the
opportunity to host CBS Radio's Sportsradio  93.7  The  Fan's  night  in
2010,  when  the  station  launched  a  new  all-sports radio station in
Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania; six months later, he was promoted to host  the
station's  morning  show; later, he would further establish himself as a
prominent voice in sports broadcasting by hosting a weekend show on  CBS
Sports Radio; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Venturing into college sports, Gregg Giannotti served as a
sideline reporter and pregame host for University of Pittsburgh football
in the 2012 and 2013 seasons; and
 
  WHEREAS, Launched in January of  2018,  Boomer  &  Gio  has  enjoyed
continued  success as the premier sports radio morning show in New York;
Gregg Giannotti has used his platform to support a number of  charitable
causes,  helping  to raise money for cystic fibrosis research as well as
the LT Michael P. Murphy Navy Seal Museum; and
 
  WHEREAS, Residing in Sayville, New York, with  him  throughout  have
been  his  wife, Gina, and his two daughters, Sabrina and Summer, all of
whom feel privileged to be a  part  of  his  life  and  rejoice  in  his
achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that when
individuals of such noble aims and accomplishments are  brought  to  our
attention,  they should be celebrated and recognized by all the citizens
of this great Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend Gregg "Gio" Giannotti upon the occasion of  his  designation  as
the  2024  Italian  American  of  the Year by the New York Conference of
Italian American Legislators; and be it further and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Gregg "Gio" Giannotti.
