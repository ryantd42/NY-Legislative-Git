## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1968
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
  HONORING Anthony Ventiera upon the occasion of his
  designation  for  special  recognition  by the Order
  Sons & Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini  Lodge
  No. 2206, on March 22, 2024
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and
pay  tribute  to  those  organizations  of  high  purpose  and  worthy
accomplishment whose endeavors have faithfully served the welfare of the
citizens of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Anthony Ventiera upon  the  occasion  of  his  designation  for  special
recognition  by  the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini
Lodge No. 2206, on Friday, March 22, 2024,  at  the  Westbury  Manor  in
Westbury, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, This year's theme is "La Festa 2024Ã Celebrating Cellini 56!
A Salute to Adele Balsamo, Kathryn Grande, and Anthony Ventiera, Cellini
Lodge  No.  2206 Past Deputies, OSDA! And Special Recognition of Cellini
Lodge Veteran Members"; and
 
  WHEREAS, The mission statement of the  Order  Sons  &  Daughters  of
Italy  in  America  is  "We  endeavor  to  keep  alive Italian culture &
heritage by inspiring members to participate in  the  Italian-  American
experience by sharing traditions and stories through lodge and community
events; and to be charitable in both educational and humanitarian causes
for and beyond our members and community"; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Anthony "Tony" Ventiera was born in Brooklyn, New York, to
Angelina and Carmine Ventiera; he attended Queen  of  All  Saints  grade
school  and  Bishop  Loughlin  Memorial High School before receiving his
Bachelor's and Master's degrees from St John's University; and
 
  WHEREAS, While in college, Anthony Ventiera  met  the  love  of  his
life,  Mary Ann; the couple was united in marriage in 1967, and together
they raised two daughters; and
 
  WHEREAS, For 35 years, Anthony Ventiera worked  in  Corporate  Human
Resources, achieving the title of Vice President; he also worked for the
Town of Oyster Bay and Prudential Real Estate; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 1993, Anthony Ventiera joined the Order Sons of Italy in
America,  Columbus  Lodge  No.  2143,  where  he the ranks to become its
President in 2009; furthermore, for more than 30 years, he has served on
several  committees,  including  27  years  as  chairman  of  the  very
successful Columbus Lodge Annual Italian Feast and Festival; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Anthony  Ventiera attributes his interest in Order of Sons
of Italy in America to his father, who served as Grand Venerable of  the
State  of  New York from 1959-61; he has fond memories of attending many
functions at the old  Romulus  and  Remus  Lodge  on  Myrtle  Avenue  in
Brooklyn;  he also remembers how his parents brought him up with all the
 
Italian traditions, including  the  importance  of  spending  time  with
family; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  a  steadfast  and  unwavering  commitment  to  his
community, Anthony Ventiera is a member of Delaney Council,  Knights  of
Columbus, and is a member of the Massapequa Chamber of Commerce where he
served  as President and is currently serving on its Board of Directors;
and
 
  WHEREAS, A resident of Massapequa Park, New York,  Anthony  Ventiera
enjoys performing with his bands, Time Was and Piece of the Rock; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  addition  to his wife of 56 years, with him throughout
have been two daughters, Christine Maddox and Michele Vollaro, and  four
grandchildren, Joseph, Lauren, Alexa and Christopher; and
 
  WHEREAS, A testament to their dedicated service to others, the Order
Sons  &  Daughters  of  Italy  in  America  maintain a highly successful
scholarship and charity  program,  raising  funds  annually  to  provide
monetary  awards  to  the  numerous  beneficiaries  of the New Hyde Park
community; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is proudly moved to honor and commend
the members of the Order Sons of Italy in America, past and present, who
have labored so conscientiously to secure and  improve  the  quality  of
life for the community it serves; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend Anthony Ventiera  upon  the  occasion  of  his  designation  for
special  recognition  by the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in America,
Cellini Lodge No. 2206, on March 22, 2024; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Anthony Ventiera.
