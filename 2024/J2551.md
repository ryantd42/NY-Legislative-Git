## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2551
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
  MOURNING the death of Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr.,
  beloved family man, devoted law enforcement officer,
  and distinguished member of his community
 
  WHEREAS, There are certain outstanding members of our community who,
through  their selfless commitment and dedication, have served to better
the quality of life in our community and have had a measurable  positive
impact  on  the  lives of its residents; Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr. was
one such individual; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is with  profound  sorrow  and  deep  regret  that  this
Legislative  Body  records  the  passing of Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr.,
noting the significance of his purposeful life and accomplishments; and
 
  WHEREAS, Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr. of  Putnam  Valley,  New  York,
died on Thursday, May 2, 2024, at the age of 42; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Born  on  February 7, 1982, in the Bronx, New York, Alfred
Robert Mosiello, Jr. was  the  cherished  son  of  Alfred  and  Julianne
Torre-Mosiello  and  beloved  younger  brother  of  Dena;  he grew up in
Mahopac, New York, and graduated from Mahopac High School in 2000  where
he  was a defensive lineman on the football team (No. 71) for six years;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr.  continued  his  education  and
love  for  football  at  Hobart  College,  graduating  in  2005  with an
Associate's degree in Engineering and a Bachelor of  Science  degree  in
Economics/Finance,  and  forged  lasting  friendships in his fraternity,
Kappa Sigma; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2015, Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr. married  the  love  of
his  life,  Angela,  with whom he shared 15 years of laughter, love, and
companionship; together, they raised  a  beautiful  family  and  created
countless memories that will be cherished forever; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  dedicated  Police Officer known for his unwavering work
ethic and commitment to excellence, Alfred Robert  Mosiello,  Jr.  began
his  career  with the Village of Pelham Manor Police Department in 2013;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr. went on to join the Westchester
County Police Department in 2021; he was also a  member  of  the  Patrol
Services  Division; he took great pride in his work and was respected by
colleagues and friends, alike; and
 
  WHEREAS, Outside of work, Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr. found  joy  in
hunting,  tending  to  dogs and chickens, riding ATVs with his children,
throwing pool parties, and  often  spending  weekends  with  family  and
friends pursuing his passions; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Alfred  Robert Mosiello, Jr. is most known for his uncanny
ability to always rescue friends in need, regardless of time  or  place;
 
there  is nothing he would not do for a friend or family member in need;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  His  personal  life  was  marked  by  a  profound love and
commitment to his children, Jaxson and Isabella, a role model delighting
in every moment spent with them and imparting wisdom and love  at  every
opportunity; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  addition,  to  his  wife  and  children, Alfred Robert
Mosiello, Jr. is survived by his adored dog, Ruger; his parents,  Alfred
Sr. and Julie; and his sister, Dena (Andrew); and
 
  WHEREAS,  Alfred  Robert  Mosiello,  Jr.'s infinite selflessness and
benevolence will shine on through his family's vivid and happy memories;
his insight and strength will forever serve as a beacon of love,  light,
and hope to the countless lives he touched; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Armed  with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of
compassion, Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr. leaves  behind  a  legacy  which
will  long  endure  the  passage of time and will remain as a comforting
memory to all who were privileged  to  have  known  and  loved  such  an
amazing  man;  he  will  be  deeply missed and truly merits the grateful
tribute of this Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
mourn  the  death  of  Alfred  Robert  Mosiello, Jr., and to express its
deepest condolences to his family; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Alfred Robert Mosiello, Jr.
