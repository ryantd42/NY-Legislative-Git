**Status:** Adopted

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 525
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING  Rosemary Benzo Bonacci upon the occasion
  of her retirement after 30  years  of  distinguished
  service to the Charles T. Sitrin Health Care Center
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense of this Legislative Body to acknowledge
those individuals whose professional lives and civic endeavors serve  to
enhance the stature of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Rosemary Benzo Bonacci upon the occasion  of  her  retirement  after  30
years  of  distinguished  service  to  the Charles T. Sitrin Health Care
Center; and
 
  WHEREAS, For the past 30  years,  Rosemary  Benzo  Bonacci  rendered
faithful,  conscientious,  and valuable service to the Charles T. Sitrin
Health Care Center; and
 
  WHEREAS, In her capacity as Vice President of  Sitrin  Foundation  &
Communications,  Rosemary  Benzo Bonacci served with loyalty, honor, and
distinction; and
 
  WHEREAS, In her official acts, Rosemary Benzo Bonacci  was  governed
by  a  keen  sense  of  duty  and  always showed a unique grasp of human
problems; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born in Utica and currently residing in Whitesboro with her
husband  Michael  Bonacci,  Rosemary  Benzo  Bonacci  holds  degrees  in
Business,  Human  Resource  Management,  and Communications, from Mohawk
Valley Community College, The New School for Social  Research,  and  the
College of St. Rose, respectively; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Working  at  Utica  University  as an Adjunct Professor in
Business & Justice Studies, Rosemary Benzo Bonacci  teaches  courses  in
public relations management, case analysis, and strategic campaigns; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Well-versed  in  her  field  of employment, Rosemary Benzo
Bonacci is adept in non-profit philanthropy, advancement, grant proposal
writing, special events,  and  communications  and  marketing,  and  has
always  proven  to  be  an  asset  to  the Charles T. Sitrin Health Care
Center; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Her  skills  relating  to  strategic  planning,  program
development,  legislative  relations,  media  relations,  crisis
communication, as well as social media management, have allowed  her  to
excel in her field, serving as a role model for others; and
 
  WHEREAS, Active in her community, Rosemary Benzo Bonacci created the
Easter Bag Brigade in 2016, a program to provide custom-made Easter bags
to  Pre-K  children  at  the  Thea  Bowman  House  as  well  as  at  the
Neighborhood Center;  approximately  200  children  receive  these  bags
annually  as  a direct result of the devoted volunteers who consistently
show up for the Easter Bag Brigade; and
 
 
  WHEREAS, In her giving nature, Rosemary Benzo Bonacci is also active
with  Halloween  Bags  for  Bowman  which  provides candy bags for Pre-K
children at the Thea Bowman House yearly; she also participates  in  the
annual  Christmas  Adopt-A-Family  program through the House, as well as
selflessly supports Mother Marianne's West Side Kitchen through  holiday
giving; and
 
  WHEREAS, Rosemary Benzo Bonacci also proudly holds membership on the
Slocum-Dickson Foundation and Sitrin Foundation Boards; and
 
  WHEREAS,  During  her  tenure with the Charles T. Sitrin Health Care
Center, Rosemary  Benzo  Bonacci  earned  the  admiration,  esteem,  and
affection  of  her  colleagues,  and gave unstintingly of herself to the
progression,  growth,  and  success  of  her  career  endeavors;  now,
therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Rosemary Benzo Bonacci upon the occasion of her  retirement  after
30 years of distinguished service; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Rosemary Benzo Bonacci.
