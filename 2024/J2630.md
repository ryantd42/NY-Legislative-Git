**PDF:** [J2630 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J2630)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2630
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING  Fire  Captain  Anthony  Zumpano upon the
  occasion  of  his  retirement  after  36  years  of
  distinguished service to the Utica Fire Department
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense of this Legislative Body to acknowledge
those individuals whose professional lives and civic endeavors serve  to
enhance the stature of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Fire Captain Anthony Zumpano upon the occasion of his  retirement  after
36  years  of  distinguished  service  to  the  Utica Fire Department on
Thursday, June 20, 2024; and
 
  WHEREAS, For the past 36 years,  Captain  Anthony  Zumpano  rendered
faithful,  conscientious,  and  valuable  service  to  the  Utica  Fire
Department; and
 
  WHEREAS, Anthony Zumpano was hired by the Utica Fire  Department  on
May  21, 1988, and diligently worked his way up the ranks, achieving the
positions of Fire Lieutenant (2000) and Fire  Captain  (2009);  he  also
served as a paramedic during his tenure with the department; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Serving throughout the city, Captain Anthony Zumpano spent
the majority of his time at Station No. 7,  located  in  Utica's  Oneida
Square area; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In his official acts, Captain Anthony Zumpano was governed
by a keen sense of duty and  always  showed  a  unique  grasp  of  human
problems; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  him  throughout have been his beloved wife, Lisa and
their three cherished children, all of whom feel privileged to be a part
of his life and rejoice in his achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS, In his capacity as Captain, Anthony Zumpano  always  served
with loyalty, honor and distinction; and
 
  WHEREAS,  During  his tenure with the Utica Fire Department, Captain
Anthony Zumpano earned the admiration,  esteem,  and  affection  of  his
colleagues; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Fire Captain Anthony Zumpano upon the occasion of  his  retirement
after  36  years  of distinguished service to the Utica Fire Department;
and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Fire Captain Anthony Zumpano.
