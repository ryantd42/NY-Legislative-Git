## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1419
 
BY: Senator TEDISCO
 
  COMMENDING Clifford J. Lange posthumously upon the
  occasion  of  his  designation  as  recipient  of  a
  Liberty Medal, the highest honor  bestowed  upon  an
  individual by the New York State Senate
 
  WHEREAS,  Members  of the Armed Services from the State of New York,
who have served so  valiantly  and  honorably  in  wars  in  which  this
country's  freedom was at stake, as well as in the preservation of peace
in peacetime, deserve a special salute from this Legislative Body; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is justly proud to  commend  Clifford
J.  Lange posthumously upon the occasion of his designation as recipient
of a Liberty Medal, the highest honor bestowed upon an individual by the
New York State Senate; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The New York State Senate Liberty Medal was established by
Resolution and is  awarded  to  individuals  who  have  merited  special
commendation  for exceptional, heroic, or humanitarian acts on behalf of
their fellow New Yorkers; and
 
  WHEREAS, Clifford J. Lange was born on June 20, 1941  in  Troy,  New
York  to  Donna and Clifford Lange; he graduated from Albany High School
before being recruited to serve the Nation; and
 
  WHEREAS, Entering into the United States Marine  Corp  on  July  14,
1960,  Clifford J. Lange served as an Aviation Photo Technician with the
Marine Composite Reconnaissance Squadron 2(VMCJ-2), 2nd Marine  Aircraft
Wing; and
 
  WHEREAS,  VMCJ-2  made  its  mark  on  history by becoming the first
Marine unit to be awarded a Navy Unit Commendation in peacetime for  its
electronic  and  photo  reconnaissance  missions tracking the buildup of
Soviet supplied military capabilities in communist Cuba during the Cuban
Missile Crisis; as part of this mission, Clifford J. Lange  would  often
photograph Soviet missiles in Cuba; and
 
  WHEREAS, For his courageous service during the Cold War, Clifford J.
Lange  rose to the rank of Lance Corporal and was awarded the Naval Unit
Commendation, the Good Conduct Medal, and the Marine Corp  Expeditionary
Medal; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Upon  the  completion  of his military service on July 13,
1964, Clifford J. Lange was an insurance agent for Prudential  Insurance
for 38 years, retiring in 2006; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Clifford  J.  Lange  was  married to the love of his life,
Diane, for 51 years, together they had one son James and made a lifetime
of memories which will forever be cherished; and
 
  WHEREAS, Active in his community, Clifford J. Lange  was  a  devoted
member  of  the  Town  of  Malta  Republican  Committee,  serving on the
Planning Boards and as Malta Town Councilman for 26 years, was a  member
of  the  Ballston  Spa  Lions Club, Franklin Masonic Lodge 22, the Malta
 
Rotary Club, and enjoyed taking photos for  the  Malta  Ridge  Volunteer
Fire  Department;  he  was  also  a  member of the Malta Ridge Methodist
Church assisting every year with their Chicken Barbeque; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Clifford J. Lange died on December 30, 2021, at the age of
80; he leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time
and will remain as a comforting memory to all he served and  befriended;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Residents of this great State must never forget the courage
with  which these men and women served their country, and must recognize
that no greater debt is owed than that owed  to  those  who  gave  their
lives  for  their beloved Nation and to those who continue to be missing
in action; and
 
  WHEREAS, Having exhibited his patriotism both at  home  and  abroad,
Clifford  J.  Lange  demonstrated  his  love  for his country and merits
forevermore the highest respect of his State and Nation; and
 
  WHEREAS, Our Nation's veterans deserve to be  recognized,  commended
and thanked by the people of the State of New York for their service and
for their dedication to their communities, their State and their Nation;
now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend  Clifford  J.  Lange  posthumously  upon  the  occasion  of  his
designation  as recipient of a Liberty Medal, the highest honor bestowed
upon an individual by the New York State Senate; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Clifford J. Lange.
