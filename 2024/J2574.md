## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2574
 
BY: Senator SEPULVEDA
 
  HONORING  the  inaugural  projection  of  the
  documentary "Orgullo de Quisqueya" in  New  York  in
  June  of 2024 at Lehman College, and recognizing the
  film's significant contributions  to  the  Dominican
  community and the cultural landscape of New York
 
  WHEREAS,  The State of New York, renowned for its cultural diversity
and vibrant  artistic  community,  proudly  recognizes  and  honors  the
contributions  of  filmmakers who celebrate and promote the heritage and
accomplishments of the Dominican community; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to  honor  the
inaugural  projection  of  the documentary "Orgullo de Quisqueya" in New
York, recognizing the film's profound impact on the  cultural  landscape
of  New  York,  and  commending  the filmmakers for their dedication and
visionary efforts; and
 
  WHEREAS, "Orgullo de Quisqueya," a  2023  documentary  feature  film
with  a  duration  of  90 minutes, produced by Jessica Hasbun and Kelvin
Liria, directed by Tito Rodriguez, and featuring the  cinematography  of
Oliver  Mota  SDC,  along with the screenwriting team of Jessica Hasbun,
Kamila Orizondo, and Junior Rosario, is a  tribute  to  the  resilience,
sacrifice, and dreams of Dominican emigrants; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  documentary captures the stories and memories of nine
distinguished Dominicans living around the  world,  including  Francisca
Lachapel,  Ariel  Suero,  Scarlin  Hernandez, Quemuel Arroyo, Jose Ramon
Genao, Dra. Bernarda Jimenez, Vicente Garcia, Julissa Reynoso,  and  Dr.
Kathleen  Martinez,  showcasing  their  exceptional  contributions  and
achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS, Among these distinguished individuals,  Quemuel  Arroyo,  a
New  Yorker  and  the  Director  of  Accessibility  at  the Metropolitan
Transportation Authority (MTA), exemplifies the dedication and impact of
Dominican immigrants in New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, "Orgullo de Quisqueya" pays homage to Dominicans  who  have
become  ambassadors  of  their  culture,  highlighting  their  courage,
dedication, and the enduring impact they have on their  communities  and
beyond; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  film's inaugural projection at Lehman College in June
2024 marks a significant cultural event for New York, bringing  together
the Dominican and broader Hispanic communities to celebrate their shared
heritage and achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  strategic  foresight  and  leadership  of  the film's
creators, including producers, directors, and  contributors,  have  been
instrumental in bringing these powerful stories to light, ensuring their
place  in  the  cultural  narrative  and  promoting Dominican excellence
worldwide; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  State  of  New  York  acknowledges  the  cultural
significance  of  "Orgullo  de  Quisqueya" and its role in enriching the
cultural fabric of New York, fostering a deeper connection  between  the
Dominican community and their heritage; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That this Legislative Body pauses in its deliberations to
honor the documentary "Orgullo de Quisqueya" and its creators for  their
remarkable  achievements  and contributions to the cultural diversity of
New York; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That copies of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted  to  the producers of "Orgullo de Quisqueya," Jessica Hasbun
and Kelvin Liria.
