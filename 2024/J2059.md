## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2059
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING  Mary  Mandel  upon  the  occasion of her
  retirement after 15 years of  distinguished  service
  to the New Hartford Central School District
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  seeks  to  acknowledge  those
individuals whose  professional  lives  and  civic  endeavors  serve  to
enhance the stature of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Mary Mandel upon the occasion of her retirement on Thursday, August  22,
2024,  after  15  years  of  distinguished  service  to the New Hartford
Central School District, and 34 total years of service to the  field  of
public education; and
 
  WHEREAS,  For  the  past  15  years,  Mary Mandel rendered faithful,
conscientious, and valuable service to the New Hartford  Central  School
District  and  was regarded as a pillar of the school community, proving
pivotal  during  budget  planning  and  aiding  colleagues  in  various
endeavors; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Mary  Mandel  began  working  as Supervisor of the Central
Business Office at Oneida-Herkimer-Madison BOCES, moving on to work as a
business administrator for the  Oriskany  Central  School  District  for
eight  years,  then  as  Assistant  Superintendent  of  Business for the
Holland Patent School District for four years, until she moved on to the
New Hartford Central School District; and
 
  WHEREAS, In her capacity as Assistant  Superintendent  for  Business
Affairs, Mary Mandel served with loyalty, honor and distinction; and
 
  WHEREAS,  As  Assistant  Superintendent  for  Business Affairs, Mary
Mandel oversaw the school district's business office and was responsible
for  developing  the  budget,  supervising  the  buildings  and  grounds
department, overseeing food services and transportation, and more; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  her  official acts, Mary Mandel was governed by a keen
sense of duty and always showed a unique grasp of human problems; and
 
  WHEREAS, During her tenure with  the  New  Hartford  Central  School
District,  Mary  Mandel  earned the admiration, esteem, and affection of
her colleagues; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  Mary Mandel upon the occasion of her retirement after 15 years of
distinguished service to the New Hartford Central School District and 34
dedicated years of service to the field of public education; and  be  it
further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Mary Mandel.
