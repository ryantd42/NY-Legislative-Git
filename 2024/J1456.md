## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1456
 
BY: Senator ORTT
 
  COMMEMORATING the 200th Anniversary of the Town of
  Lockport, New York
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  intent  of this Legislative Body to honor and
commemorate  the  distinguished  histories  of  the  communities  which
comprise the noble body of this great Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern  and  in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
commemorate  the 200th Anniversary of the Town of Lockport, New York, to
be celebrated with a Birthday Celebration on Friday, February  2,  2024;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  Town  of Lockport, New York is a community located in
the south-central part of Niagara County and is surrounded by  the  City
of Lockport, 35 minutes from Niagara Falls to the west and approximately
60 miles from Rochester to the east; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Established  in  1824  and  encompassed  by  farmland, the
Transit North retail corridor, and the Niagara Escarpment, the  Town  of
Lockport  is  home  to the historic Erie Canal which travels through the
northern part of the Town; and
 
  WHEREAS, Proudly home to significant industries, Lockport's  notable
businesses  are the Verizon Media/Yahoo complex, sawmills, data centers,
a brand-new Lockport Memorial Hospital, a campus  of  Mount  St.  Mary's
affiliated  with  the Catholic Health Hospital System, and everything in
between; and
 
  WHEREAS, Furthermore,  the  Town  is  home  to  the  Niagara  County
Historical  Society,  Day  Road  Park, John Austin Nature Trail, and the
Historic Pomeroy School; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Town of Lockport lies within the Lockport  City  School
District, small areas also attend Akron, Newfane, Royalton-Hartland, and
Starpoint  Central  School  Districts  all  of  which  offer  quality
educational opportunities for students; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Promoting  a  business-friendly  culture  for  the  Town's
economic  future,  combined  with  a sense of pride and a historic past,
Lockport is enthusiastically pursuing businesses to locate to its quaint
little town; and
 
  WHEREAS, Remaining fruitful over the ebb  and  flow  of  decades  of
growth  and  change,  the  Town  of Lockport continues its commitment to
enhancing the quality of life  of  its  citizens,  ensuring  a  positive
business,  institutional  and  educational  climate,  and  providing all
essential services; and
 
  WHEREAS, In recognition of the Town of Lockport's rich  history  and
enduring contribution to the State of New York, this Legislative Body is
proud  to pay tribute to this lively town upon the occasion of its 200th
Anniversary; now, therefore, be it
 
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commemorate the 200th Anniversary of the Town  of  Lockport,  New  York,
recognizing  the  significance  of  the role it continues to play in the
State of New York and its communities; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Mark C. Crocker.
