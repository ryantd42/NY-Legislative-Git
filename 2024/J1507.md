## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1507
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING Jack Romagnoli upon the occasion of being
  inducted into the National Wrestling Hall of Fame on
  September 24, 2023
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
outstanding  athletes  who  have  distinguished themselves through their
exceptional performance, attaining unprecedented success and the highest
level of personal achievement; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Jack  Romagnoli  upon  the  occasion of being inducted into the National
Wrestling Hall of Fame as recipient of the Outstanding  American  Award,
which  was  celebrated on Sunday, September 24, 2023, at the Double Tree
Hotel in East Syracuse, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Upstate New York Chapter is dedicated to the main goals
of the National Wrestling Hall of Fame, which are recognizing excellence
in wrestling in Upstate New  York,  preserving  the  rich  and  colorful
history  of  wrestling  in  the  State of New York, and inspiring future
generations of New Yorkers to participate in one of the oldest sports in
the world; and
 
  WHEREAS, Jack Romagnoli of Cazenovia, New  York,  was  motivated  to
participate  in  wrestling  when  he  was involved in an incident in the
school hallway in the eighth grade; his principal suggested joining  the
wrestling  team  in  lieu  of  receiving  detention  for his role in the
altercation; and
 
  WHEREAS, Finding his niche in sports, Jack Romagnoli made the junior
high wrestling team after which he continued using  his  talent  on  the
varsity team; he graduated from Canastota High School in 1972, wrestling
there  from 1968-1972; in 1976, he finished his college education at St.
Lawrence University where he wrestled from 1972-1976 as  a  starter  for
the  Saints  all  four  years; later, he obtained his Master of Business
Administration degree from Syracuse University in 1993; and
 
  WHEREAS, When Jack Romagnoli joined wrestling, Section III dominated
the sport with 80 schools participating; this talented young man won the
league's title and placed in the section's tournaments twice; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 1977,  Jack  Romagnoli  began  his  coaching  career  as
Assistant  Varsity  Coach at Cazenovia High School; four years later, he
served as Head Modified Coach at Canastota High School; and
 
  WHEREAS, As Varsity Co-Head Coach  at  Canastota  High  School  from
1982-1988,  Jack  Romagnoli  produced one New York State champion, three
state place winners, and seven Section III  champions;  his  Red  Raider
teams won two Section III team championships; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Jack Romagnoli was instrumental in placing three Canastota
High School wrestlers at St.  Lawrence University;  two  of  them  were
 
named All-Americans, and one participated on the St. Lawrence University
Wrestling National Championship Team in 1988; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Contributory  to  the  inception of Jamesville-Dewitt High
School's youth program and the reinstatement of the varsity programs  at
Jamesville-Dewitt  High  School  and  Christian  Brothers  Academy, Jack
Romagnoli served as Assistant Coach; additionally, he coordinated  youth
wrestling programs throughout Central New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Jack  Romagnoli  is  Founder  and  President  of  Abscope
Environmental  Incorporated,  a  national  environmental  remediation
company;  he  is also Co-Founder of CCI Companies, Inc., a major highway
and bridge construction company; collectively, the companies employ more
than 200 people, some of whom are former Central New York wrestlers; and
 
  WHEREAS, Active in his community, Jack Romagnoli dedicates his  time
and  support  to various organizations and boards such as Madison County
IDA, Rescue Mission, Canastota  Booster  Club,  Jamesville-Dewitt  Youth
Athletic  Association,  Friends  of  Section  III  Booster Club, and Big
Brothers Big Sisters Utica Chapter; he is also a member of  the  Section
III Wrestling Hall of Fame; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  Jack  Romagnoli  has been his wife of 30 years, Mary
Beth, an accomplished athlete in her own right; the couple's  two  sons,
JT,  a three-time Section III champion, and Andrew received scholarships
to play lacrosse for the University of Delaware; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his life, Jack Romagnoli  has  graciously  given
countless  hours  of  his  time  and  energy  to  the  residents  of his
community,  respected  for  his  ability  to  develop  potential  into
excellence;  his  legacy will endure the passage of time at the National
Wrestling Hall of Fame in Stillwater,  Oklahoma,  where  his  image  and
biography will be consecrated and treasured for generations to come; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that when
individuals of such outstanding athletic accomplishments are brought  to
our  attention,  they  should  be recognized by all the citizens of this
great Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  Jack  Romagnoli  upon  the  occasion  of  being inducted into the
National Wrestling Hall of Fame on September 24, 2023; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Jack Romagnoli.
