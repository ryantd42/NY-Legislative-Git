# Honoring the life and heroic actions of Daniel S. DeWolf, renowned firefighter, devoted father and grandfather, and distinguished member of his community

**Bill ID:** K856
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Brian Manktelow
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [K856 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/K856)

---

## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 856
 
BY: M. of A. Manktelow
 
  HONORING  the life and heroic actions of Daniel S.
  DeWolf, renowned  firefighter,  devoted  father  and
  grandfather,  and  distinguished  member  of  his
  community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this  Legislative  Body  to  convey  its
grateful appreciation and heartfelt regret in recognition of the loss of
a  courageous  and  hardworking firefighter who dedicated his purposeful
life and career in faithful service to both his family and the residents
of New York State; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is with profound  sadness  that  this  Legislative  Body
records the tragic and untimely passing of Firefighter Daniel S. DeWolf,
who  died in the line of duty on Sunday, January 21, 2024, at the age of
52; and
 
  WHEREAS, A 32-year veteran of the Fairville  Fire  Department,  past
Chief  Daniel S. DeWolf served with dedication, loyalty, and compassion,
truly devoting his life and career to helping others; and
 
  WHEREAS, Daniel S. DeWolf was born on May 10, 1971, in  Newark,  New
York, to Edward and Betty (Fisher) DeWolf; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  devoted  and  lifelong local resident, Daniel S. DeWolf
attended Newark High School, during which  time  he  honed  his  skilled
trade in welding and fabricating at BOCES before graduating in 1989; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout his illustrious career, Daniel S. DeWolf served
as a dedicated welder  and  fabricator,  most  recently  showcasing  his
excellence  at  Halstead  Machine,  however, it was his selflessness and
humble nature that truly defined him; and
 
  WHEREAS, Daniel S. DeWolf was a silent force for good,  consistently
helping  others  without  seeking  recognition;  he  demonstrated  this
generous spirit as a 32-year member of the  Fairville  Fire  Department,
where  his  contributions were immeasurable, earning him the prestigious
Fireman of the  Year  Award  multiple  times;  he  tirelessly  undertook
various  tasks  for the department during his long and impactful tenure;
and
 
  WHEREAS, A man of indomitable faith and dedication, Daniel S. DeWolf
was an active member of the Newark Assembly of God; his devotion to  his
church truly reflected his solid values and principles; and
 
  WHEREAS, Furthermore, in his spare time, Dan found joy in riding his
motorcycle and pursuing his passion for hunting; and
 
  WHEREAS,  While  he  was passionate about many things, above all his
greatest passion was spending time with his cherished family, especially
his beloved granddaughter who will forever hold a special place  in  his
heart; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Daniel  S.  DeWolf  is  survived  by his son, Chet DeWolf;
granddaughter, Addyson DeWolf; mother, Betty  DeWolf;  siblings,  Edward
Jr.  (Renee) DeWolf and Greg (Tammy) DeWolf; and a sister-in-law, Carol
Austin; as well as  nieces  and  nephews,  Allison,  Mitchell  (Alissa),
McKenzie,  Aliza,  Christina,  Rebecca,  Jeffrey,  and Aleisha; and many
friends; he was predeceased by his father, Edward Sr.;  and  a  brother,
Jeffrey Austin; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Daniel  S.  DeWolf's infinite selflessness and benevolence
will shine on through his family's vivid and happy memories; his life of
service and strength will forever serve as a beacon of love,  light  and
hope to the countless lives he touched; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Armed  with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of
compassion, Daniel S. DeWolf leaves behind  a  legacy  which  will  long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
who were privileged to have known and loved such an amazing man; he will
be  deeply  missed  and  truly  merits  the  grateful  tribute  of  this
Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  the  life  and heroic actions of Daniel S. DeWolf, and to express
its deepest condolences to his family; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Daniel S. DeWolf.
