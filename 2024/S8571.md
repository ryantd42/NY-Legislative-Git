# Establishes a task force on pandemic learning loss in New York state; and provides for the repeal of such provisions upon the expiration thereof

**Bill ID:** S8571
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** James Tedisco
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S8571 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S8571)

## Summary

Establishes the task force on pandemic learning loss in New York state to assess the impact of remote learning on children's progress in various disciplines; requires a report; provides for the repeal of such provisions upon the expiration thereof.

  Section  1. 1. Task force on pandemic learning loss in New York state.
 There is hereby established a task force on pandemic  learning  loss  in
 New  York  state.  The  purpose  of the task force shall be to conduct a
 comprehensive study on  the  impacts  of  remote  learning  on  children
 enrolled in pre-kindergarten, elementary, and secondary education during
 the COVID-19 pandemic, including but not limited to:
  (a) how much regression in reading has occurred;
  (b) how much regression in math has occurred;
  (c) how much regression in science has occurred;
  (d) how many students were disenrolled from schooling;
  (e) the impact of masking on speech development;
  (f) the impact of remote learning on children's mental health;
  (g) the impact of remote learning on standardized test scores;
  (h)  the  effectiveness of remote-learning compared to classroom-based
 learning; and
  (i) the disparity of the impact on minority  communities  compared  to
 the impact of remote learning on non-minority communities.
  2.  The  task force shall consist of twelve members to be appointed as
 follows:
  (a) three members appointed by the Governor;
  (b) the Commissioner of Education;
  (c) one member appointed by the Speaker of the Assembly;
  (d) one member appointed by the Temporary President of the Senate;
  (e) one member appointed by the Assembly Minority Leader;
  (f) one member appointed by the Senate Minority Leader;
  (g) one member of New York State United Teachers;
  (h) one member of the New York State School Boards Association; and
