## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2192
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
  MOURNING  the  death  of  Joseph  Iantorno,
  distinguished citizen  and  devoted  member  of  his
  community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
citizens  of  the  State  of  New York whose lifework and civic endeavor
served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and the great
State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Joseph Iantorno of Bedford, New  York,  died  on  Saturday,
December 9, 2023, at the age of 88; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Born  in  Port  Chester,  New York, on September 15, 1935,
Joseph Iantorno's parents are the late Guiseppe Iantorno and  Antoinette
Caminitti Iantorno; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Furthering  his  education, Joseph Iantorno graduated from
SUNY New York with a Horticulture Associate Degree; and
 
  WHEREAS, With a passion for landscape and  design,  Joseph  Iantorno
established  a  partnership  with  the  business  Designs  by Lee; after
retiring, he worked at Perennial Gardens; and
 
  WHEREAS, Serving the community for many years, Joseph Iantorno was a
member of the Bedford Fire Department  for  most  of  his  life,  having
joined  in  1962;  he  held  positions  such  as:  Line  Officer, Chief,
President, Chairman Board of  Fire  Commissioners,  and  was  the  Grand
Marshal of the 100th Anniversary Parade in 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Devoting  his time to associations for public good, Joseph
Iantorno was a member of the  Westchester  County  Association  of  Fire
Districts  since  1986,  and  served  as  President  in 1993 until 1994;
Westchester County  Volunteer  Firemans'  Association  since  1986,  and
served  as  Chairman  of Fallen Firefighters Memorial from 1990 to 2023;
Westchester County Association of Fire Chiefs  since  1969,  serving  as
Director  from  1984  to  2002, 2008 to 2023, and President from 2002 to
2005; NYS Association of Fire Chiefs from 1971 to 2023, serving  as  the
Westchester  County  Representative  since 1998; and lastly, Westchester
County Fire Advisory Board since 2006; and
 
  WHEREAS, In  his  free  time,  Joseph  Iantorno  enjoyed  gardening,
camping, and spending time with his beloved family and friends; and
 
  WHEREAS, Joseph Iantorno distinguished himself in his profession and
by his sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of
his community; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Joseph Iantorno's commitment to excellence, and his spirit
of humanity, carried over  into  all  fields  of  enterprise,  including
charitable and civic endeavors; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Joseph  Iantorno  is  survived  by  his beloved wife of 63
years, Rita (Brust) Iantorno; son-in-law, Justin Kernan;  grandchildren,
 
Nicole  Riann,  Marissa  Elise, and Justin Ryan Kernan; brother, Michael
Iantorno; and several nieces and nephews;  he  was  predeceased  by  his
daughter,  Cara  Iantorno  Kernan  and  parents,  Guiseppe  Iantorno and
Antoinette Caminitti Iantorno; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Armed  with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of
compassion, Joseph Iantorno leaves  behind  a  legacy  which  will  long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn the death of Joseph Iantorno, distinguished  citizen  and  devoted
member of his community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the family of Joseph Iantorno.
