# Mandates remote access to public meetings and establishes a fund to assist in implementation

**Bill ID:** A9339
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Anna Kelles
**Status:** In Assembly Committee
**PDF:** [A9339 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/A9339)

## Summary

Mandates remote access to public meetings; establishes a fund to assist in implementing such access.

  Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may  be  cited  as

  §  2. The public officers law is amended by adding a new section 103-b
 to read as follows:
  § 103-B. REMOTE ACCESS FOR OPEN MEETINGS. 1.  FOR THE PURPOSES OF THIS
 SECTION, THE FOLLOWING TERMS SHALL HAVE THE FOLLOWING MEANINGS:
  (A) "HYBRID MEETING" SHALL MEAN A MEETING HELD BY A PUBLIC BODY  WHICH
 IS  CONDUCTED  SIMULTANEOUSLY  IN  A  SPACE  THAT IS OPEN AND PHYSICALLY
 ACCESSIBLE TO THE PUBLIC AND VIA REMOTE ACCESS.
  (B) "REMOTE ACCESS" SHALL MEAN  ACCESS  THROUGH  THE  INTERNET,  VIDEO
 CONFERENCING  OR  OTHER VIDEO TECHNOLOGY THAT ALLOWS A PERSON TO ATTEND,
 OBSERVE, LISTEN, AND, WHEN PERMITTED OR REQUIRED, TO  PARTICIPATE  IN  A
 MEETING OF A PUBLIC BODY.
  (C)  "PUBLIC  BODY" SHALL HAVE THE SAME MEANING AS DEFINED IN SUBDIVI-
 SION TWO OF SECTION ONE HUNDRED TWO OF THIS ARTICLE.
  (D) "MEETING" SHALL HAVE THE SAME MEANING AS  DEFINED  IN  SUBDIVISION
 ONE OF SECTION ONE HUNDRED TWO OF THIS ARTICLE.
  2.  ALL OPEN MEETINGS CONDUCTED BY A PUBLIC BODY SHALL BE CONDUCTED AS
 HYBRID MEETINGS, EXCEPT FOR PUBLIC BODIES  OPERATING  UNDER  A  HARDSHIP
 WAIVER AS DEFINED IN SECTION ONE HUNDRED TEN OF THIS ARTICLE.
  3. WHEN CONDUCTING HYBRID MEETINGS, PUBLIC BODIES SHALL:
  (A) ALLOW MEMBERS OF THE PUBLIC TO ATTEND, OBSERVE, AND PARTICIPATE IN
 REAL-TIME, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE;
  (B)  BE  FREELY  ACCESSIBLE  IN  ALL  AVAILABLE  FORMATS  WITH NO PAID
 SUBSCRIPTION OR TOLL;

  § 3. Sections 110 and 111 of the public officers law, as renumbered by
 chapter 652 of the laws of 1983, are renumbered sections 111 and 112 and
 a new section 110 is added to read as follows:
  § 110. PROCEDURE FOR HARDSHIP WAIVERS. 1. ON BEHALF OF  A  NON-ELECTED
 LOCAL  PUBLIC  BODY WITHIN ITS JURISDICTION, A MUNICIPALITY MAY SUBMIT A
 REQUEST FOR AN ECONOMIC HARDSHIP WAIVER TO THE NEW YORK STATE  COMMITTEE
 ON  OPEN  GOVERNMENT  TO  PROVIDE  A  VIRTUAL OPTION FOR PUBLIC MEETINGS
 PURSUANT TO SECTION ONE HUNDRED THREE-B OF THIS ARTICLE.
  2. THE MUNICIPALITY SHALL DEMONSTRATE IN ITS REQUEST TO THE  COMMITTEE
 ON OPEN GOVERNMENT THAT, DESPITE BEST EFFORTS, THE PUBLIC BODY IS UNABLE
 TO  CONDUCT  HYBRID  MEETINGS  DUE  TO INSUFFICIENT STAFF, RESOURCES, OR
 INFRASTRUCTURE.
  3. A PUBLIC BODY THAT IS GRANTED A HARDSHIP WAIVER SHALL  BE  RELIEVED
 OF  THE  OBLIGATION TO CONDUCT HYBRID MEETINGS AND SHALL INSTEAD CONDUCT
 ITS MEETINGS VIA REMOTE ACCESS. IF  THE  COMMITTEE  ON  OPEN  GOVERNMENT
 DETERMINES  THAT  THE  PUBLIC BODY IS UNABLE TO CONDUCT ITS MEETINGS VIA
 REMOTE ACCESS DESPITE BEST EFFORTS, THE PUBLIC BODY  SHALL  CONDUCT  ITS
 OPEN  MEETINGS  IN  A  PUBLIC PLACE THAT IS PHYSICALLY ACCESSIBLE TO THE
 PUBLIC.
  4. A HARDSHIP WAIVER MAY BE RENEWED ANNUALLY UPON A WRITTEN FINDING BY
 THE COMMITTEE ON OPEN GOVERNMENT, PROVIDED  THAT  THE  MUNICIPALITY  HAS
 DEMONSTRATED EFFORT IN GOOD FAITH TO SECURE FUNDING TO ENHANCE THE TECH-
 NICAL  AND  STAFFING CAPACITY OF THE PUBLIC BODY TO CONDUCT ITS MEETINGS
 IN A HYBRID MANNER, INCLUDING  APPLYING  TO  THE  HYBRID  MEETING  GRANT
 PROGRAM  ESTABLISHED  IN  SUBDIVISION TWENTY-FOUR OF SECTION ONE HUNDRED
 THREE OF THE STATE TECHNOLOGY LAW.
  5. THE COMMITTEE ON OPEN GOVERNMENT SHALL ESTABLISH CRITERIA  FOR  THE
 GRANTING AND RENEWING OF HARDSHIP WAIVERS, WHICH SHALL TAKE INTO ACCOUNT
 FACTORS INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO THE ECONOMIC HARDSHIP OF THE PUBLIC
 BODY, THE LACK OF AFFORDABLE TECHNOLOGICAL SOLUTIONS, THE FEASIBILITY OF

  § 4. The state finance law is amended by adding a new section 99-rr to
 read as follows:
  §  99-RR.  MUNICIPAL  HYBRID  MEETING  TRUST  FUND. 1. THERE IS HEREBY
 ESTABLISHED IN THE JOINT  CUSTODY  OF  THE  STATE  COMPTROLLER  AND  THE
 COMMISSIONER  OF  TAXATION AND FINANCE A SPECIAL FUND TO BE KNOWN AS THE
 "MUNICIPAL HYBRID MEETING TRUST FUND".
  2. THE FUND SHALL BE CREDITED WITH APPROPRIATIONS,  BOND  PROCEEDS  OR
 OTHER MONEY AUTHORIZED OR TRANSFERRED TO SUCH FUND FROM THE GENERAL FUND
 AND  LOCAL ASSISTANCE ACCOUNT, OR ANY OTHER MONIES REQUIRED TO BE TRANS-
 FERRED OR DEPOSITED PURSUANT TO LAW, AND ANY  INTEREST  EARNED  ON  SUCH
 MONEY.  REVENUES DEPOSITED IN THE FUND THAT ARE UNEXPENDED AT THE END OF
 A  FISCAL  YEAR SHALL NOT REVERT TO THE GENERAL FUND AND SHALL BE AVAIL-
 ABLE FOR EXPENDITURE IN THE FOLLOWING FISCAL YEAR.
  3. MONIES OF THE FUND SHALL BE EXPENDED JOINTLY BY  THE  COMMITTEE  ON
 OPEN  GOVERNMENT  AND  THE OFFICE OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY SERVICES FOR
 THE PURPOSE OF ASSISTING MUNICIPALITIES EXPAND THEIR REMOTE  AND  HYBRID
 MEETING  CAPABILITIES  FOR NON-ELECTED MUNICIPAL BODIES SO THAT THEY MAY
 BETTER SERVE THEIR CONSTITUENTS. MONIES IN THE TRUST FUND MAY BE USED TO
 SUPPORT THE REPLICATION AND THE DISSEMINATION OF BEST  PRACTICES  GENER-
 ATED THROUGH THE COMPETITIVE GRANT PROGRAM OUTLINED IN SUBDIVISION TWEN-
 TY-FOUR OF SECTION ONE HUNDRED THREE OF THE STATE TECHNOLOGY LAW.
  §  5.  Section  103 of the state technology law is amended by adding a
 new subdivision 24 to read as follows:
  24. TO DEVELOP AND ADMINISTER A COMPETITIVE GRANT PROGRAM FOR  MUNICI-
 PALITIES TO SUPPORT HYBRID MEETING IMPLEMENTATION AMONG THEIR NON-ELECT-
 ED  LOCAL PUBLIC BODIES. TO THIS END, FUNDS FOR SUCH GRANT PROGRAM SHALL
 BE DRAWN FROM THE MUNICIPAL HYBRID MEETING  TRUST  FUND  ESTABLISHED  IN
 SECTION  NINETY-NINE-RR OF THE STATE FINANCE LAW. SUCH COMPETITIVE GRANT
 PROGRAM SHALL BE SUBJECT TO THE FOLLOWING CRITERIA:
  (A) ALL APPLICATIONS FOR A GRANT  SHALL  INCLUDE,  BUT  SHALL  NOT  BE
 LIMITED  TO, A PLAN FOR ENABLING THE PUBLIC BODY TO CONDUCT HYBRID MEET-
 INGS AND AN ESTIMATE OF THE PROJECTED COSTS OF THE PLAN.
  (B) IN APPROVING GRANT APPLICATIONS, THE OFFICE OF  INFORMATION  TECH-
 NOLOGY SERVICES MAY GIVE PREFERENCE TO RURAL AREAS.
  (C)  MUNICIPALITIES  WHICH  ARE AWARDED FUNDS THROUGH SUCH COMPETITIVE
 GRANT PROGRAM SHALL SUBMIT  TO  THE  OFFICE  OF  INFORMATION  TECHNOLOGY
 SERVICES  AND  THE  COMMITTEE FOR OPEN GOVERNMENT A REPORT DETAILING HOW
 SUCH FUNDS HAVE BEEN EXPENDED  AND  THE  PROGRESS  THAT  HAS  BEEN  MADE
 TOWARDS THE IMPLEMENTATION OF HYBRID MEETINGS WITHIN THE MUNICIPALITY.
  (D) ANNUALLY, NOT LATER THAN DECEMBER FIRST, THE OFFICE OF INFORMATION
 TECHNOLOGY  SERVICES  SHALL  SUBMIT A REPORT DETAILING EXPENDITURES FROM
 THE TRUST FUND TO THE CLERKS OF THE SENATE AND ASSEMBLY, THE  CHAIRS  OF
 THE  SENATE  AND  ASSEMBLY  COMMITTEES  ON  WAYS AND MEANS, THE ASSEMBLY
 SUBCOMMITTEE ON INTERNET AND NEW TECHNOLOGY,  AND  THE  SENATE  STANDING
 COMMITTEE ON INTERNET AND TECHNOLOGY.
