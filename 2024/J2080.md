## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2080
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
  MOURNING  the  death  of  Judith Anderson, devoted
  wife  and  mother,  distinguished  citizen,  and
  dedicated member of her community
 
  WHEREAS, There are certain outstanding members of our community who,
through  their selfless commitment and dedication, have served to better
the quality of life in our community and have had a measurable  positive
impact  on  the  lives  of  its  residents; Judith Anderson was one such
individual; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is with  profound  sorrow  and  deep  regret  that  this
Legislative  Body  records  the  passing  of Judith Anderson, a woman of
devout faith renowned for her ability to  bring  comfort  to  those  who
needed it most; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Judith  Anderson  of  Croton-on-Hudson,  New York, died on
Friday, June 26, 2023, at the age of 75; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Well-known  for  her  service  as  Lay  Chaplain  for  the
Croton-on-Hudson  Fire Department for several years, Judith Anderson was
a source of comfort and warmth for many members and brought a  sense  of
faith to firefighters and their loved ones; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Born  in  Queens,  New  York,  in  1947,  Judith  Anderson
graduated  from  Dominican  Commercial  High  School  in  1964,  before
beginning  her  career  at  the William J. Burns International Detective
Agency in Manhattan at the young age of 16; and
 
  WHEREAS, After relocating to Westchester for the agency, Judith  met
and married the love of her life, Robert Anderson; the couple went on to
build  their home in Croton-on-Hudson, and celebrated 50 wonderful years
of marriage in October of 2022; and
 
  WHEREAS, In addition to her service with the fire department, Judith
Anderson gave tirelessly of her time and energies toward drug  addiction
counseling  and  rehabilitation  programs throughout New York State with
Father Daniel Egan of the Franciscan Friars of the Atonement at Graymoor
in Garrison; and
 
  WHEREAS, Furthermore, she spent many weekends helping  residents  at
St.  Francis Farm in Lacona and New Hope Manor in Barryville, and served
as the co-Director of the Hudson Valley Coalition  for  Life;  she  also
served her community as a Eucharistic Minister for the Holy Name of Mary
Church and as a religious education teacher; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Judith  Anderson is survived by her husband, Robert; their
four children, Lee,  Michael,  Debra,  and  Megan;  five  grandchildren,
Mason,  Logan,  Livia,  Saoirse,  and  Levon;  two  sisters, Dolores and
Jeanne; a brother, Thomas; and many other family members  including  her
cherished nieces and nephews; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Judith  Anderson's  infinite  selflessness and benevolence
will shine on through her family's vivid and happy memories; her insight
 
and strength will forever serve as a beacon of love, light and  hope  to
the countless lives she touched; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Armed  with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of
compassion, Judith Anderson leaves  behind  a  legacy  which  will  long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
who  were  privileged to have known and loved such an amazing woman; she
will be deeply missed and truly merits  the  grateful  tribute  of  this
Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn  the  death  of  Judith  Anderson,  and  to  express  its  deepest
condolences to her family; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the family of Judith Anderson.
