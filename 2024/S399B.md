# Relates to the enactment of the safer consumption services pilot program act

**Bill ID:** S399BB
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Gustavo Rivera
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S399BB PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S399BB)

## Summary

Enacts the safer consumption services pilot program act which provides for the establishment of a pilot program to provide overdose prevention center pilot programs.

 OR  PARTICIPANT  OF  THE PROGRAM. THE APPOINTED COMMUNITY LIAISON AS SET
 FORTH IN SUBDIVISION THREE OF THIS SECTION SHALL BE ONE OF  THE  MEMBERS
 OF THE COMMUNITY ADVISORY BOARD.

 COLLABORATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT;

 THE PILOT PROGRAM OPERATIONS, ADDRESS ANY CONCERNS, AND WORK WITH  LOCAL
 COMMUNITY  STAKEHOLDERS  SUCH AS COMMUNITY BASED ORGANIZATIONS PROVIDING

  §  3399-E.  REPORTING.  1.  AN ENTITY OPERATING AN OVERDOSE PREVENTION
 CENTER PILOT PROGRAM UNDER THIS ARTICLE SHALL PROVIDE AN  ANNUAL  REPORT
 TO  THE DEPARTMENT AT A DATE SET BY THE DEPARTMENT. SUCH REPORT SHALL BE
 POSTED ON THE DEPARTMENT'S WEBSITE AND SHALL INCLUDE:
  (A) THE NUMBER OF PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS;
  (B) AGGREGATE INFORMATION REGARDING  THE  CHARACTERISTICS  OF  PROGRAM
 PARTICIPANTS;
  (C)  THE NUMBER OF HYPODERMIC NEEDLES AND SYRINGES DISTRIBUTED FOR USE
 ON-SITE;
  (D) THE NUMBER OF  OVERDOSES  EXPERIENCED,  THE  NUMBER  OF  OVERDOSES
 INTERVENED ON-SITE, AND THE OUTCOMES; AND
  (E)  THE NUMBER OF INDIVIDUALS DIRECTLY AND FORMALLY REFERRED TO OTHER
 SERVICES AND THE TYPE OF SERVICE.
  2. WITHIN SIX MONTHS OF THE CONCLUSION OF THE INITIAL FIVE-YEAR  PILOT
 PROGRAM, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ISSUE A REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR, THE TEMPO-
 RARY  PRESIDENT  OF  THE  SENATE,  AND THE SPEAKER OF THE ASSEMBLY. SUCH
 REPORT SHALL CONTAIN, AT A MINIMUM:
  (A) AGGREGATE  INFORMATION  REGARDING  THE  CENTERS  UNDER  THE  PILOT
 PROGRAM;
  (B) THE FEASIBILITY OF CONTINUING THE PILOT PROGRAM;
  (C)  THE  FEASIBILITY  OF  INCREASING THE NUMBER OF PROGRAMS UNDER THE
 PILOT PROGRAM;
  (D) WHETHER THE PILOT PROGRAM IS WORKING AS INTENDED; AND
  (E) ANY OTHER INFORMATION RELEVANT TO THE PILOT PROGRAM.
  § 3399-F. IMMUNITY PROVIDED. NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER STATUTE, LAW OR
 RULE TO THE CONTRARY, THE  FOLLOWING  PERSONS  SHALL  NOT  BE  ARRESTED,
 CHARGED,  OR  PROSECUTED  FOR  ANY CRIMINAL OFFENSE OR BE SUBJECT TO ANY
 CIVIL OR ADMINISTRATIVE PENALTY,  INCLUDING  SEIZURE  OR  FORFEITURE  OF
 ASSETS OR REAL PROPERTY OR DISCIPLINARY ACTION BY A PROFESSIONAL LICENS-
 ING BOARD, OR BE DENIED ANY RIGHT OR PRIVILEGE, SOLELY FOR PARTICIPATION
 OR  INVOLVEMENT  IN AN OVERDOSE PREVENTION CENTER PILOT PROGRAM APPROVED
 BY THE DEPARTMENT SO LONG AS SUCH PARTICIPATION OR  INVOLVEMENT  WAS  IN
 GOOD FAITH AND IN ACCORDANCE WITH ESTABLISHED PROTOCOLS PURSUANT TO THIS
 ARTICLE:
  1. A PARTICIPANT;
  2.  A  STAFF MEMBER OR ADMINISTRATOR OF A PROGRAM, INCLUDING A HEALTH-
 CARE PROFESSIONAL, MANAGER, EMPLOYEE, BOARD MEMBER, DIRECTOR, OR  VOLUN-
 TEER; OR
  3.  A  PROPERTY  OWNER WHO OWNS PROPERTY AT WHICH A PROGRAM IS LOCATED
 AND OPERATES.
  § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
