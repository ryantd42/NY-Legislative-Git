## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 1014
 
BY: M. of A. Buttenschon
 
  HONORING  the  legacy  of  James  Blackshear  and
  commemorating  the  15th  Annual  James  Blackshear
  Memorial Prayer Luncheon on April 4, 2024
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative Body to publicly
recognize those extraordinary individuals who have made contributions to
the cultural enrichment of the State of New York, and to the success and
progress of society during their life's work; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
the  legacy  of  James  Blackshear and commemorate the 15th Annual James
Blackshear Memorial Prayer Luncheon on Thursday, April 4, 2024, at  Club
Monarch in Yorkville, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Blackshear, a native of Utica, New York, received his
bachelor's degree from the State University of New York (SUNY) Institute
of Technology; and
 
  WHEREAS, As the chief financial officer of Hope Chapel for 20 of his
40  years  of  membership,  James  Blackshear  served  with  unwavering
commitment and dedication; he also joined the United  States  Navy  from
1967 to 1971, and was later employed by UNIVAC; and
 
  WHEREAS,  James  Blackshear  served as the Executive Director of the
Cosmopolitan  Center  for  22  years  where  he  diligently  worked  to
positively impact his community; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A testament to community involvement, James Blackshear was
chairman of the Utica Human Relations Commission, a commissioner of  the
New  York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation, a
member of the African-American Coalition of Oneida County, and the Utica
Minority Coalition; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Blackshear has demonstrated  a  belief  in  community
service through not just words, but through his meaningful and impactful
contributions and hard work; and
 
  WHEREAS,  James  Blackshear  also served as president of the Kiwanis
Club of Utica, was honored in 1991 as  the  winner  of  the  Len  Wilbur
Award,  and  was  the  first recipient of the Mohawk Valley Frontiersman
Martin Luther King Jr. Award; he was inducted into the MVCC Hall of Fame
in 2000, as well as the Oneida County Historical Society Hall of Fame in
2005; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  significant  contributions  of  James  Blackshear
throughout  his  distinguished  career  and  enduring legacy, unwavering
dedication, remarkable vision, and renowned leadership for opportunities
for marginalized communities and the greater Utica  community  at  large
are hereby recognized and celebrated at the Memorial Prayer Luncheon and
by the citizens of this great State and Nation; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor the legacy of James Blackshear and  commemorate  the  15th  Annual
James Blackshear Memorial Prayer Luncheon on April 4, 2024.
