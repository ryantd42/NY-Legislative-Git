## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1759
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING  Arnold  Galin of Rome, New York upon the
  occasion of his special recognition for 50 years  of
  service to Rome Community Theater
 
  WHEREAS,  Individuals  who give of their time and energies and serve
the best interests of their communities are an asset beyond remuneration
and cannot be sufficiently extolled; and
 
  WHEREAS, Arnold Galin has given not only of his  time  and  energies
but also of his competence, intelligence and leadership and consequently
has been designated for special honor; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions this Legislative Body is justly proud to  honor
Arnold  Galin  of  Rome,  New  York  upon  the  occasion  of his special
recognition for 50 years of service to Rome Community Theater; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born in the Bronx in New York, New York, Arnold Galin moved
to Rome, New York to pursue a career with  BOCES  in  the  early  1970s;
after  attending  SUNY  Albany, where he developed a love for acting, he
aspired to become involved in community theater in the Rome area; and
 
  WHEREAS, Whether he's working backstage, building sets, or  learning
all  different  aspects  of  working with theatrical productions, Arnold
Galin has a strong passion for the arts; he started  working  with  Rome
Community  Theater  in  the  1970s  and started auditioning within a few
years and his first part was a small role in "Lion in Winter"  in  1975,
followed by a larger role in "A Thousand Clowns" in 1976; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Inspired  by  Rome Community Theater, Arnold Galin started
directing with "Charley's Aunt" in 1978; in addition, he served  as  the
troupe's  photographer  and  grant  writer,  introduced  Rome  Community
Theater to automated ticketing, and helped bring  in  money  for  better
curtains  and  other  theater  facelifts,  including  some  acting  and
directing workshops over the years; and
 
  WHEREAS, Passionate about acting and enticed with the idea of "being
someone else" while performing, Arnold Galin  has  either  acted  in  or
directed 58 plays for Rome Community Theater; he also served as the Rome
Free  Academy  drama  club advisor from 1980 to 2015, directing numerous
plays and musicals over the decades; and
 
  WHEREAS, Arnold Galin has joined an exciting  new  venture  to  help
grow  a  brand new theater program called the Black Bear Players of View
in Old Forge, New York; his new project will be directing "Almost Maine"
at the Views Arts Center  in  Old  Forge  this  spring,  with  auditions
beginning in March; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  only art center in the lower Adirondacks that is open
year-round, The View Arts Center is the perfect space for the Black Bear
Players to  piece  together  their  stage  productions;  driven  by  the
community and led by Arnold Galin, there is much talent in the Rome area
 
and  this  project  allows like-minded people to come together and enjoy
the arts; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Arnold  Galin  has been called upon to contribute his time
and talents to countless civic and charitable endeavors and  has  always
given of himself unstintingly; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout  the  entire period of his community service, a
period of constructive involvement, Arnold Galin has stood  constant  in
dignity, good grace and humor; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rare  indeed  is  the  impressive  dedication  shown by an
individual for the benefit of others which Arnold  Galin  has  displayed
throughout his life; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Arnold Galin of Rome, New York upon the occasion  of  his  special
recognition for 50 years of service to Rome Community Theater; and be it
further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Arnold Galin and Rome Community Theater.
