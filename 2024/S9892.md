# Establishes the study commission on preventing snow emergencies

**Bill ID:** S9892
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** George Borrello
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S9892 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S9892)

## Summary

Establishes the study commission on preventing snow emergencies to evaluate the impact of grid electrification on the safety and reliability of heating systems in snowstorms that cause power outages, and to develop strategies and best practices to maximize grid reliability and resiliency during extreme winter weather events.

  THE  PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND ASSEM-
 BLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:

  Section 1.  There  is  hereby  established  the  study  commission  on
 preventing  snow  emergencies,  hereinafter  referred  to  as the "study
 commission". The purpose of the study  commission  is  to  evaluate  the
 impact  of grid electrification on the safety and reliability of heating
 systems in snowstorms that cause power outages, and to  develop  strate-
 gies  and  best  practices  to  maximize grid reliability and resiliency
 during extreme winter weather events.
  § 2. The study commission shall consist of fifteen members as follows:
  1. the chair of the public service commission, or such chair's  desig-
 nee;
  2. one member to be appointed by the governor;
  3. one member to be appointed by the speaker of the assembly;
  4. one member to be appointed by the minority leader of the assembly;
  5.  one  member  to  be  appointed  by  the temporary president of the
 senate;
  6. one member to be appointed by the minority leader of the senate;
  7. the chair of the senate energy and telecommunications committee;
  8. the ranking minority member of the senate energy  and  telecommuni-
 cations committee;
  9. the chair of the assembly energy committee;
  10. the ranking minority member of the assembly energy committee;
  11.  three  members  to be appointed each by the three largest utility
 companies servicing Western New York;
  12. the commissioner of the division of homeland security and emergen-
 cy services, or such commissioner's designee; and
  13. one member to  be  appointed  by  the  largest  trade  association
 representing electrical workers in Western New York.

  §  3.  The  chair  of  the  public service commission, or such chair's
 designee, shall be designated as the chairperson of  the  study  commis-
 sion.  The members of the study commission shall serve without compen-
 sation, except that members shall be allowed their necessary and  actual
 expenses incurred in the performance of their duties under this act.
  § 4. The study commission shall be authorized to hold public hearings,
 and  to consult with any organization, educational institution, or other
 government entity or person, to enable it to accomplish its duties.
  § 5. To effectuate the purposes of this act, the study commission  may
 request  and shall receive from any department, division, board, bureau,
 commission, or other agency of the state or any state  public  authority
 such  assistance, information, and data as will enable the study commis-
 sion to properly carry out its powers and duties hereunder.
  § 6. The study commission shall issue a report  of  its  findings  and
 legislative  recommendations  to the governor, the speaker of the assem-
 bly, the minority leader of the assembly, the temporary president of the
 senate, and the minority leader of the senate.
  § 7. The public service commission shall use  the  study  commission's
 report's  findings and recommendations to determine whether, how, and on
 what timetable to modify the targets  set  forth  in  subdivision  2  of
 section  66-p  of  the public service law. The public service commission
 shall consult with the department of  environmental  conservation  about
 whether,  how,  and on what timetable to modify the targets set forth in
 subdivision 1 of section 75-0107 of the environmental conservation  law,
 based  upon the study commission's report's findings and recommendations
 issued pursuant to this act.
  § 8. This act shall take effect immediately and shall  expire  and  be
 deemed repealed two years after it shall have become a law.
