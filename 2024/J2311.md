**Status:** Adopted

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2311
 
BY: Senator TEDISCO
 
  HONORING  Tom  Chestnut upon the occasion of being
  inducted  into  the  Schenectady  School  District
  Athletic Hall of Fame
 
  WHEREAS,  It is the sense of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
outstanding athletes who have  distinguished  themselves  through  their
exceptional performance, attaining unprecedented success and the highest
level of personal achievement; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern  and  in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Tom Chestnut upon the occasion of being inducted  into  the  Schenectady
School  District Athletic Hall of Fame on Monday, September 16, 2024, at
The Event Center at Rivers Casino & Resort, Schenectady, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, A three-sport athlete at Linton High School,  Tom  Chestnut
played basketball for Schenectady Hall of Famer Walt Przybylo, football;
and played tennis on Linton's 1964 Sectional championship team; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Tom  Chestnut  graduated  high  school  a  year  early and
attended Princeton University where his 1966-67 basketball team finished
25-3, won the Ivy League title and garnered a ranking as high as  number
three in the nation, and finished fifth in the final poll; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  the 1968-69 season, Tom Chestnut scored 22 points with
13 rebounds in a game vs. Harvard leading  Princeton  to  a  second  Ivy
League  title  during his tenure where the team finished with an overall
record of 19-7; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Following  his  collegiate  career,  Tom  Chestnut  played
semi-pro  basketball  with  the  Schaefer Brewers, and pro basketball in
Europe for three years where he was a two-time scoring champion  in  the
Dutch League; and
 
  WHEREAS,  After  his  playing  days,  Tom  Chestnut forged a stellar
career as a sports executive serving as President  and  Chief  Operating
Officer  of  the  NBA's  Cleveland  Cavaliers  from  1990-95,  and Chief
Operating Officer of the Philadelphia 76ers during the  1995-96  season;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Tom  Chestnut  continued  demonstrating his administrative
acumen as Vice President and General Manager of Sports Channel New York,
which later became  Fox  Sports  Net,  overseeing  production  of  games
involving the Mets, Yankees, Islanders, Devils & Nets; and
 
  WHEREAS,  An  Upstate  New  York Basketball Hall of Fame member, Tom
Chestnut has been an inspiration to countless basketball fans,  as  well
as  to  the  community-at-large,  and accordingly merits recognition and
applause for his tremendous passion for the sport  and  his  competitive
spirit; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  custom  of  this  Legislative  Body that when
individuals of such outstanding athletic accomplishments are brought  to
 
our  attention,  they  should  be recognized by all the citizens of this
great Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Tom  Chestnut  upon  the  occasion  of  being  inducted  into  the
Schenectady  School  District  Athletic  Hall  of  Fame,  and  for  his
extraordinary  accomplishments  which  were  a  tribute  to  his  skill,
dedication and commitment; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Tom Chestnut.
