# Provides for penalties for violations of the alcoholic beverage control law, requirements for certain retail licensees for on-premises consumption and labeling of disposable cups and similar to-go containers

**Bill ID:** S4961
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Robert Jackson
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S4961 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S4961)

## Summary

Provides for a fixed schedule of penalties for violations of the alcoholic beverage control law, requirements for certain retail licensees for on-premises consumption and labeling of disposable cups and similar to-go containers containing alcoholic beverages.

  Section 1. Subdivisions 2 and 3 of section 119 of the alcoholic bever-

 1995, and subdivision 3 as amended by chapter 658 of the laws  of  1989,
 are amended and a new subdivision 6 is added to read as follows:
  2.  The  liquor authority may on its own initiative or on complaint of
 [any person] A GOVERNMENT AGENCY institute proceedings to revoke, cancel
 or suspend any retail license and may impose a civil penalty against the
 licensee after a hearing at which the licensee shall be given an  oppor-

 THE ALLEGED VIOLATION OF THE PROVISIONS OF THIS CHAPTER in  such  manner

 authority.
  3. All other licenses or permits issued  under  this  chapter  may  be

 manner to be determined by the rules of the liquor authority. THE LIQUOR
 AUTHORITY SHALL ESTABLISH A FIXED CIVIL PENALTY FEE SCHEDULE WHICH SHALL
 BE BASED ON THE SEVERITY OF THE VIOLATION  OF  THE  PROVISIONS  OF  THIS
 CHAPTER.  SUCH  FEE SCHEDULE SHALL BE PROVIDED TO EACH LICENSEE UPON THE
 ISSUANCE  OF  RENEWAL  OF  SUCH  LICENSEE'S  LICENSE  PURSUANT  TO  THE
 PROVISIONS OF THIS CHAPTER.

  6.  NOTWITHSTANDING  ANY  PROVISION OF LAW TO THE CONTRARY, THE LIQUOR
 AUTHORITY SHALL ONLY SUSPEND THE LICENSE OF A  LICENSEE  WITHOUT  NOTICE
 WHERE  SUCH  LICENSEE  HAS  RECEIVED  NOTICE BY THE AUTHORITY OR ANOTHER
 REGULATORY AGENCY IN THE PRECEDING THIRTY DAYS THAT SUCH LICENSEE IS  IN
 VIOLATION OF A PROVISION OF THIS CHAPTER OR A RULE OR REGULATION PROMUL-
 GATED  THEREUNDER  WHICH HAS BEEN IN EFFECT NO LESS THAN FORTY-FIVE DAYS
 PRIOR TO SUCH VIOLATION.
  § 2. The alcoholic beverage control law is amended  by  adding  a  new
 section 106-c to read as follows:
  §  106-C.  FRONTAGE  RESPONSIBILITY IN CITIES WITH A POPULATION OF ONE
 MILLION OR MORE. 1. A RETAIL LICENSEE FOR ON-PREMISES CONSUMPTION  IN  A
 CITY  WITH A POPULATION OF ONE MILLION OR MORE SHALL ONLY BE RESPONSIBLE
 FOR VIOLATIONS WHICH OCCUR IN LICENSED AREAS.
  2. (A) AS USED IN THIS SECTION, THE TERM "LICENSED AREA" SHALL MEAN:
  (I) INDOOR PREMISES;
  (II) OUTDOOR SIDEWALK CAFES LICENSED BY THE CITY OF NEW YORK; AND
  (III) OUTDOOR SEATING AREAS AUTHORIZED AND INSPECTED BY  THE  CITY  OF
 NEW YORK.
  (B)  AS  USED  IN  THIS  SECTION,  THE  TERM "LICENSED AREA" SHALL NOT
 INCLUDE:
  (I) SIDEWALK SPACE WHICH IS CONSIDERED A RIGHT OF  WAY  OR  PEDESTRIAN
 CLEAR PATH; AND
  (II) OPEN STREET RESTAURANT AREAS THAT ARE NOT CURRENTLY BEING USED OR
 HAVE NOT BEEN USED BY THE LICENSEE IN THE PRECEDING THIRTY MINUTES.
  §  3.  The  alcoholic  beverage control law is amended by adding a new
 section 107-b to read as follows:
  § 107-B. LABELING OF DISPOSABLE  CUPS  AND  SIMILAR  TO-GO  CONTAINERS
 CONTAINING  ALCOHOLIC  BEVERAGES.  THE LIQUOR AUTHORITY SHALL PROMULGATE
 RULES AND REGULATIONS GOVERNING THE  LABELING  OF  DISPOSABLE  CUPS  AND
 SIMILAR  TO-GO CONTAINERS CONTAINING ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES. SUCH RULES AND
 REGULATIONS SHALL INCLUDE, BUT NOT BE LIMITED TO, A REQUIREMENT THAT ALL
 RETAIL LICENSEES FOR ON-PREMISES CONSUMPTION THAT HAVE OUTDOOR  LICENSED
 SPACE  AND  ARE  WITHIN  ONE  HUNDRED  FEET  OF  A LICENSED PREMISES ARE
 REQUIRED TO HAVE DISPOSABLE CUPS THAT HAVE MARKINGS  WHICH  INCLUDE  THE
 NAME OF THE BUSINESS OF THE LICENSEE IN A FONT GREATER THAN ONE INCH AND
 THE LAST FOUR NUMBERS OF THE LICENSEE'S LICENSE NUMBER.
  §  4.  This  act shall take effect on the thirtieth day after it shall
 have become a law.  Effective  immediately,  the  addition,  amendment
 and/or repeal of any rule or regulation necessary for the implementation
 of  this  act  on  its  effective  date  are  authorized  to be made and
 completed on or before such effective date.
