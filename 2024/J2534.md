## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2534
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  CONGRATULATING William Salzillo, Professor of Fine
  Arts,  upon  the occasion of his retirement after 51
  years of distinguished service to  Hamilton  College
  in Clinton, New York
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the custom of this Legislative Body to acknowledge
the significant milestones in  the  distinguished  careers  of  esteemed
residents of this noble Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
congratulate William Salzillo, Professor of Fine Arts, upon the occasion
of  his  retirement  after 51 years of distinguished service to Hamilton
College in Clinton, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, William Salzillo, of Utica, New York,  obtained  an  Artium
Baccalaureus  degree  from  Middlebury  College, a Bachelor of Fine Arts
degree from Rhode Island School of Design, and a  Master  of  Fine  Arts
degree from Cranbrook Academy of Art; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Painter  William  Salzillo  began  his  teaching career at
Kirkland College in 1973; in 1978, the accomplished  artist  joined  the
faculty  of  Hamilton  College when both Clinton, New York, institutions
merged; and
 
  WHEREAS, During his 51 years  in  education,  William  Salzillo  has
instructed countless students in drawing, design, and painting; and
 
  WHEREAS,  From  1982-1992,  William  Salzillo served as the founding
Director of the  Emerson  Gallery  at  Hamilton  College;  the  National
Academy  of  Design  and  the 14 Sculptors Gallery in New York City, the
Everson Museum in Syracuse, and the Munson-Williams-Proctor Institute in
Utica have exhibited  his  work;  his  most  recent  works  explore  the
landscapes of California, the American Southwest, and Italy; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body that, when
individuals of such noble aims and accomplishments are  brought  to  our
attention,  they should be celebrated and recognized by all the citizens
of the great State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Rare, indeed, is the occurrence  of  such  a  compassionate
blending  of  strength  and  commitment  as that demonstrated by William
Salzillo over a lifetime of sacrifice and  dedication  to  others;  now,
therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate William Salzillo, Professor of Fine Arts, upon the occasion
of his retirement after 51 years of distinguished  service  to  Hamilton
College  in  Clinton,  New  York, and to wish him well in all his future
endeavors; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to William Salzillo.
