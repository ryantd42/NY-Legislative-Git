## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1905
 
BY: Senator WALCZYK
 
  CONGRATULATING  Eleanor  Cunningham  upon  the
  occasion of celebrating her 100th Birthday
 
  WHEREAS, Senior citizens bring a wealth of experience and  knowledge
to  the  increasingly  active  roles they play in today's society; their
past contributions and future participation are a  vital  part  of,  and
valuable asset to, the fabric of community life and activity; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the custom of this Legislative Body to commemorate
and recognize certain milestones celebrated by citizens  of  this  great
Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
congratulate  Eleanor Cunningham upon the occasion of her 100th Birthday
on Monday, March 4, 2024; and
 
  WHEREAS, Eleanor Randall was born to George and  Delora  Randall  in
Ogdensburg, New York, on March 4, 1924; and
 
  WHEREAS, Spending her childhood during the Great Depression, Eleanor
Randall enjoyed spending time on the nearby St. Lawrence River, fishing,
swimming,  and frogging with her father; as a teenager, she indulged her
passion for music  and  learned  to  play  the  ukulele;  prior  to  her
marriage, she was employed by Cleveland Container in Ogdensburg; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  1948,  Eleanor  Randall married Daniel Cunningham; the
couple were blessed with four daughters, Karen,  Patricia,  Pamela,  and
Darcy;  from  this  union,  the  family  extended  to  include  nine
grandchildren and 15 great-grandchildren; and
 
  WHEREAS, After her husband's death in 1978, Eleanor  Cunningham  was
employed as a library clerk at the St. Lawrence Psychiatric Center where
she  later  retired;  throughout that decade, she was a member of the CB
Radio Club; and
 
  WHEREAS, Eleanor  Cunningham's  hobbies  have  been  playing  bingo,
collecting  antiques,  and using modern technology; surfing the internet
and spending time on Facebook are two pastimes she  has  truly  enjoyed;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  As  an  active  constituent  in  her  community,  Eleanor
Cunningham has been a member of Notre Dame Church; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Additionally,  Eleanor  Cunningham  has  been  physically
active,  participating  in  tai chi into her 70s and playing tennis with
her youngest grandchild in her 80s; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout her century-long life,  Eleanor  Cunningham  has
inspired and enhanced the lives of her beloved family and friends; and
 
  WHEREAS, This exceptional centenarian has experienced the incredible
joys  and  sorrows  characteristic  of  and  reserved for those with the
stamina and courage to savor a full life; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Eleanor  Cunningham has enriched the lives of those around
her through her joyous and sincere  love  for  others  and  through  the
vibrant  charm  and  infinite  wisdom which come only from a fullness of
years; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is the intent  of  this  Legislative  Body  to  publicly
recognize  those  who  have  reached  such a remarkable age and who have
witnessed and celebrated the  innovations,  cultural  developments,  and
outstanding  achievements  of this Nation during the last century, while
themselves contributing to the  growth  and  excellence  of  this  great
Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate Eleanor Cunningham upon the  occasion  of  celebrating  her
100th Birthday; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Eleanor Cunningham.
