**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Joseph A. Griffo
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J1510 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J1510)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1510
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  COMMENDING the Honorable Anthony J. Garramone upon
  the  occasion  of his induction into the 2023 Oneida
  County History Center's  Richard  W.  Couper  Living
  Legends Hall of Fame
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body that the quality
and character of life in this great Empire State is  indelibly  enriched
by  the  faithful  and  untiring  efforts  of those individuals who have
helped shape the history of their area; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
commend the Honorable Anthony J. Garramone  upon  the  occasion  of  his
induction into the 2023 Oneida County History Center's Richard W. Couper
Living Legends Hall of Fame, to be honored at the annual Historical Hall
of  Fame  and  Living  Legends  Awards Celebration at the Irish Cultural
Center in Utica, New York on Thursday, October 19, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS, Founded  in  1946,  the  Historical  Hall  of  Fame  honors
individuals from Oneida County whom have made an impact on the community
and  the  world;  it's  goal  is to ensure that noteworthy people in the
Oneida community and their achievements are recorded in  local  history;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  respected judge with a long and distinguished career in
public service, the Honorable Anthony J.  Garramone  is  a  graduate  of
Thomas R.  Proctor High School and Utica College; he honorably served in
the  United  States  Navy  and  earned  a  Juris Doctorate from Syracuse
University College of Law; and
 
  WHEREAS, Prior to his judicial  career,  the  Honorable  Anthony  J.
Garramone  served  as counsel to the City of Utica Board of Education on
the Utica Common Council and devotedly on the City of Utica Water  Board
as well; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Elected  to  the  first  of three terms as a City of Utica
Judge in 1981, the Honorable Anthony J. Garramone rose to Chief Judge in
1994; and
 
  WHEREAS, Retiring with esteem in  2003,  the  Honorable  Anthony  J.
Garramone  is a past presiding member of the Judicial Section of the New
York State Bar Association and the past President of the New York  State
Association of City Court Judges; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Committed  to  supporting education and the community, the
Honorable Anthony J. Garramone taught at Utica College  from  the  years
1965  to  2007 and served on the boards of many community organizations,
including the Utica Children's Museum, Community  Arts  Council,  Munson
Williams  Proctor  Arts  Institute, Mohawk Valley Community College, and
the Mohawk Valley Community College Foundation; today he serves  as  the
City of Utica Common Council's attorney; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  a  tireless and unwavering commitment to Central New
York and beyond, the Honorable Anthony J. Garramone devoted  his  entire
life  to  making  communities  a  better place for its citizens to live,
work, and play; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  extraordinary  man  has  an immensely beautiful life
built on compassion, kindness, and respect for others; he is one of  the
most  significant and enduring civic and political leaders in the County
of Oneida, and his insight and strength will forever serve the countless
people he has touched; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
commend  the  Honorable  Anthony  J.  Garramone upon the occasion of his
induction into the 2023 Oneida County History Center's Richard W. Couper
Living Legends Hall of Fame; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the Honorable Anthony J. Garramone.
