## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1964
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
  HONORING  Adele  Balsamo  upon the occasion of her
  designation for special  recognition  by  the  Order
  Sons  & Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini Lodge
  No. 2206, on March 22, 2024
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to  recognize  and
pay  tribute  to  those  organizations  of  high  purpose  and  worthy
accomplishment whose endeavors have faithfully served the welfare of the
citizens of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Adele  Balsamo  upon  the  occasion  of  her  designation  for  special
recognition by the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in  America,  Cellini
Lodge  No.  2206,  on  Friday,  March 22, 2024, at the Westbury Manor in
Westbury, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, This year's theme is "La Festa 2024...  Celebrating Cellini
56! A Salute to Adele Balsamo, Kathryn  Grande,  and  Anthony  Ventiera,
Cellini  Lodge No. 2206 Past Deputies, OSDIA! And Special Recognition of
Cellini Lodge Veteran Members"; and
 
  WHEREAS, The mission statement of the  Order  Sons  &  Daughters  of
Italy  in  America  is  "We  endeavor  to  keep  alive Italian culture &
heritage by inspiring members to participate in  the  Italian-  American
experience by sharing traditions and stories through lodge and community
events; and to be charitable in both educational and humanitarian causes
for and beyond our members and community"; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Adele  Balsamo has been selected as an esteemed honoree of
this  year's  Scholarship/Charitable  Benefit  Dinner  Dance  for  her
dedicated  and valuable service as Past Lodge Deputy of the Order Sons &
Daughters of Italy in America, Aida Lodge No. 2163A; and
 
  WHEREAS, Adele Balsamo was born  on  October  10,  1939  in  Corona,
Queens  to  Yolanda  and Joseph Motola; raised in New York City with her
younger sister, JoAnn, her family lineage is from  Naples  and  Abruzzi,
Italy; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Working throughout high school to support her family after
her father's tragic passing, Adele Balsamo attended Flushing High School
and worked part-time during the summers  at  the  same  factory  as  her
mother;  upon  her  graduation,  she  began a career as a bookkeeper for
Stardust Lingerie on 31st Street and Madison Avenue where she worked for
eight years, while also working part-time at a restaurant as a  hostess;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Furthering  her  career,  Adele  Balsamo  worked for Smith
Corona Typewriters on Fifth Avenue and also  worked  for  Oppenbeim  and
Collins  Department  Store in the jewelry department; for two years, she
worked in Queens for Queensboro Toyota in Long Island City, then went on
to work for Stanley Kilburn  Toyota  in  Bayside  for  six  years  as  a
bookkeeper,  later  she worked for Astro Nissan in Flushing for 11 years
 
as an office manager and for the Helms Brothers, a division of  Mercedes
Benz, for four years; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Dedicated  to her community, Adele Balsamo volunteered for
the North Shore Cerebral Palsy Foundation  and  was  involved  in  their
fundraising  committee  for  three  years; in addition, she was involved
with Italian Charities and became a  member  after  two  years,  getting
involved with the entertainment committee; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Moving to Seaford, New York, Adele Balsamo joined the OSIA
Lodge, Aida Lodge No. 2163A in 1996; as a testament  to  her  leadership
capabilities, she became president from 1997 to 1999; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Devoted  to the mission of the Lodge, Adele Balsamo helped
to organize fundraisers such as fashion shows, dinner dances,  celebrity
functions,  a  football  wedding,  military  bridges,  Chinese Auctions,
Atlantic City trips, and many other functions to raise  funds  for  such
charities  as  Cooley's  Anemia,  Gift  of  Sight, CSJ, Garibaldi-Meucci
Museum, St. Francis Heart Fund, Children's  Inn,  Birthright,  and  many
other charitable organizations; and
 
  WHEREAS, Serving a two-year term as State Trustee, Adele Balsamo was
Deputy  to Cellini Lodge and Enrico Fermi Lodge throughout her term; she
also served on Arbitration for two terms and as District 2 Treasurer for
eight years at the Kings and Queens Pageant; and
 
  WHEREAS, With her throughout has been her husband, Vincent  Balsamo,
her two children, Hayden and Elle, her sister JoAnn, and her niece Lisa,
all  of whom feel privileged to be a part of her life and rejoice in her
achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS, A testament to their dedicated service to others, the Order
Sons & Daughters of  Italy  in  America  maintain  a  highly  successful
scholarship  and  charity  program,  raising  funds  annually to provide
monetary awards to the numerous  beneficiaries  of  the  New  Hyde  Park
community; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is proudly moved to honor and commend
the  members of the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in America, past and
present, who have labored so conscientiously to secure and  improve  the
quality of life for the community it serves; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Adele Balsamo upon the occasion of  her  designation  for  special
recognition  by  the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini
Lodge No. 2206, on March 22, 2024; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Adele Balsamo.
