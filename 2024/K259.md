## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 259
 
BY: M. of A. Levenberg
 
  MOURNING the death of Cheryl Timmons distinguished
  citizen and devoted member of her community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
citizens  of  the  State  of  New York whose lifework and civic endeavor
served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and the great
State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Cheryl Timmons of Nelsonville, New York, died  on  February
21, 2023, at the age of 70; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Cheryl Timmons distinguished herself in her profession and
by her sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of
her community; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born on October 13, 1952, in Ossining,  New  York,  to  the
late  Berline  and  Grace DiRocco, Cheryl Timmons attended Ossining High
School; and
 
  WHEREAS, Devoted to her career, Cheryl Timmons worked for  the  Town
of Ossining's Office of the Assessor for over 35 years; and
 
  WHEREAS, She was known for always going above and beyond to help the
senior  citizens,  veterans, volunteer firefighters and individuals with
disabilities as they tried to navigate through the process  of  securing
their tax exemptions; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Cheryl  Timmons  was  a devoted parishioner at Our Lady of
Loretto Church located in Cold Spring, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Cheryl Timmons' commitment to excellence, and her spirit of
humanity,  carried  over  into  all  fields  of  enterprise,  including
charitable and civic endeavors; and
 
  WHEREAS, With a great love for animals, Cheryl Timmons joyfully took
care of and fed her neighborhood cats; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Cheryl  Timmons  is  survived by her devoted husband of 51
years, James Timmons; loving children,  James,  and  William;  cherished
grandchildren,  James,  Theresa,  William,  Dylan  and Amanda, Allen and
Johnny, and her dear sister, Laurel; and
 
  WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a  sense  of
compassion, Cheryl Timmons leaves behind a legacy which will long endure
the  passage  of  time and will remain as a comforting memory to all she
served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
mourn  the  death  of  Cheryl  Timmons distinguished citizen and devoted
member of her community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Cheryl Timmons.
