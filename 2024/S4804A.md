# Directs the department of environmental conservation to establish a Long Island deer management pilot program

**Bill ID:** S4804A
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Anthony H. Palumbo
**Status:** Signed by Governor
**PDF:** [S4804A PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S4804A)

## Summary

Directs the department of environmental conservation to establish a Long Island deer management pilot program in the town of Southold.

 AN ACT directing the department of environmental conservation to  estab-
  lish a deer management pilot program in the town of Southold

  THE  PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND ASSEM-
 BLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
 
  Section 1. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, rule, or  regu-
 lation  to the contrary, the department of environmental conservation is
 hereby authorized and directed to  establish  a  deer  management  pilot
 program  in the town of Southold. Such program may, at the discretion of
 the department of environmental conservation:
  a. set and modify deer hunting seasons and regulate  methods  of  take
 and bag limits in the town of Southold;
  b.  allow  use  of firearms to take deer during the regular season and
 eliminate acreage minimums and special  season  constraints  within  the
 town of Southold;
  c.  regulate crossbow use within the town of Southold, including when,
 where, and who is eligible to use crossbows for the taking of deer;
  d. set conditions under which hunters can receive some form of compen-
 sation for killing deer within the town of Southold;
  e. allow 12- and 13-year-old hunters to hunt deer with the supervision
 of a licensed, experienced adult within the town of Southold;
  f. allow 12- and 13-year-old hunters to hunt  using  firearms  from  a
 tree  stand  within  the  town  of Southold, provided it is a tree stand
 designed to accommodate two or more persons and  provided  further  that
 the supervising, experienced hunter is in the tree stand with the 12- or
 13-year-old;
  g.  allow the department of environmental conservation to establish or
 extend, as needed, a special January firearm season within the  town  of
 Southold;
