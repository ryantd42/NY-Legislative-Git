**Sponsor:** Kevin S. Parker
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [J455 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J455)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 455
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  HONORING  Angelique Agudo upon the occasion of her
  designation as recipient of the PASEsetter Award  by
  the  Partnership  for  After  School  Education  on
  February 28, 2023
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize that
the quality and character of life in the  communities  across  New  York
State  are  reflective  of  the concerned and dedicated efforts of those
individuals who would devote themselves to the welfare of the  community
and its citizenry; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Angelique Agudo upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the
PASEsetter Award by the Partnership for After School  Education  at  its
30th  Anniversary  PASEsetter  Awards  Benefit  on Tuesday, February 28,
2023, at Cipriani, New York, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Angelique Agudo is receiving this prestigious award for her
role as an afterschool educator whose commitment, energy and  creativity
has  had an indelible impact on the children and youth of New York City;
and
 
  WHEREAS, PASE is a  child-focused  organization  that  promotes  and
supports  quality afterschool programs, particularly those serving young
people from underserved communities; Angelique Agudo  truly  exemplifies
this vital organization's mission of delivering consistent, high-quality
programming  to  youth and increases their capacity to meet the needs of
their communities; and
 
  WHEREAS, At the young age of eight, Angelique Agudo  started  taking
tap  classes with Marguerite D'Aloia at Groove With Me, and thus began a
lifelong love of dance, and a lifelong connection to  the  organization;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  After  graduating from high school, Angelique Agudo earned
her Bachelor of Arts degree in  both  Sociology  and  Anthropology  from
Saint  Michael's College in Vermont; she became an academic counselor at
Upward Bound and  then  returned  to  her  alma  mater  to  serve  as  a
residential  director;  after this position, she worked as a residential
manager at Boys Hope Girls Hope; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2018, Angelique Agudo returned to her  roots  and  began
her  career  with Groove With Me as the program director; throughout her
tenure,  she  has  served  as  an  enthusiastic  and  kind  leader  who
steadfastly supports her students, alumni, staff, and coworkers; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Remembering her days as a young student at Groove With Me,
Angelique Agudo understands and empathizes  with  her  students'  hopes,
wishes  and concerns and truly endeavors to make the dance studio a home
for all; and
 
  WHEREAS, Angelique Agudo has tirelessly worked to expand Groove With
Me's programming and partnerships, and has  encouraged  increased  staff
diversity,  and  realizes  the  importance  of  an on-site mental health
counselor; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Last year, Angelique Agudo engaged DanceWave to design and
facilitate teacher trainings for the staff, and spearheaded Groove  With
Me's  first  summer  day  camp and expanded the camp to include not just
dance  classes,  but  also  engagement  with  a  variety  of  arts
extracurriculars; and
 
  WHEREAS,  During  the  COVID-19 pandemic, Angelique Agudo diligently
strived to support and stay in  contact  with  her  students  and  their
families,  continuing  to  connect  them  with jobs and internships, and
helping them with the college application process; and
 
  WHEREAS, Angelique Agudo's steadfast and unremitting commitment  and
guidance  has  been  a driving force in the lives of so many young women
and their families; currently, she is working towards a master's  degree
in  clinical psychology in order to further expand her abilities to mold
and shape the youth of her community; and
 
  WHEREAS, It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that  when
individuals  of  such  noble aims and accomplishments are brought to our
attention, they should be celebrated and recognized by all the  citizens
of the great State of New York; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Angelique Agudo upon the occasion of her designation as  recipient
of  the  PASEsetter Award by the Partnership for After School Education;
and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Angelique Agudo.
