**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Kevin S. Parker
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J585 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J585)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 585
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  COMMENDING  Margaret  E. McGruder-Collins upon the
  occasion of her designation for special  recognition
  by  the  Most  Worshipful Prince Hall Grand Lodge of
  New York at its Celebration of Women Luncheon
 
  WHEREAS, Individuals who give of their time and energies  and  serve
the best interests of their communities are an asset beyond remuneration
and cannot be sufficiently extolled; and
 
  WHEREAS, Margaret E. McGruder-Collins has given not only of her time
and energies but also of her competence, intelligence and leadership and
consequently has been designated for special honor; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative Body is justly proud to commend Margaret
E.  McGruder-Collins upon the occasion of her  designation  for  special
recognition  by  the Most Worshipful Prince Hall Grand Lodge of New York
to be recognized at its Celebration of Women Luncheon on Saturday, March
25, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS, Each year, New York State officially  sets  aside  time  to
recognize  the unique contributions that New York women have made to New
York State and beyond; and
 
  WHEREAS, Margaret  E.  McGruder-Collins  has  been  called  upon  to
contribute  her  time  and  talents  to  countless  civic and charitable
endeavors and has always given of herself unstintingly; and
 
  WHEREAS, A truly multifaceted individual and a qualified nurse  from
Suffolk  Community College, Margaret E. McGruder-Collins went on to earn
a Pre-Law and Paralegal Studies degree from  Liberty  University  and  a
Business Management and Accounting degree from Howard University; and
 
  WHEREAS, Margaret E. McGruder-Collins' illustrious career as a nurse
has  traversed  more  than 18 years; today, she is a traveling nurse who
selflessly attends to her patients in a variety of  settings,  including
correctional  facilities,  in-home  hospice  and  palliative  care,
residential treatment, and rehabilitation centers; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Finding  time  for  all  her  passions,  Margaret  E.
McGruder-Collins  is  also  a  travel  agent and legal Nurse Consultant,
appearing in court as an expert witness, as well as the  proud  business
owner  of  Mobile  Notary  &  Signing  Company, and a Trucking Logistics
Company; and
 
  WHEREAS, Prior to her Mobile  Notary  and  Signing  Agent  Business,
Margaret  E.  McGruder-Collins  spent  20  years  as a notary public and
insurance underwriter for AIG Insurance Company; and
 
  WHEREAS, After being diagnosed with  cancer  in  2014,  Margaret  E.
McGruder-Collins created PinkFire collective, where she works to improve
the  lives and experiences of cancer patients by adhering to the premise
of treating the "whole" person; and
 
  WHEREAS, Under her able leadership, the PinkFire collective provides
individuals with cancer with rides to treatment appointments as well  as
support  prior  to  surgery, 24/7 phone counseling, school supplies, and
gifts for the holidays for children with parents affected by the  tragic
condition; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Furthermore,  the  initiative  works  with the Long Island
Branch of the  Fire  Fighters  Cancer  Support  Network  to  spread  the
importance  of  fire  personnel  screening,  prevention,  and  treatment
efforts; and
 
  WHEREAS, Dedicated to helping others, Margaret  E.  McGruder-Collins
is  a  member of Deborah Chapter No. 31 Eureka Grand Chapter Prince Hall
Order of Eastern Star, Inc., Prince Hall Medical Foundation,  Al  Maseer
Court No. 226 Oasis of Amityville, Desert of New York, AEAONMS, American
Nursing  Association,  National  League  of  Nurses, NAACP, Fire Fighter
Cancer  Support  Network,  Pink  Shoes,  Inc.,  and  LI  Breast  Cancer
Coalition; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rare  indeed  is  the  impressive  dedication  shown by an
individual for the benefit of others which Margaret E.  McGruder-Collins
has displayed throughout her life; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Margaret E. McGruder-Collins upon the occasion of her  designation
for  special  recognition by the Most Worshipful Prince Hall Grand Lodge
of New York at its Celebration of Women Luncheon; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Margaret E. McGruder-Collins.
