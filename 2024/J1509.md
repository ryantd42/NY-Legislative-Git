**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Joseph A. Griffo
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J1509 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J1509)

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1509
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  COMMENDING  Evon  Ervin  upon  the occasion of her
  induction  into  the  2023  Oneida  County  History
  Center's  Richard  W.  Couper Living Legends Hall of
  Fame
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that  the  quality
and  character  of life in this great Empire State is indelibly enriched
by the faithful and untiring  efforts  of  those  individuals  who  have
helped shape the history of their area; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
commend  Evon  Ervin  upon  the  occasion of her induction into the 2023
Oneida County History Center's Richard W. Couper Living Legends Hall  of
Fame,  to  be  honored  at the annual Historical Hall of Fame and Living
Legends Awards Celebration at the Irish Cultural Center  in  Utica,  New
York on Thursday, October 19, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Founded  in  1946,  the  Historical  Hall  of  Fame honors
individuals from Oneida County who have made an impact on the  community
and  the  world;  its  goal  is  to ensure that noteworthy people in the
Oneida community and their achievements are recorded in  local  history;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Born and raised in Utica, New York, Evon Ervin continuously
advocates for change and unity through her service and leadership in the
community;  she  graduated from Utica College of Syracuse University and
earned a Bachelor of Science degree in psychology, specializing in child
life and development; and
 
  WHEREAS, Having served people living in poverty in  the  Oneida  and
Herkimer  Counties,  Evon  Ervin recently retired as Deputy Director for
Mohawk  Valley  Community  Action  Agency  Inc.;  she  is  a  licensed
Evangelist,  President  of  St.  Matthew's  Temple Missionary Board, and
Certified Community Action Professional; and
 
  WHEREAS, Elected as Oneida County's  first  female  African-American
legislator  in  2020,  Evon  Ervin  continues to represent Oneida County
Legislator District No. 20 and serves on the  Health  &  Human  Service,
Economic Development, and Public Safety committees; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Devoted  to helping others, Evon Ervin has shared her time
and  expertise  with  countless  community  organizations,  such  as
Communities  That  Care,  NeighborWorks HomeOwnership Center, Utica Safe
Schools, Utica City School District Board  of  Education,  Utica  School
District  Policy  Committee,  and BOCES School Board Institute Executive
Committee; Evon Ervin is currently involved with The  ARC,  Oneida-Lewis
Chapter,  Oneida  County  NAACP, Black Leadership Coalition, MVCAA Board
Member, Utica Public Library, and MVCC Alumni Committee; and
 
  WHEREAS, With her throughout has been her beloved spouse for over 43
years, Venice Ervin, who has been privileged to be a part  of  her  life
and rejoices in her many achievements; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  With  a  tireless and unwavering commitment to Central New
York  and  beyond,  Evon  Ervin  devoted  her  entire  life  to  making
communities a better place for its citizens to live, work, and play; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  extraordinary  woman has an immensely beautiful life
built on compassion, kindness, and respect for others; she is one of the
most significant and enduring civic and political leaders in the  county
of Oneida, and her insight and strength will forever serve the countless
people she has touched; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend Evon Ervin upon the occasion of  her  induction  into  the  2023
Oneida  County History Center's Richard W. Couper Living Legends Hall of
Fame; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Evon Ervin.
