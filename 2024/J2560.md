## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2560
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  MOURNING  the  death  of  Albertina  Yvonne,
  distinguished citizen  and  devoted  member  of  her
  community
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  with  great  sorrow  and  deep  regret  that  this
Legislative Body records the passing of  Albertina  Yvonne,  noting  the
significance of her purposeful life and accomplishments; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Albertina  Yvonne  distinguished herself in her profession
and by her  sincere  dedication  and  substantial  contribution  to  the
welfare of her community; and
 
  WHEREAS, Albertina Yvonne's commitment to excellence, and her spirit
of  humanity,  carried  over  into  all  fields of enterprise, including
charitable and civic endeavors; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born May 13, 1935, in Saddle Hill, Maraval, Trinidad,  West
Indies  to  parents  Charles  and Madeline Felix, Albertina Yvonne later
traveled to the United States with the help of her daughter, Verna; and
 
  WHEREAS, Albertina  Yvonne  is  survived  by  her  children,  Verna,
Everard,  Brian,  Sharon,  Elizabeth,  Kathy, and Shurekka; her sisters,
Elaine and Rosie; 18 grandchildren;  17  great-grandchildren;  and  many
nieces, nephews, relatives, and friends; and
 
  WHEREAS,  She  is predeceased by her beloved husband, Byron Ettienne
and her son, Rammy; and
 
  WHEREAS, Albertina Yvonne's passions in life were spending time with
her cherished family, dancing, traveling,  and  listening  to  soca  and
calypso  music;  she  could  often  be  found  enjoying the songs of her
favorite artist, The Mighty Sparrow; and
 
  WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit,  imbued  with  a  sense  of
compassion,  and  comforted  by a loving family, Albertina Yvonne leaves
behind a legacy which will long endure the  passage  of  time  and  will
remain  as  a  comforting  memory to all she served and befriended; now,
therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
mourn  the  death of Albertina Yvonne, distinguished citizen and devoted
member of her community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Albertina Yvonne.
