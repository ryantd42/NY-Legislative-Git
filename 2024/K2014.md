# Congratulating Francis L. Crumb upon the occasion of being honored for more than 30 years of distinguished public service to the Village of Holland Patent, New York

**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Marianne Buttenschon
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [K2014 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/K2014)

## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 2014
 
BY: M. of A. Buttenschon
 
  CONGRATULATING  Francis L. Crumb upon the occasion
  of  being  honored  for  more  than  30  years  of
  distinguished  public  service  to  the  Village  of
  Holland Patent, New York
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  seeks  to  acknowledge  those
individuals  whose  professional  lives  and  civic  endeavors  serve to
enhance the stature of the State of New York and takes  great  pride  in
recognizing  and  applauding  those  individuals  who have distinguished
themselves through their service to their communities; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
congratulate Francis L. Crumb upon the occasion  of  being  honored  for
more than 30 years of distinguished public service to be celebrated at a
special  Ceremony  in  Holland  Patent,  New York, on Tuesday, April 23,
2024; and
 
  WHEREAS, After graduation from the University of Notre Dame in 1967,
Francis L. Crumb served his country in the United States  Army,  working
at the Pentagon, from 1967-1969; and
 
  WHEREAS,  From  1969-1984, Francis L. Crumb worked as a reporter and
photographer  for  the  Utica  Observer-Dispatch  newspaper;  for
approximately  13 years, he worked in the Army Reserve's medical unit in
Utica, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, From 1984-2007, Francis L. Crumb was employed as the  Chief
of  Public  Affairs  for  the  Rome Laboratory (RADC) prior to retiring;
however, he continues serving his community; and
 
  WHEREAS, For more than 30 years, Francis L. Crumb rendered faithful,
conscientious, and valuable service to the residents of Holland  Patent;
during  his tenure, he has served as a Village Trustee as well as Public
Safety Commissioner; and
 
  WHEREAS, For the residents of Holland Patent, Francis L.  Crumb  was
instrumental  in the provision of a public sewer system and natural gas;
additionally, in conjunction with the Mohawk Valley Water Authority,  he
played a part in the upgrade of Holland Patent's clean water system; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  his  capacity  as  a  public servant, Francis L. Crumb
served with loyalty, honor, and distinction; and
 
  WHEREAS, In his official acts, Francis L. Crumb was  governed  by  a
keen  sense  of duty and always showed a unique grasp of human problems,
providing essential services to members of his community; and
 
  WHEREAS, During his tenure with Holland  Patent,  Francis  L.  Crumb
earned  the  admiration,  esteem,  and affection of his colleagues; now,
therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
congratulate  Francis  L.  Crumb  upon the occasion of being honored for
more than 30 years of distinguished public service  to  the  Village  of
Holland Patent, New York; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Francis L. Crumb.
