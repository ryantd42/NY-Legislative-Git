## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1969
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
  HONORING  Kathryn  Grande upon the occasion of her
  designation for special  recognition  by  the  Order
  Sons  & Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini Lodge
  No. 2206, on March 22, 2024
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to  recognize  and
pay  tribute  to  those  organizations  of  high  purpose  and  worthy
accomplishment whose endeavors have faithfully served the welfare of the
citizens of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Kathryn  Grande  upon  the  occasion  of  her  designation  for  special
recognition by the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in  America,  Cellini
Lodge  No.  2206,  on  Friday,  March 22, 2024, at the Westbury Manor in
Westbury, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, This year's theme is "La Festa 2024Ã Celebrating Cellini 56!
A Salute to Adele Balsamo, Kathryn Grande, and Anthony Ventiera, Cellini
Lodge No. 2206 Past Deputies, OSDA! And Special Recognition  of  Cellini
Lodge Veteran Members"; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  mission  statement  of  the Order Sons & Daughters of
Italy in America is  "We  endeavor  to  keep  alive  Italian  culture  &
heritage  by  inspiring  members to participate in the Italian- American
experience by sharing traditions and stories through lodge and community
events; and to be charitable in both educational and humanitarian causes
for and beyond our members and community"; and
 
  WHEREAS, Kathryn Grande has been selected as an esteemed honoree  of
this  year's  Scholarship/Charitable  Benefit  Dinner  Dance  for  her
dedicated and valuable service as Past Lodge Deputy of the Order Sons  &
Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini Lodge No. 2206; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Active  in  her  community, Kathryn Grande was involved in
many  organizations  and  committees,  including:  the  Organization  of
Parents  and  Teachers;  North  Shore Schools Board of Education; Loggia
Glen Cove No.  10161, where she worked  her  way  up  to  the  role  of
president-  the  fourth  woman  to  serve  such  role,  and  chaired the
Scholarship Committee, (where she conducted an  annual  Scholarship  and
Awards  Night)  Membership  Committee,  Annual  Dinner Dance and Journal
Committee, Nominating Committee, Awards Dinner Dance Committee, Heritage
and Culture Committee Budget Committee and the Memorial  Day  Committee;
while  she  was  president,  she  initiated  the  first St. Joseph's Day
celebration and the money raised was given to St. Rocco's Church to help
the less fortunate; and
 
  WHEREAS, Kathryn  Grande  had  several  guest  speakers  on  Italian
Culture  at  the  membership meetings, set up the first Italian Heritage
and Culture displays  at  the  Glen  Cove  Public  Library  and  brought
prolific speakers to the library during the month of October; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Kathryn  Grande  was  chairperson  for  the  District  II
Presidents' Council; she was Lodge Deputy to  the  John  Michael  Marino
Lodge  No.  1389 for four years, then was appointed to the Cellini Lodge
No. 2206 for four years, and again to the John Michael Marino Lodge  No.
1389 for another four years; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Kathryn  Grande sponsored two State Scholarships in memory
of her parents, Rose and Frank Della Ratta; she also sponsors the  Dante
Medal for Excellence in Italian; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Kathryn  Grande  is  active  in the New York State Retired
Teachers' Association; she has been on the Legislative Action  Committee
representing  North  Nassau  Retired  Teachers'  Association,  West  End
Retired Teachers' Association, and was on the  State-level  representing
the Long Island Zone; she has served four years as President of the Long
Island Zone and has been a delegate to the Animal Convention since 2002;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Kathryn  Grande  continued  to  enjoy  working  on all the
activities and  events  she  started  in  2000,  when  she  returned  as
President  in  2018;  Loggia  Glen Cove has been an integral part of her
life for the past 48 years; and
 
  WHEREAS, With her throughout has been  her  husband,  Angelo;  their
children,  Ignatius  and  Angela Rose; her brother, John; and nieces and
nephews, John, Regina, and Andrew, all of whom feel privileged to  be  a
part of his life and rejoice in his achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS, A testament to their dedicated service to others, the Order
Sons  &  Daughters  of  Italy  in  America  maintain a highly successful
scholarship and charity  program,  raising  funds  annually  to  provide
monetary  awards  to  the  numerous  beneficiaries  of the New Hyde Park
community; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is proudly moved to honor and commend
the members of the Order Sons of Italy in America, past and present, who
have labored so conscientiously to secure and  improve  the  quality  of
life for the community it serves; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Kathryn Grande upon the occasion of her  designation  for  special
recognition  by  the Order Sons & Daughters of Italy in America, Cellini
Lodge No. 2206, on March 22, 2024; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Kathryn Grande.
