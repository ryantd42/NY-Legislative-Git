# Relates to the withholding of salaries of elected officials who have been indicted on charges of any crimes related to their public office

**Sponsor:** Kenneth Zebrowski
**Status:** Stricken
**PDF:** [A5621 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/A5621)

## Summary

Relates to the withholding of salaries of elected officials who have been indicted on charges of any crimes related to their public office.

  § 112. DEFINITIONS. AS USED IN THIS ARTICLE:
  1. "ELECTED OFFICIALS" MEANS EVERY OFFICER WHO IS ELECTED BY  ELECTORS

  B.  A FELONY COMMITTED IN DIRECT CONNECTION WITH SERVICE AS AN ELECTED
 OFFICIAL; OR

 ADVANTAGE  FOR  HIMSELF OR HERSELF OR FOR SOME OTHER PERSON, THROUGH THE
 USE OR ATTEMPTED USE OF THE POWER, RIGHTS, PRIVILEGES OR DUTIES  OF  HIS
 OR HER POSITION AS AN ELECTED OFFICIAL.
  §  113.  TERMS  AND  CONDITIONS.  NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER LAW TO THE
 CONTRARY, IT SHALL BE A TERM AND CONDITION  OF  HOLDING  PUBLIC  ELECTED
 OFFICE  IN  THE  STATE  OF  NEW  YORK THAT ALL ELECTED OFFICIALS' SALARY
 PAYMENTS ARE SUBJECT TO THE PROVISIONS OF THIS ARTICLE.
  § 114. WITHHOLDING OF SALARY PAYMENTS. 1. A. UPON NOTIFICATION  OF  AN
 INDICTMENT PURSUANT TO SECTION 190.95 OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE LAW, THE
 PUBLIC EMPLOYER OF AN ELECTED OFFICIAL, WHO HAS BEEN INDICTED ON CHARGES
 OF  ANY  CRIMES  RELATED  TO  THEIR  PUBLIC  OFFICE, SHALL WITHHOLD SUCH
 ELECTED OFFICIAL'S SALARY  PAYMENTS.  IF  SUCH  ELECTED  OFFICIAL  SEEKS
 ANOTHER  PUBLIC OFFICE OR ANOTHER PUBLIC EMPLOYMENT POSITION WHILE UNDER
 INDICTMENT, IT SHALL BE THE BURDEN OF THE  ELECTED  OFFICIAL  TO  NOTIFY
 THEIR  NEW PUBLIC EMPLOYER OF THEIR INDICTMENT. THIS SECTION SHALL APPLY
 FOR ALL SALARY PAYMENTS WHILE EMPLOYED AS AN ELECTED OFFICIAL,  OFFICIAL
 OR EMPLOYEE OF THE STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT.
  B.  A PUBLIC EMPLOYER SHALL NOTIFY THE ELECTED OFFICIAL OF SUCH SALARY
 PAYMENT WITHHOLDING AND PROVIDE THEM WITH A COPY OF NOTIFICATION OF  THE
 INDICTMENT PURSUANT TO SECTION 190.95 OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE LAW.
  2.  UPON  NOTIFICATION, THE PUBLIC EMPLOYER OR EMPLOYERS OF AN ELECTED
 OFFICIAL FACING CHARGES OF CRIMES RELATED TO THEIR  PUBLIC  OFFICE  THAT
 HAS  HAD SUCH CHARGES DISMISSED, ACQUITTED OR HAVE BEEN FOUND NOT GUILTY
 BY TRIAL JURY, SHALL PROMPTLY PAY THE WITHHELD SALARY  PAYMENTS  TO  THE
 ELECTED OFFICIAL WITHIN SIXTY DAYS.
  3.  UPON  A  PLEA  OF  GUILTY,  OR BY CONVICTION AFTER TRIAL TO CRIMES
 RELATED TO PUBLIC OFFICE OF AN ELECTED  OFFICIAL,  THE  WITHHELD  SALARY
 PAYMENTS SHALL NOT BE PAID AND RETURNED. SUCH WITHHELD PAYMENTS SHALL BE
 DEPOSITED INTO THE PUBLIC EMPLOYER'S GENERAL FUND.
  4.  A  PUBLIC  EMPLOYER  SHALL  PROVIDE  THE  ELECTED  OFFICIAL WITH A
 DETAILED ACCOUNTING  OF  THE  WITHHELD  SALARY  PAYMENTS  FOLLOWING  THE
 CONCLUSION OF AN INDICTMENT PURSUANT TO SUBDIVISION TWO OR THREE OF THIS
 SECTION.
  §  2.  The  criminal  procedure law is amended by adding a new section
 190.95 to read as follows:
 § 190.95 GRAND JURY; NOTIFICATION OF INDICTMENT IN CERTAIN CASES.
  UPON THE INDICTMENT OF AN ELECTED OFFICIAL ON CRIMES RELATED TO PUBLIC
 OFFICE, AS DEFINED IN SECTION ONE HUNDRED TWELVE OF THE PUBLIC  OFFICERS
 LAW,  THE  GRAND  JURY  SHALL DIRECT THE DISTRICT ATTORNEY TO NOTIFY THE
 ELECTED OFFICIAL'S PUBLIC  EMPLOYER  OF  THE  INDICTMENT.  THE  DISTRICT
 ATTORNEY  SHALL  NOTIFY  THE  PUBLIC  EMPLOYER NO LATER THAN THIRTY DAYS
 FOLLOWING THE INDICTMENT.
  § 3. The criminal procedure law is amended by  adding  a  new  section
 220.52 to read as follows:
 § 220.52 NOTICE OF ENTRY OF GUILTY PLEA INVOLVING AN ELECTED OFFICIAL.
  THE  COURT  SHALL NOTIFY THE PUBLIC EMPLOYER OR EMPLOYERS OF A DEFEND-
 ANT, WHO IS FACING CRIMES RELATED TO THEIR PUBLIC OFFICE AS  AN  ELECTED
 OFFICIAL AS DEFINED IN SECTION ONE HUNDRED TWELVE OF THE PUBLIC OFFICERS
 LAW,  THAT  HAS ENTERED A GUILTY PLEA TO A COUNT OR COUNTS OF AN INDICT-
 MENT OF A CRIME THAT WAS RELATED TO THEIR PUBLIC OFFICE. THE COURT SHALL
 NOTIFY SUCH DEFENDANT'S PUBLIC EMPLOYER OR EMPLOYERS OF THE ENTRY  OF  A
 GUILTY PLEA WITHIN THIRTY DAYS.
  §  4.  The  criminal  procedure law is amended by adding a new section
 310.90 to read as follows:
