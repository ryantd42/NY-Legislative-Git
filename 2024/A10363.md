# Preserves the ability to appeal a violation of a defendant's right to make a statement personally in his or her own behalf at sentencing

**Bill ID:** A10363
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Rodneyse Bichotte Hermelyn
**Status:** In Assembly Committee
**PDF:** [A10363 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/A10363)

## Summary

Preserves the ability to appeal a violation of a defendant's right to make a statement personally in his or her own behalf at sentencing notwithstanding a defendant signed an otherwise valid waiver of appeal.

  THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND  ASSEM-

  Section  1.  Subdivision 1 of section 380.50 of the criminal procedure
 law, as amended by chapter 307 of the laws of 1992, is amended  to  read
 as follows:
  1.  At  the  time  of  pronouncing sentence, the court must accord the

 relevant to the question of sentence. The court must then accord counsel
 for the defendant an opportunity to speak on behalf  of  the  defendant.

 or her own behalf, and before pronouncing sentence the  court  must  ask
 the  defendant  whether  he  or  she  wishes to make such a statement. A
 DEFENDANT MAY CHALLENGE ON APPEAL, NOTWITHSTANDING  AN  OTHERWISE  VALID
 WAIVER  OF  APPEAL,  A  VIOLATION  OF  THE DEFENDANT'S RIGHTS UNDER THIS
