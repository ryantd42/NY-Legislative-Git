# Directs the New York state energy research and development authority to conduct a study on powering cryptocurrency mining facilities with renewable energy

**Bill ID:** S7906
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Kevin S. Parker
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S7906 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S7906)

## Summary

Directs the New York state energy research and development authority to conduct a study on powering cryptocurrency mining facilities with renewable energy.

 AN  ACT  directing  the  New  York state energy research and development
  authority to conduct a study on powering cryptocurrency mining centers
  with renewable energy

  THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND  ASSEM-
 BLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
 
  Section  1.  a. The state energy research and development authority is
 hereby directed to conduct a study  on  powering  cryptocurrency  mining
 centers in New York state.
  b. For purposes of this act:
  (i) "cryptocurrency" shall mean a digital currency in which encryption
 techniques are used to regulate the generation of units and currency and
 verify  the  transfer  of  funds, operating independently from a central
 bank;
  (ii) "mining" shall mean the process of  spending  computer  power  to
 process  transactions  and  add such transactions to a cryptocurrencies'
 blockchain;
  (iii) "blockchain" shall mean a digital ledger in  which  transactions
 made in cryptocurrencies are recorded chronologically and publicly.
  c. The study shall include, but not be limited to:
  (i)  a  survey  of  existing cryptocurrency mining centers in New York
 state, and whether and to what extent such centers are powered by renew-
 able energy;
  (ii) an evaluation of the economic and technical feasibility of power-
 ing all cryptocurrency mining centers in the state with renewable energy
 and the construction of additional facilities powered by renewable ener-
 gy;
  (iii) an assessment of the economic impacts on affected communities of
 powering cryptocurrency mining centers with renewable energy,  including
 impacts on local employment and job creation; and
  (iv) an evaluation of whether cryptocurrency mining powered by renewa-
 ble energy could develop into a sustainable industry in the state.
