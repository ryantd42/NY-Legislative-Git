# Congratulating the Moravia High School Girls Basketball Team upon the occasion of capturing the NYSPHSAA Basketball Class C Championship on March 15, 2024

**Bill ID:** J2382
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Rachel May
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J2382 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J2382)

---

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2382
 
BY: Senator MAY
 
  CONGRATULATING  the  Moravia  High  School  Girls
  Basketball Team upon the occasion of  capturing  the
  NYSPHSAA  Basketball  Class  C Championship on March
  15, 2024
 
  WHEREAS, Individual and team championships are highly  sought  after
in  high  school  sports;  this  Legislative Body commends rare athletic
achievements and pays special  recognition  to  those  who  pursue  such
excellence and become examples for the youth of this great Empire State;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Athletic  competition  helps to enhance both the moral and
physical development of young athletes, preparing them for the future by
instilling in them the importance of teamwork, encouraging a standard of
healthy living, and developing a sense of fair play and competition; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is justly proud to  congratulate  the
Moravia High School Girls Basketball Team upon the occasion of capturing
the  NYSPHSAA Basketball Class C Championship, held on Friday, March 15,
2024, at Hudson Valley Community College in Troy, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, To the praise and  applause  of  their  excited  fans,  the
Moravia High School Girls Basketball Team won their first state title in
a decisive victory over Duanesburg with a final score of 46-45; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  Blue  Devils  made  history  this season, remarkably,
winning their first Section IV championship over Watkins Glen, and  then
driving  on  to their first state title championship in program history;
and
 
  WHEREAS, With the Moravia High School Boys Basketball Team capturing
the championship as well on March 16, 2024, it is the first time ever  a
school  from  Section IV has earned both the boys and girls state titles
in the same year; both teams received a joyous  welcome  home  from  the
community with a "champions parade" through the village; and
 
  WHEREAS, Proudly donning the school's colors of navy blue and white,
family, friends, and the community at large loyally and enthusiastically
supported  the  Blue Devils throughout their journey as they ended their
season with an impressive 24-1 record; and
 
  WHEREAS, In a sport  such  as  basketball,  which  demands  athletic
prowess,  speed  and  agility,  Head Coach John Crossgrove and Assistant
Coach Scott  Badman  have  worked  hard  to  hone  the  skills  of  this
championship  team,  teaching  these  outstanding athletes lessons which
will prove invaluable both on and off the court; and
 
  WHEREAS, Head Coach John Crossgrove, Assistant Coach  Scott  Badman,
and  all  of  the  outstanding athletes on the Moravia High School Girls
Basketball Team have clearly  utilized  dedication,  determination,  and
teamwork in providing a lasting contribution to the spirit of excellence
which is a tradition of their school; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate the members of the Moravia  High  School  Girls  Basketball
Team:  Kylee  Cooper,  Daisy  Drake, Olivia Genson, Gabriella Heim, Lily
Heim, Lashley Heredia Castillo, Kendall Hess,  Jessalyn  Jones,  Allison
Kehoe, Jordan Smith, and Caraline Wasileski; Head Coach John Crossgrove;
and  Assistant  Coach  Scott  Badman  upon the occasion of capturing the
NYSPHSAA Basketball Class C Championship on March 15, 2024;  and  be  it
further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  copies  of  this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the members of the Moravia High School  Girls  Basketball
Team; Head Coach John Crossgrove; and Assistant Coach Scott Badman.
