**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Anthony H. Palumbo
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J1448 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J1448)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1448
 
BY: Senator PALUMBO
 
  HONORING  Paumanok  Vineyards upon the occasion of
  its designation as recipient of the  Governor's  Cup
  Award by the New York Wine and Grape Foundation
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize the
achievements  of  those  individuals  and  businesses  that  contribute
substantially  to  the  economic  vitality of their communities, thereby
enhancing economic well-being of the entire Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Paumanok  Vineyards upon the occasion of its designation as recipient of
the Governor's Cup Award by the New York Wine and  Grape  Foundation  at
its 37th Annual New York Wine Classic on Thursday, July 13, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  37th  Annual  New  York  Wine  Classic,  a nationally
recognized  competition  highlighting  the  best  of  New  York's  wine,
recognized Paumanok Vineyards with its Governor's Cup, the most esteemed
award  given at the Classic, for their 2019 Late Harvest Sauvignon Blanc
which scored 97 points and a platinum rating; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Governor's Cup, a large silver chalice, recognizes  the
"Best of Show" or top prize of all entries in the New York Wine Classic;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Seeing  unprecedented  growth  over the past few years and
allowing wine enthusiasts to enjoy this unique  part  of  the  New  York
experience,  Paumanok Vineyards has truly showcased why this great State
is a premier wine destination; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  addition  to  winning  the  Governor's  Cup,  Paumanok
Vineyards,  based  in Aquebogue, New York, also received recognition for
the Best Dessert Wine in the competition; and
 
  WHEREAS, Judging for the New York State Wine Classic took place from
April 15 through June 15,  2023,  and  included  749  entries  from  108
wineries across the state, with Paumanok Vineyards finishing at the top;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Founded in the spring of 1983, Paumanok Vineyards is a 127
acre estate which is entirely owned and managed by  Ursula  and  Charles
Massoud and their three sons; the premium vinifera grapevines they plant
consist  of Chardonnay, Riesling, Sauvignon Blanc, Chenin Blanc, Merlot,
Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc and Petit Verdot; and
 
  WHEREAS, Under the able leadership of Ursula and Charles Massoud and
their sons, the success of Paumanok Vineyards is a true testament to its
unwavering dedication to over three decades of extraordinary  winemaking
on Long Island; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Over its distinguished 30-year history, Paumanok Vineyards
has demonstrated continuous service to  the  residents  and  communities
which it serves; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  The success of Paumanok Vineyards is in direct correlation
to the efforts of its devoted staff, whose involvement  is,  and  always
has  been,  characterized  by  an  impressive  commitment,  an unbridled
enthusiasm,  and  an  uncompromising  standard  of  excellence  in  all
endeavors on behalf of the organization and the community it serves; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  with  great  pleasure  that  this Legislative Body
acknowledges this exceptional business, its contributions to  the  local
and  state  economies,  and its vibrant role in improving the quality of
life in its community, fully confident that it will  continue  to  enjoy
the  sparkling successes it has experienced thus far; now, therefore, be
it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  Paumanok  Vineyards  upon  the  occasion  of  its  designation as
recipient of the Governor's Cup Award by the New  York  Wine  and  Grape
Foundation; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Paumanok Vineyards, Aquebogue, New York.
