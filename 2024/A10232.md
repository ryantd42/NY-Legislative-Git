# Protects valuable community services affected by the sale, mortgage and lease of real property of religious corporations

**Bill ID:** A10232
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Emily Gallagher
**Status:** In Assembly Committee
**PDF:** [A10232 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/A10232)

## Summary

Protects valuable community services affected by the sale, mortgage and lease of real property of religious corporations.

  9.  IN THE CASE OF A RELIGIOUS CORPORATION, AS SUCH TERM IS DEFINED IN
 SECTION TWO OF THE RELIGIOUS CORPORATIONS  LAW,  A  DESCRIPTION  OF  THE

 SERVICES  OR  THE  NUMBER OF INDIVIDUALS ROUTINELY SERVED, THE TERMS AND

 AFFIRMATION  SIGNED  BY A MEMBER, MANAGER, OR DIRECTOR OF EACH ORGANIZA-

 CORPORATION  IF APPLICABLE, ATTESTING TO THE ACCURACY OF THE INFORMATION
 DESCRIBED THEREIN. FOR THE PURPOSES OF THIS SECTION, THE TERM "COMMUNITY

 MUSICAL PERFORMANCES, AND RECREATIONAL ACTIVITIES.

  (d)  If  it  shall  appear, to the satisfaction of the court, that the
 consideration and the terms of the transaction are fair  and  reasonable
 to  the  corporation  [and], that the purposes of the corporation or the
 interests of the members will be promoted, AND, IN THE CASE A  RELIGIOUS

 shall  direct the disposition of the consideration to be received there-
 under by the corporation.
  (E) IN THE CASE OF A RELIGIOUS CORPORATION, AS SUCH TERM IS DEFINED IN
 SECTION TWO OF THE RELIGIOUS CORPORATIONS LAW,  IF  THE  COURT  SUSPECTS
 THAT  THE  TRANSACTION MAY CAUSE A DISRUPTION TO COMMUNITY SERVICES THAT
 IS IRREVERSIBLE OR IMMEDIATELY  HARMFUL  TO  SIGNIFICANT  AND  SENSITIVE
 COMMUNITY  INTERESTS, THE COURT MAY, IN ITS DISCRETION, SCHEDULE A SEPA-
 RATE HEARING ON THE QUESTION AND INVITE TESTIMONY  OR  SUBMISSIONS  FROM
 AFFECTED MEMBERS OF THE COMMUNITY. IF THE COURT FINDS, WITH OR WITHOUT A
 SEPARATE  HEARING  ON  THE  QUESTION, THAT THE TRANSACTION SHALL CAUSE A
 DISRUPTION TO COMMUNITY SERVICES THAT  IS  IRREVERSIBLE  OR  IMMEDIATELY
 HARMFUL TO SIGNIFICANT AND SENSITIVE COMMUNITY INTERESTS, THE COURT MAY,
 IN  ITS  DISCRETION AND AS AN ALTERNATIVE TO DENYING THE PETITION, ORDER
 THE TRANSACTION TO BE DELAYED UNTIL A REASONABLE DATE CERTAIN  BY  WHICH
 AFFECTED  COMMUNITY  ORGANIZATIONS CAN MAKE AN OFFER FOR THE PURCHASE OF
 THOSE ASSETS NECESSARY TO CONTINUE THE PROVISION OF  COMMUNITY  SERVICES
 IDENTIFIED  BY  THE COURT AS SERVING SIGNIFICANT AND SENSITIVE COMMUNITY
 INTERESTS OR ACCEPT FROM THE CORPORATION A STIPULATION ENTERED INTO WITH
 AFFECTED COMMUNITY ORGANIZATIONS TO MAKE AVAILABLE SUITABLE  ALTERNATIVE
 SPACE  OR  ANY  OTHER  APPROPRIATE  FORM  OF GUARANTEE OR SURETY FOR THE
 CONTINUED PROVISION OF THOSE COMMUNITY SERVICES IDENTIFIED BY THE  COURT
 AS SERVING SIGNIFICANT AND SENSITIVE COMMUNITY INTERESTS.
