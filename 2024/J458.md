## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 458
 
BY: Senator PARKER
 
  HONORING  Bryanna Jenkins upon the occasion of her
  designation as recipient of the PASEsetter Award  by
  the  Partnership  for  After  School  Education  on
  February 28, 2023
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize that
the quality and character of life in the  communities  across  New  York
State  are  reflective  of  the concerned and dedicated efforts of those
individuals who would devote themselves to the welfare of the  community
and its citizenry; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Bryanna Jenkins upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the
PASEsetter Award by the Partnership for After School  Education  at  its
30th  Anniversary  PASEsetter  Awards  Benefit  on Tuesday, February 28,
2023, at Cipriani, New York, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Bryanna Jenkins is receiving this prestigious award for her
role as an afterschool educator whose commitment, energy and  creativity
has  had an indelible impact on the children and youth of New York City;
and
 
  WHEREAS, PASE is a  child-focused  organization  that  promotes  and
supports  quality afterschool programs, particularly those serving young
people from underserved communities; Bryanna Jenkins  truly  exemplifies
this vital organization's mission of delivering consistent, high-quality
programming  to  youth and increases their capacity to meet the needs of
their communities; and
 
  WHEREAS, Bryanna Jenkins grew up in a large family in the Bronx, New
York, where she was inspired by supportive educators to become a teacher
and role model for each and every student she encountered; and
 
  WHEREAS, At the age of 19, Bryanna Jenkins started her  career  with
BronxWorks  as  a  member  of  the  Summer  Youth  Employment  Program;
appreciated for her gentle and compassionate way with the children,  she
was  invited  to  become a permanent youth counselor for the afterschool
program; and
 
  WHEREAS, Bryanna Jenkins was placed with the kindergarten and  first
grade students at BronxWorks, and from day one, she strived to make each
and every child feel loved and special; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout  the  last  six  years  of  her employment with
BronxWorks, Bryanna  Jenkins  has  become  a  confident  leader  in  the
classroom  and  often steps up to help new counselors when they join the
staff; and
 
  WHEREAS, Staff members often look to Bryanna Jenkins for  advice  on
navigating  difficult  situations that arise in their classrooms because
they know she is open and honest about her successes and  struggles  and
has the experience and knowledge to make them a better educator; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout  the  years,  Bryanna  Jenkins has truly made a
difference in the lives of numerous students, helping  them  to  be  the
very best they can be; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Bryanna  Jenkins  is  currently  majoring  in sociology at
Lehman College, and after graduating,  she  hopes  to  become  a  school
guidance  counselor and continue in her mission to support students with
careful attention, kindness, and compassion; and
 
  WHEREAS, It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that  when
individuals  of  such  noble aims and accomplishments are brought to our
attention, they should be celebrated and recognized by all the  citizens
of the great State of New York; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Bryanna Jenkins upon the occasion of her designation as  recipient
of  the  PASEsetter Award by the Partnership for After School Education;
and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Bryanna Jenkins.
