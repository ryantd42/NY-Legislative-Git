# Requires a study of the utilization of state government payments as an opportunity to transition unbanked and underbanked state residents into the banking system

**Bill ID:** S5669
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** James Sanders Jr.
**Status:** Senate Floor Calendar
**PDF:** [S5669 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S5669)

## Summary

Requires a study of the utilization of state government payments as an opportunity to transition unbanked and underbanked state residents into the banking system; requires a report shall be compiled and provided on the findings no later than eighteen months after the effective date of this act.

 AN ACT in relation to the study of the utilization of  state  government
  payments  as  an  opportunity  to  transition unbanked and underbanked
  state residents into the banking system

  THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF NEW YORK, REPRESENTED IN SENATE AND  ASSEM-
 BLY, DO ENACT AS FOLLOWS:
 
  Section 1. The superintendent of the department of financial services,
 in  conjunction  with  the director of the state budget, the director of
 state operations and the commissioner of the department of taxation  and
 finance, and in consultation with the state comptroller, shall study and
 issue  a comprehensive report on methods and opportunities to transition
 unbanked and underbanked state residents entitled  to  state  government
 payments into the banking system.
  1.  For  purposes  of this section, the following terms shall have the
 following meanings:
  a. "unbanked" shall mean an individual not served by an insured insti-
 tution in any capacity;
  b. "underbanked" shall mean  an  individual  with  an  account  at  an
 insured  institution but who has obtained alternative, nonbank financial
 services in the past twelve months; and
  c. "state agency" shall mean any agency, department,  bureau,  commis-
 sion,  board,  task  force,  committee,  public  benefit corporation, or
 public authority, where a member is appointed by the governor.
  2. Such study shall:
  a. examine the types of payments made by state agencies to state resi-
 dents including but not limited to tax returns, benefits, credits, unem-
 ployment insurance, and the lottery, which  are  made  in  a  sufficient
 amount to the unbanked and underbanked;

  b.  examine  what  methods  of  payment are used by state agencies and
 whether direct deposit and debit  cards  are  included  as  options  for
 recipients of such state payments;
  c.  determine whether opportunities exist when state payments are made
 to enable state agencies to provide financial education as it relates to
 the value of participation in the banking system;
  d. examine any barriers to choice of direct deposit and the use of the
 banking system based on contact with state agencies;
  e. examine the use of debit cards of other new financial  technologies
 as  a  method  for  disbursement  of  state payments to the unbanked and

  f. examine whether a contractual relationship with a minority bank  or
 banks that define specific terms of financial service would be an effec-

  g.  determine in which, if any, circumstances state payments should be
 exempted from an execution of judgment to encourage use of  the  banking

  h.  examine barriers and opportunities to charter minority-owned banks
 in unbanked or underbanked communities  including  capital  availability

  i.  include any concerns or recommendations that the superintendent of
 the department of financial services deems necessary.
  3. A comprehensive report summarizing all of the findings from  subdi-
 vision two of this section shall be sent to the governor, the speaker of
 the  assembly  and  the  temporary president of the senate no later than
 eighteen months after the effective date of this act.
  4. The superintendent of the  department  of  financial  services  may
 request  and  is  authorized  to  receive any information from any state
 agencies that is relevant and material to the completion of  this  study
 and  report.  Such  information  received by the superintendent of the
 department of financial services shall be subject to the  same  require-
 ments  for confidentiality and limitations on use, if any, as are appli-
 cable to such state agency's use of such information.
