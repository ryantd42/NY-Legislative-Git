## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1472
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  CONGRATULATING  Arnold R. Fisher upon the occasion
  of celebrating his 100th Birthday
 
  WHEREAS, Senior citizens bring a wealth of experience and  knowledge
to  the  increasingly  active  roles they play in today's society; their
past contributions and future participation are a  vital  part  of,  and
valuable asset to, the fabric of community life and activity; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the custom of this Legislative Body to commemorate
and recognize certain milestones celebrated by citizens  of  this  great
Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this  Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to
congratulate Arnold R. Fisher upon the occasion of his 100th Birthday to
be celebrated on Tuesday, October 31, 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Arnold  Fisher was born on October 31, 1923 and resides in
Hamilton, New York, where he spent most of his life; and
 
  WHEREAS, Devoted to serving his community, Arnold Fisher has been  a
member  of the Morrisville State College Council since 1967, holding the
position of chairman from 1997 to 2008;  he  was  also  chairman  and  a
founding member of the College's Foundation Board; and
 
  WHEREAS, In addition to making generous donations of his own, Arnold
Fisher  has  worked  both  publicly and privately to encourage financial
support of the college with the efforts of his wife Arlene; the  mustang
sculpture  that  stands in the quad and the high-quality cattle given to
the dairy program can be attributed to the Fisher's  dedication  to  the
important entity of the community; and
 
  WHEREAS,  While  operating his own dairy farm for many years, Arnold
Fisher also served in a myriad of respected roles, such as the  Hamilton
Village  Judge,  New  York State Commissioner of Motor Vehicles, Madison
County Clerk, and  Madison  County  Commissioner  of  Jurors;  with  his
knowledge  of  the  industry, he was a member of the Madison County Farm
Bureau and Grange, the 4-H Executive Committee, and the New  York  State
Fair Advisory Board; and
 
  WHEREAS, To honor Arnold Fisher's public service and philanthropy to
Morrisville  State College, the Arnold R. Fisher Dairy Complex was named
after him in 2009; and
 
  WHEREAS, This distinguished man has experienced the incredible  joys
and  sorrows  characteristic  of and reserved for those with the stamina
and courage to savor a full life; and
 
  WHEREAS, Arnold Fisher has enriched the lives of  those  around  him
through  his  joyous and sincere love for others and through the vibrant
charm and infinite wisdom which comes only from a fullness of years; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is the intent  of  this  Legislative  Body  to  publicly
recognize  those  who  have  reached  such a remarkable age and who have
witnessed and celebrated  the  innovations,  cultural  developments  and
awesome  achievements  of  this  country  during the last century, while
themselves  contributing  to  the  growth  and  excellence of this great
Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
congratulate Arnold R. Fisher upon the occasion of celebrating his 100th
Birthday; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Arnold R. Fisher.
