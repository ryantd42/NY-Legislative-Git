# Honoring Lisbeth Fumagalli upon the occasion of her retirement after 22 years of distinguished service to the Town of Bedford, New York

**Session:** 2024

**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [K1011 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/K1011)

## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 1011
 
BY: M. of A. Burdick
 
  HONORING  Lisbeth  Fumagalli  upon the occasion of
  her  retirement  after  22  years  of  distinguished
  service to the Town of Bedford, New York
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative  Body  seeks  to  acknowledge  those
individuals whose  professional  lives  and  civic  endeavors  serve  to
enhance the stature of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Lisbeth Fumagalli upon the occasion of her retirement after 22 years  of
distinguished  service  to  the  Town of Bedford, New York, on March 28,
2024; and
 
  WHEREAS, For the past 22 years,  Lisbeth  "Boo"  Fumagalli  rendered
faithful,  conscientious,  and  valuable service to the Town of Bedford,
New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Winning her first election in 2001 and being inaugurated on
January 1, 2002, Lisbeth Fumagalli  went  on  to  win  every  subsequent
re-election; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Lisbeth  Fumagalli  worked  on  a  wide  range of projects
including:  the town website, insurance  claims,  the  film  ordinance,
cemeteries, and the installation of the new phone system; and
 
  WHEREAS,  One  of her favorite duties that came with serving in this
official capacity as the Town of Bedford Clerk was performing marriages;
she was instrumental in having a New York State law passed to waive  the
marriage license for active-duty military personnel; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Lisbeth  Fumagalli diligently worked as the steward of the
town's  records  and  implemented  a  new  records  center  and  record
management  program to make this aspect of her job more efficient, easy,
and accurate; during her tenure, she worked to preserve the  older  town
documents  and  vital  records  such  as meeting minutes for the Clerk's
Office and Tax Office; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  testament  to  her  devotion  to  her  career,  Lisbeth
Fumagalli  was  President of the Westchester Clerks Association for many
years; she was also an officer of the New York State Clerks  Association
and  was recognized as both Town and District Clerk of the Year in 2014;
and
 
  WHEREAS, Lisbeth Fumagalli  was  elected  to  the  Katonah-Lewisboro
School  Board  and served as its President; she has a deep commitment to
community service and was also the President of the  Katonah  Elementary
School  PTA,  a  member of the Katonah Village Improvement Society Board
for many years, and has served as an office  and  board  member  of  the
Bedford  Hills  Neighborhood Association; she is also a volunteer at the
Community Center of Northern  Westchester,  the  Katonah  Memorial  Park
Association,  and  is a member and one-time officer of the Bedford Hills
Lions Club; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  A  role  model  for  women  across  this State and Nation,
Lisbeth Fumagalli was selected as a 2023 Women Innovator and is a member
of both the Bedford Hill Women's Club  and  the  Katonah  Women's  Civic
Club; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  her  retirement, Lisbeth Fumagalli will enjoy spending
time at the beach, either on Martha's Vineyard or on Long  Beach  Island
where  she  grew  to  love vacations in her grandmother's house in Beach
Haven, and can now be found in Harvey Cedars creating memories  for  her
granddaughter, Reagan; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  her  capacity  as Town Clerk, Lisbeth Fumagalli served
with loyalty, honor and distinction; and
 
  WHEREAS, In her official acts, Lisbeth Fumagalli was governed  by  a
keen  sense  of duty and always showed a unique grasp of human problems;
and
 
  WHEREAS, During  her  tenure  with  the  Town  of  Bedford,  Lisbeth
Fumagalli  earned  the  admiration,  esteem,  and  affection  of  her
colleagues; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor  Lisbeth  Fumagalli  upon  the occasion of her retirement after 22
years of distinguished service; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Lisbeth Fumagalli.
