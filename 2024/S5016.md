# Creates the consumer overdraft protection act

**Bill ID:** S5016
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** James Sanders Jr.
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [S5016 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/S5016)

## Summary

Creates the consumer overdraft protection act to ensure that consumers are informed of their options in regard to overdraft protection.

  § 62. RESTRICTIONS ON OVERDRAFT PROTECTION PROGRAMS  OR  SERVICES.  1.
 GENERAL PROVISIONS. IN THE CASE OF ANY TRANSACTION ACCOUNT OF A CONSUMER
 AT  ANY  INSTITUTION, NO OVERDRAFT PROTECTION FEE MAY BE IMPOSED ON SUCH
 ACCOUNT FOR ANY EXTENSION OF FUNDS BY THE INSTITUTION TO COVER ANY CHECK

 OR OTHER DEBIT FOR WHICH THERE ARE INSUFFICIENT FUNDS IN THE  CONSUMER'S
 ACCOUNT TO PAY SUCH CHECK OR OTHER DEBIT, UNLESS:
  (A)  THE CONSUMER HAS PROVIDED SPECIFIC WRITTEN CONSENT TO ANY PROGRAM
 OR SERVICE THAT PROVIDES FOR CHARGING SUCH FEES IN CONNECTION  WITH  ANY
 SUCH EXTENSION OF FUNDS;
  (B)  SUCH  FEE IS IMPOSED PURSUANT TO THE TERMS OF A WRITTEN AGREEMENT
 WITH THE CONSUMER WHICH DISCLOSES, IN A CLEAR AND CONSPICUOUS MANNER:
  (I) THE AMOUNT OF ANY FEE IMPOSED IN CONNECTION WITH PAYING  AN  OVER-
 DRAFT;
  (II)  ANY APPLICABLE DISCLOSURE REQUIRED BY THIS ARTICLE IN CONNECTION
 WITH SUCH EXTENSION OF CREDIT;
  (III) THE CATEGORIES OF TRANSACTION FOR WHICH A FEE FOR PAYMENT OF  AN
 OVERDRAFT  MAY  BE  IMPOSED,  INCLUDING  WHETHER AN OVERDRAFT CREATED BY
 WITHDRAWALS AT AUTOMATED TELLER MACHINES OR OTHER ELECTRONIC FUND TRANS-
 FERS WILL BE COVERED AND A FEE IMPOSED;
  (IV) THE TIME PERIOD BY WHICH THE CONSUMER MUST  REPAY  OR  COVER  ANY
 EXTENSION OF CREDIT IN THE FORM OF PAYMENT OF AN OVERDRAFT;
  (V)  THE CIRCUMSTANCES UNDER WHICH THE INSTITUTION IN WHICH AN ACCOUNT
 IS HELD WILL NOT PAY AN OVERDRAFT; AND
  (VI) OTHER INFORMATION REQUIRED TO BE DISCLOSED BY LAW;
  (C) SUCH FEE IS SEPARATELY AND CONSPICUOUSLY DISCLOSED, EACH TIME  THE
 FEE  IS IMPOSED, IN ANY PERIODIC STATEMENT PROVIDED TO THE CONSUMER WITH
 RESPECT TO SUCH ACCOUNT AND IS INCLUDED IN THE CALCULATION OF THE ANNUAL
 PERCENTAGE RATE AS REQUIRED BY STATE AND FEDERAL LAW;
  (D) IN NO CASE SHALL OVERDRAFT FEES OVER THE COURSE OF ONE YEAR EXCEED
 ONE HUNDRED  DOLLARS,  INCLUDING  ANY  ADDITIONAL  CHARGES  AND/OR  FEES
 IMPOSED PURSUANT TO THIS SECTION;
  (E)  EVERY  INSTITUTION SHALL ESTABLISH AN AUTOMATIC BOUNCE SYSTEM FOR
 ANY AMOUNT EXCEEDING THE LIMIT  PRESCRIBED  BY  PARAGRAPH  (D)  OF  THIS
 SUBDIVISION.
  2.  MONTHLY  FEE.  ANY INSTITUTION MAY REQUIRE A FEE OF ONE DOLLAR FOR
 ANY CHECKING ACCOUNT FOR THE MAINTENANCE OF AN AUTOMATIC BOUNCE  SYSTEM.
 SUCH FEE MUST BE DISCLOSED PURSUANT TO SUBDIVISION ONE OF THIS SECTION.
  3.  CLARIFICATION  RELATING  TO  OVERDRAFT FEES. IN CASE OF ANY TRANS-
 ACTION ACCOUNT OF A CONSUMER AT ANY INSTITUTION, THE PROHIBITION AGAINST
 AN OVERDRAFT PROTECTION FEE UNDER SUBDIVISION ONE OF THIS SECTION  SHALL
 APPLY  REGARDLESS  OF  WHETHER THE AMOUNT OF SUCH FEE IS THE SAME AS, OR
 LESS THAN, ANY FEE IMPOSED BY  THE  INSTITUTION  WITH  RESPECT  TO  SUCH
 ACCOUNT FOR A CHECK OR OTHER DEBIT THAT IS RETURNED UNPAID.
  4.  PROHIBITION  ON MISREPRESENTATION. IT SHALL BE A VIOLATION OF THIS
 ARTICLE IF ANY INSTITUTION:
  (A) WILL NOT EXTEND FUNDS UNDER  SPECIFIC  CIRCUMSTANCE  TO  COVER  AN
 OVERDRAFT  IN  ANY TRANSACTION ACCOUNT OF A CONSUMER AT THE INSTITUTION;
 OR
  (B) RESERVES THE RIGHT TO EXTEND FUNDS TO PAY ANY SUCH OVERDRAFT ON  A
 DISCRETIONARY  BASIS,  ANY  REPRESENTATION  BY SUCH INSTITUTION THAT THE
 INSTITUTION WILL EXTEND CREDIT TO COVER ALL OVERDRAFTS ON SUCH ACCOUNT.
  § 63. RESTRICTIONS ON ADVERTISING OF OVERDRAFT PROTECTION PROGRAMS  OR
 SERVICES.  1.  GENERAL  PROVISIONS.  IN  THE CASE OF AN INSTITUTION THAT
 MAINTAINS TRANSACTION ACCOUNTS FOR CONSUMERS AND  OFFERS  A  PROGRAM  OR
 SERVICE UNDER WHICH THE INSTITUTION PAYS ANY OVERDRAFT ON THE ACCOUNT IN
 EXCHANGE FOR PAYMENT OF AN OVERDRAFT PROTECTION FEE, THE INSTITUTION MAY
 NOT MAKE ANY OF THE FOLLOWING REPRESENTATIONS OR STATEMENTS WITH RESPECT
 TO SUCH PROGRAM OR SERVICE IN ANY ADVERTISEMENT OR PROMOTION:
  (A)  ANY  REPRESENTATION OR STATEMENT DESCRIBING A TRANSACTION ACCOUNT
 AS FREE OR NO COST IF THE ACCOUNT INCLUDES, OR IS PROMOTED AS INCLUDING,
 S. 5016  4
 
 OVERDRAFT PROTECTION SERVICES THAT  INVOLVE  THE  PAYMENT  OF  OVERDRAFT
 PROTECTION FEES.
  (B) ANY REPRESENTATION OR STATEMENT ENCOURAGING THE USE OF THE ACCOUNT
 AS  A SERVICE TO MEET SHORT-TERM CREDIT NEEDS OR TO OBTAIN ADVANTAGES ON
 A CONSUMER'S NEXT PAYMENT OF SALARY, WAGES, BENEFITS, OR OTHER INCOME.
  (C) ANY REPRESENTATION OR STATEMENT  THAT  THE  FINANCIAL  INSTITUTION
 WILL  HONOR ALL CHECKS OR OTHER DEBITS PRESENTED AGAINST THE ACCOUNT, IF
 THE INSTITUTION RETAINS DISCRETION AT ANY TIME NOT TO HONOR ANY CHECK OR
 OTHER DEBIT PRESENTED.
  2. REGULATIONS. THE BOARD OF GOVERNORS OF THE FEDERAL  RESERVE  SYSTEM
 SHALL  PRESCRIBE  REGULATIONS  IMPLEMENTING THE RESTRICTION SET FORTH IN
 SUBDIVISION ONE OF THIS SECTION PURSUANT TO THE AUTHORITY OF  THE  BOARD
 UNDER  SECTION  18(F)  OF  THE FEDERAL TRADE COMMISSION ACT, AND MAY, BY
 REGULATION OR ORDER, RESTRICT SUCH ADDITIONAL ACTS OR PRACTICES THAT THE
 BOARD FINDS TO BE UNFAIR OR DECEPTIVE IN CONNECTION WITH  THE  OFFERING,
 OPERATION,  AND  ADVERTISING  OF  OVERDRAFT  PROTECTION  PROGRAMS  AND
 SERVICES.
  § 2. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after
 it shall have become a law.
