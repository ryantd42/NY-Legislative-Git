## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2042
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  COMMEMORATING  the  95th  Anniversary  of  the
  Florentine Pastry Shop of Utica, New York
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize that
the quality and character of life in the  communities  across  New  York
State  are  reflective  of  the concerned and dedicated efforts of those
individuals and businesses that  are  devoted  to  the  welfare  of  the
community and its citizenry; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is also the intent of this Legislative Body to recognize
and  commend  those who work to better their local community and to keep
local traditions alive; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
commemorate the 95th Anniversary of the Florentine Pastry Shop of Utica,
New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Florentine Pastry Shop has been  making  Italian  cakes
and specialties in Utica, New York for nearly a century; and
 
  WHEREAS, Opened in December of 1928 by Joseph Borgovini and Vincenzo
Gennaro,  the  Florentine  Pastry  Shop  was  originally  located at 605
Bleecker Street before moving to its current location  at  667  Bleecker
Street by Gennaro's wife Luigia in 1945; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Run by various local Italian families since its inception,
Rocco Vitullo and Gabe Alessandroni became the new owners in 1963,  with
Gabriele  Alessandroni  becoming  the  sole  owner  1973; his relatives,
Andrew and Debby Alesia, became the current owners in  1993;  their  son
Jared  is  a  graduate of the Culinary Institute of America and a pastry
chef, while their son Martin is the shop's cake decorator; and
 
  WHEREAS, For 95 years, the Florentine Pastry Shop  has  offered  its
loyal  Utica  area  customers the finest quality foods and products, and
the highest standards of service while remaining a gathering  place  for
young  and  old alike and retaining the same old-time charm which has so
characterized its past; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is  with  great  pleasure  that  this  Legislative  Body
acknowledges  this  exceptional  business  and  its contributions to the
local and State economies, fully confident  that  it  will  continue  to
enjoy  the  sparkling  successes  it  has  experienced  thus  far;  now,
therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
commemorate the 95th Anniversary of the Florentine Pastry Shop of Utica,
New York; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the Florentine Pastry Shop, Utica, New York.
