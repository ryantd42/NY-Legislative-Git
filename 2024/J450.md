# Honoring Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder upon the occasion of his designation as recipient of a Lincoln-King Visionary Award for his commitment and service to his community

**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Jack M. Martins
**Status:** In Senate Committee
**PDF:** [J450 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J450)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 450
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
  HONORING  Commissioner  Patrick  J. Ryder upon the
  occasion  of  his  designation  as  recipient  of  a
  Lincoln-King  Visionary Award for his commitment and
  service to his community
 
  WHEREAS, Individuals who give of their time and energies  and  serve
the best interests of their communities are an asset beyond remuneration
and cannot be sufficiently extolled; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Commissioner  Patrick  J.  Ryder has given not only of his
time  and  energies  but  also  of  his  competence,  intelligence  and
leadership and consequently has been designated for special honor; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Commissioner
Patrick  J. Ryder upon the occasion of his designation as recipient of a
Lincoln-King Visionary Award for  his  commitment  and  service  to  his
community; he is to be honored at the Lincoln-King Visionary Awards Gala
on Saturday, March 4, 2023, in Mineola, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Commissioner  Patrick  J.  Ryder  has  been called upon to
contribute his time  and  talents  to  countless  civic  and  charitable
endeavors and has always given of himself unstintingly; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Now  Commissioner  of  Nassau  County  Police  Department,
Patrick J.  Ryder, started his career in law enforcement  39  years  ago
with  the  New  York City Police Department in January of 1984; he later
joined the Nassau County Police Department on July  18,  1986,  and  was
assigned  to the 3rd Precinct; in 1989, he was assigned to the Bureau of
Special Operations; and
 
  WHEREAS, Promoted to Sergeant in 1995, he was designated to the  5th
Precinct,  8th  Squad,  and  the  Asset  Forfeiture  Unit  as the Deputy
Commanding Officer; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2008, he was designated the Commanding Officer of  Asset
Forfeiture  &  Intelligence  (AFI),  where  he  proactively  implemented
several life-saving programs and initiatives that helped ensure  officer
and  public  safety; these initiatives brought crime in Nassau County to
historic lows, allowing residents to enjoy one of the lowest crime rates
in the United States; and
 
  WHEREAS, Under the able leadership of Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder,
Nassau County was named the "Safest Community in America" by  U.S.  News
and World Report for the third year in a row; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Commissioner  Patrick  J.  Ryder  has a Master's degree in
Homeland Security and Management from Long Island University; and
 
  WHEREAS, He previously served as an Adjunct  Professor  of  Criminal
Justice  and  Intelligence-Led  Policing  at  Nassau  County  Community
College, as well as taught at Dowling College until 2015; and
 
  WHEREAS, Adept in his field, Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder currently
sits on the Advisory Board for Criminal Justice at SUNY Farmingdale  and
has  been  a  Counter-Terrorism  Lecturer  at the University of Maryland
Center for Health and Homeland Security; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder is a New York State Division
of Criminal Justice Services-Certified Law Enforcement Instructor, a New
York  State  Certified  Crime  and  Intelligence  Analyst,  a  Certified
Anti-Money  Laundering Specialist, and has Top Secret clearance with the
FBI's New York Field Office; and
 
  WHEREAS, He has also been deputized as an IRS Criminal Investigator,
and with his vast depth of law enforcement knowledge and implementation,
has trained members of law enforcement agencies from Egypt,  Tajikistan,
Tunisia, South Africa, and Turkey; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout  the  entire period of his community service, a
period of constructive involvement, Commissioner Patrick  J.  Ryder  has
stood constant in dignity, good grace, and humor; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  him  throughout  have been his wife, Jill, and their
three beloved children,  Liam,  Sean,  and  Meghan,  all  of  whom  feel
privileged to be a part of his life and rejoice in his achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rare  indeed  is  the  impressive  dedication  shown by an
individual for the benefit of others which Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder
has displayed throughout his life; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
honor Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder upon the occasion of his designation
as  recipient  of  a Lincoln-King Visionary Award for his commitment and
service to his community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Commissioner Patrick J. Ryder.
