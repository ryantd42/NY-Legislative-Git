## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1743
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
  HONORING  Chief Master Sergeant John A. Jones upon
  his retirement after faithful  military  service  as
  Commander of the Civil Air Patrol's NY Wing
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  custom  of this Legislative Body to recognize
those individuals  who  have  honorably  and  courageously  served  this
country,  in  order to protect the life and liberty of our citizens, and
to support civilian authorities during times of emergency; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Chief  Master  Sergeant John A. Jones upon his retirement after faithful
military service as Commander of the Civil Air Patrol's NY Wing,  to  be
celebrated  on  Saturday,  January  27,  2024,  at the Change of Command
Ceremony to be held at 1100 Fuel Cell  Hanger,  Hancock  Field  ANGB  in
Syracuse, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Having entered the Air Force in August of 1993, John Jones
served in a myriad of positions with  growing  responsibility  over  the
next  31  years,  culminating  in  his  current  position in the NYS Air
National Guard as Superintendent of  the  174th  Maintenance  Operations
Flight, 174th Maintenance Group, Hancock Field ANGB, Syracuse, New York;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Initially,  John  Jones  served  as  an  Airlift  Aircraft
Maintenance Specialist assigned to the 436th  Airlift  Wing,  Dover  Air
Force  Base;  in  1997, he transitioned to the 174th Fighter Wing of the
New York Air National Guard and for nine years held the responsibilities
of Attack Controls Systems Specialist and Electronic Integrated  Systems
Specialist,  maintaining  critical  electronic  systems  of  the F-16C/D
Fighting Falcon; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2006, John Jones moved to the Maintenance Group  Quality
Assurance  office  as  the Avionics Inspector, eventually serving as the
Chief  Inspector  and  Superintendent;  he  bravely  served  on  many
deployments  to Europe and the Middle East to support vital missions for
the Nation, such as Operations DELIBERATE FORCE, SOUTHERN  WATCH,  NOBLE
EAGLE, and ENDURING FREEDOM; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  man with extensive education and experience, John Jones
was promoted to Chief Master Sergeant on June 9, 2017; he commanded over
2,100 personnel and was responsible for organizing and executing various
missions for the U.S. Air Force, Department of  Defense,  and  New  York
State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout his distinguished military career, Chief Master
Sergeant John  A.  Jones  has  received  numerous  military  awards  and
decorations for his dedicated service, including the Meritorious Service
Medal, Air Force Commendation Medal with one oak leaf cluster, Air Force
Achievement  Medal,  Air  Reserve  Forces Meritorious Service Medal with
silver oak leaf cluster, National Defense Service Medal with one device,
Global War on Terrorism Service Medal,  Military  Outstanding  Volunteer
Service Medal, NATO Medal, and many other achievements; and
 
 
  WHEREAS,  Devoted  to his community and the armed forces, John Jones
is a member of the Civil Air Patrol, U.S. Air Force Auxiliary, Air Force
Association, Aircraft Owners and  Pilots  Association,  Service  Academy
Selection Committee, and Veteran's Affairs Committee; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  addition,  John  Jones  received  several  educational
merits, having graduated from many Air  Force  training  academies  with
honors; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Individuals  who  dedicate their lives to military service
merit the commendation and applause of all citizens for their commitment
to the preservation of American freedom; and
 
  WHEREAS, It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that  when
individuals  of  such  noble aims and accomplishments are brought to our
attention, they should be celebrated and recognized by all the  citizens
of the great State of New York; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Chief Master Sergeant John A.  Jones  upon  his  retirement  after
faithful  military  service  as  Commander  of the Civil Air Patrol's NY
Wing; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Chief Master Sergeant John A. Jones.
