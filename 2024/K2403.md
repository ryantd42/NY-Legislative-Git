# Congratulating Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake, D. Min., upon the occasion of her retirement, as Senior Pastor, after many years of distinguished ministry to the Greater Allen A.M.E. Cathedral

**Bill ID:** K2403
**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Alicia Hyndman
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [K2403 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/K2403)

---

## Full Text

Assembly Resolution No. 2403
 
BY: M. of A. Hyndman
 
  CONGRATULATING Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins
  Flake, D. Min., upon the occasion of her retirement,
  as  Senior Pastor, after many years of distinguished
  ministry to the Greater Allen A.M.E.  Cathedral
 
  WHEREAS, The character and richness of religious and community  life
in  this  noble  Empire  State  is  nourished  and fulfilled through the
faithful concern and selfless devotion of those who would  commit  their
energies and purpose to the ministry of others; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of this Legislative Body to bring full
recognition and  just  tribute  to  those  individuals  who  distinguish
themselves  and  the  ministry  through their unremitting commitment and
preeminence of service; and
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative  Body  is  justly  proud  to  congratulate
Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake, D. Min., upon the occasion of
her  retirement,  as  Senior  Pastor,  after many years of distinguished
ministry to the Greater Allen A.M.E. Cathedral  in  New  York  City,  on
Sunday, June 2, 2024; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake, D. Min., earned
her  Bachelor  of  Arts  in  English  degree  from  Fisk  University  in
Nashville,  Tennessee;  from Boston University, she earned her Master of
Arts in English degree, and she obtained her Master of  Divinity  degree
from  Union  Theological  Seminary  in  New  York  City;  from  United
Theological Seminary in Dayton, Ohio, she  was  awarded  the  Doctor  of
Ministry degree; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Fondly  called  "Reverend  Elaine"  by  her  congregation,
Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake founded  the  Allen  Christian
School  with her husband, Reverend Doctor Floyd H. Flake, in 1982; since
1983, she has served as Advisor to the Missionary Department, as well as
many other ministries, at Allen Women's Resource  Center,  which  houses
women  and  children  who  are  victims  of  domestic  violence,  the
organization she helped to develop; and
 
  WHEREAS, Recognized as a powerful preacher, teacher, and role  model
with  a  gift  for  reaching  worshipers of all ages, Reverend Margarett
Elaine McCollins Flake makes a powerful impact on  the  countless  lives
she touches; until May of 2020, she co-pastored the Greater Allen A.M.E.
Cathedral, receiving the appointment of Senior Pastor upon her husband's
retirement;  she  has  dedicated her life to the betterment of others by
leading in Christian education, evangelism, and outreach ministries; and
 
  WHEREAS, Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake proudly serves as
Advisor and  Coordinator  of  the  Allen  Women's  Ministry  Department,
hosting an annual retreat which is attended by more than 1,000 women who
journey  across  the  country  and  from  the  Caribbean; she has been a
significant influence in the growing membership  of  the  Greater  Allen
A.M.E.  Cathedral, presently numbering more than 15,000 worshipers; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout  the years, Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins
Flake rendered faithful, conscientious, and  valuable  ministry  to  the
residents  of New York City and its neighboring communities; she mentors
numerous leaders in the  New  York  City  area  and  frequently  accepts
invitations  to  speak at events throughout the country; she also serves
on several community boards; and
 
  WHEREAS, Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake, as well  as  her
husband,  has  authored  and/or  contributed  to  several books; she has
received numerous citations and awards for  her  years  of  service  and
ministry;  in March of 2017, she received the Distinguished Alumni Award
from  Union  Theological  Seminary  and  is  an  inductee  of  Morehouse
College's Martin Luther King, Jr., Board of Preachers; and
 
  WHEREAS, Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake is a proud member
of  Alpha  Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., and the National Council of Negro
Women, Inc., as well as the current  National  Chaplain  of  The  Links,
Incorporated; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  her  throughout  have  been her husband of 47 years,
Reverend Doctor Floyd H. Flake; their children,  Aliya,  Nailah,  Robert
Rasheed,  and  Harold  Hasan; and their granddaughter, Nia Renee, all of
whom feel privileged to be a  part  of  her  life  and  rejoice  in  her
achievements; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Reverend  Margarett  Elaine  McCollins Flake, D. Min., has
brought an overwhelming spirit of charity and compassion to her ministry
and community, acting from vision and enlightenment  in  conducting  her
administrative and spiritual duties, while always displaying the highest
standards of personal and professional integrity and dedication; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  distinguished  woman  of  God  has  always  given
enlightenment, guidance, and wisdom to the many entrusted  to  her  care
and  has  administered  abundantly and unstintingly to the spiritual and
corporal needs of all; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that,  when
individuals  of  such  noble aims and accomplishments are brought to our
attention, it is appropriate to publicly  and  jubilantly  proclaim  and
commend those individuals for the edification of others; now, therefore,
be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake,  D.  Min.,  upon
the  occasion  of  her retirement, as Senior Pastor, after many years of
distinguished ministry to the Greater Allen A.M.E. Cathedral in New York
City, on June 2, 2024, and extend to her the heartfelt  best  wishes  of
this Legislative Body for a future of purposeful success and well-being;
and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Reverend Margarett Elaine McCollins Flake, D. Min.
