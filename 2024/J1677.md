# Congratulating Margaret Anne Chase upon the occasion of her retirement after 10 years of distinguished public service to the Onondaga County Legislature

**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Rachel May
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J1677 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J1677)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 1677
 
BY: Senator MAY
 
  CONGRATULATING  Margaret  Anne  Chase  upon  the
  occasion  of  her  retirement  after  10  years  of
  distinguished  public service to the Onondaga County
  Legislature
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize  and
commend  those  individuals of outstanding purpose whose lives have been
committed to public service and the pursuit of excellence in the conduct
of the legislative process in this noble Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
congratulate Margaret Anne Chase upon the  occasion  of  her  retirement
after  10  years  of distinguished public service to the Onondaga County
Legislature; and
 
  WHEREAS, Margaret Anne Chase, affectionately  known  as  Peggy,  has
been  called  upon to contribute her time and talents to countless civic
and charitable endeavors and has always given of  herself  unstintingly;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Peggy Chase faithfully served as the 9th District Onondaga
County Legislator from 2013 to 2023 with great diligence and honor; and
 
  WHEREAS, Born and raised in Eastwood, New York,  Peggy  Chase  is  a
Registered Nurse with a Doctoral degree in Education Administration from
Syracuse University; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  extraordinary woman has extensive experience as both
an educator and nurse leader in local hospitals; she has taught  Nursing
at  Morrisville,  SUNY  Upstate  Medical,  and  Syracuse University, and
currently serves as an adjunct professor at Keuka College; and
 
  WHEREAS, Appointed to the State Education  Department  Nurse  Board,
Peggy Chase is involved in policy development and practice monitoring of
licensed  professionals  within  New  York  State; she is also an active
member of the New York State Nurses' Association focusing on legislative
and education issues; and
 
  WHEREAS, A true  asset  to  her  local  community,  Peggy  Chase  is
involved in several volunteer activities including as an RN at the North
Side Ministries Porvello Clinic and Blessed Sacrament Parish; and
 
  WHEREAS,  With  a  steadfast and unwavering commitment to supporting
and promoting women's issues, Peggy Chase is an  active  member  of  the
Eastwood  Neighborhood  Association  (ENA),  TNT, Women's Initiative Now
(WIN), CNY National Organization of Women (NOW),  and  Syracuse  Women's
Commission; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Throughout  the  entire period of her community service, a
period of constructive involvement, Peggy Chase has  stood  constant  in
dignity, good grace, and humor; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Rare  indeed  is  the  impressive  dedication  shown by an
individual for the benefit of others which  Peggy  Chase  has  displayed
throughout her life; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that when
individuals of such noble aims and accomplishments are  brought  to  our
attention,  they should be celebrated and recognized by all the citizens
of the great State of New York; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
congratulate  Margaret  Anne  Chase  upon the occasion of her retirement
after 10 years of distinguished public service to  the  Onondaga  County
Legislature; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Margaret Anne Chase.
