**Session:** 2024
**Sponsor:** Jake Ashby
**Status:** Adopted
**PDF:** [J2016 PDF](https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2024/J2016)

## Full Text

Senate Resolution No. 2016
 
BY: Senator ASHBY
 
  HONORING  Diane X. Burman upon the occasion of the
  completion of her term as Commissioner  of  the  New
  York State Public Service Commission
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to acknowledge the
significant  milestones  in  the  distinguished  careers  of  esteemed
residents of this noble Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Diane  X.  Burman  upon  the  occasion  of the completion of her term as
Commissioner of the New York State Public Service Commission; and
 
  WHEREAS, For the past 11 years, and in her previous capacity holding
the position of Public Service Commissioner, Diane  X.  Burman  rendered
faithful,  conscientious,  and  valuable  service to the citizens of the
State of New York, earning the admiration, esteem, and affection of  her
colleagues; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Starting  in  1991,  Diane  X.  Burman spent several years
working in the New York State Court system, first  as  a  Clerk  at  the
Court  of  Appeals  and  subsequently  as a Senior Court Attorney at the
Supreme Court; and
 
  WHEREAS, Showing a true dedication to the practice  of  law  in  the
public  interest, Diane X. Burman held various positions as an Assistant
Attorney General at  the  Office  of  the  State  Attorney  General,  an
Assistant  Counsel  at  the  New  York  State  Department  of  Economic
Development, and a Special Counsel to the Commission  at  the  New  York
State Public Service Commission; and
 
  WHEREAS,  As a woman with a zeal to improve the lives of others in a
professional capacity, Diane X. Burman worked as  the  Director  of  the
Department  of  Pro  Bono Affairs at the New York State Bar Association,
the Assistant Executive Director at  the  Sidney  Albert  Albany  Jewish
Community  Center  and  as  the Executive Director of the New York State
Parent Teacher Association; and
 
  WHEREAS, As a director,  advisor  and  volunteer  in  many  industry
panels  and  eleemosynary  organizations,  including  as a Member of the
Albany NY Women in Energy Group, the Women in Energy and  Communications
in  New  York  and  the  Congregation  Beth Emeth in Albany NY, Diane X.
Burman tirelessly contributed to the welfare of the community at  large;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Earning  a  reputation  as  a  hands-on  attorney  who was
committed to the welfare of the People of  New  York,  Diane  X.  Burman
answered  the  call  to  become  the  first  female Chief Counsel to the
Majority Conference of the New York State Senate; and
 
  WHEREAS, Having earned the respect, admiration and trust of Senators
from all parties and all parts of the State, she was nominated  in  2013
 
to  the  New  York  State  Public  Service  Commission  and  confirmed
unanimously by the New York State Senate; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Having  distinguished herself as a thoughtful, open-minded
and caring adjudicator, Diane X. Burman was again nominated to  the  New
York  State  Public Service Commission in 2018 and unanimously confirmed
by the New York State Senate; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  her  capacity  as  New  York  State  Public  Service
Commissioner,  Diane  X.  Burman  served  with  dedication,  honor  and
distinction, governed by a keen sense of duty while displaying a  unique
grasp  of  the  industries  that  she was charged to regulate and in her
official acts, kept the safety, reliability  and  affordability  of  the
state's  energy,  telecommunications  and  private  water systems at the
forefront of her efforts on behalf of  utility  customers  in  New  York
State,  and  served  in  numerous  roles  at the National Association of
Regulatory Utility Commissioners, including Chair of the Gas  Committee,
Member  of  the  Board  of  Trustees, and the Chair of the United States
Department of  Energy  and  NARUC  Natural  Gas  Partnership,  and  lead
consensus-building  at  the  Gas  Technology  Institute  Public Interest
Advisory Panel and as the Vice Chair of the United States Department  of
Transportation  Pipeline  and  Hazardous Materials Safety Administration
Gas Policy Advisory Committee, among her  numerous  professional  roles;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that when
individuals of such noble aims and accomplishments are  brought  to  our
attention,  they should be celebrated and recognized by all the citizens
of the great State of New York; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
express  our  gratitude on behalf of the state's citizens and to commend
and honor Diane X. Burman upon the occasion of  the  completion  of  her
term  as  Commissioner  of the New York State Public Service Commission;
and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Diane X. Burman.
